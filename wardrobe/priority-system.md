# Chaos Incorporated Priority System

Every item in the wardrobe catalog carries exactly one priority tag. This keeps the system honest about what's foundational vs. what's a bet.

## Tiers

### Must-Have
The category or item is foundational to the wardrobe. It gets worn in heavy weekly rotation, in multiple colorways/washes if it's a bottom, and the wardrobe is considered incomplete without it.
- Build in duplicate/multiple colorways once validated.
- Fit must be dialed in exactly — no "good enough" on a Must-Have.
- Example: a straight-leg mid-rise jean in a reliable, comfortable stretch denim.

### High
A strong, validated addition that fills a real gap in the existing rotation or meaningfully elevates a category. Not yet proven at the weekly-wear level, but there's a clear, specific reason to believe it will be.
- Build in one colorway first, expand if it performs.
- Example: a wide-leg jean to diversify silhouette against an all-skinny/straight rotation.

### Medium
A good idea that isn't urgent. Worth building once the Must-Have and High layers are solid, to round out the system or cover an occasional-use case.
- Lower urgency — fine to sit in the backlog.
- Example: tailored trousers for occasional dressier occasions.

### Experimental
A bet on a new silhouette, fabric, era, or aesthetic direction the system hasn't validated yet. Small quantity. The point is to learn whether it works, not to commit to it.
- Build exactly one, in the safest colorway, before deciding whether to expand the bet.
- If it flops, that's a successful experiment — cut it and document why in the item's notes.
- Example: a barrel-leg jean, an unfamiliar silhouette for a 64" frame that needs real-world testing before scaling.

## How to Assign a Tier

Ask, in order:
1. **Does she already reach for this category weekly?** → Must-Have.
2. **Is there a specific, named gap this fills?** → High.
3. **Is this a "nice to have, no rush" addition?** → Medium.
4. **Is this untested — new silhouette/fabric/vibe we haven't validated on her frame?** → Experimental.

## Rules

- No item skips straight to Must-Have without first existing as High or Experimental and proving itself. The one exception is a direct, like-for-like replacement of an already-proven Must-Have (e.g., replacing a worn-out pair of the exact same jean).
- Experimental items get revisited after a defined trial period (season, or ~10 wears) and are either promoted or retired — they do not linger indefinitely.
- Priority is about the *category fit*, not sunk cost. A beautiful piece that doesn't get worn is still Medium/Experimental regardless of how much effort went into sourcing it.
