## Why This Combination Works
- **One loud color element, not two:** the dress carries the Starlink Emerald color statement; the jacket and shoes stay Jet Black rather than doubling the saturated green — the same "one loud element at a time" discipline used throughout the bottoms and tops system (barrel jean, baggy jean, tiered ruffle top).
- **Ring visibility, checked at every layer:** the dress sleeve is specced 3/4-length so the ring shows through motion; the jacket's sleeve is hemmed to the wrist per its mandatory spec, so putting it on for arrival/departure doesn't hide the ring either.
- **Two ring echoes, two different registers:** the pendant is a quiet, collarbone-level echo (color and motif, not scale); the jacket's chain-link trim is a loud, hardware-level echo (scale and material, not color). Together they reference the ring without either one competing with it directly.
- **Structure/soft balance even at statement scale:** the dress's structured bodice is the "hard architecture," its fluid skirt is the "soft shape" — the same soft-shape/hard-architecture logic the ring itself is built on, now expressed at full-outfit scale.

- This is the one look in the system where every single piece was chosen with the ring as the explicit design brief, not a secondary check — see the individual Starlink One Pairing sections in the dress, jacket, pump, and pendant files for the specifics.
