# The Heart-Emerald Pendant — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for jeweler quoting and prototyping
**Prepared for:** Fine jewelry manufacturer or bench jeweler — a direct commission from Starlink One's own jeweler is strongly preferred over a non-custom production house (see Section 9)
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending jeweler confirmation of stone sourcing, casting method, and finish process)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into metal, stone, and construction specs for an initial quoting and prototyping conversation. It is not a finalized production spec — Section 9 lists what needs jeweler confirmation before casting begins.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A sterling silver pendant necklace, heart-cut emerald or emerald-adjacent stone in an open-gallery four-prong setting, 20–24" adjustable chain. Two stone/setting configurations under consideration (Section 5); one shared chain and clasp spec across both.

## 2. Metal Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Metal | Sterling silver, 925 | No gold, gold vermeil, or rose gold anywhere on the piece — matches the wardrobe-wide hardware-metal rule |
| Finish | Unrhodium-plated, left to tarnish naturally | **Non-negotiable** — do not apply a standard rhodium flash as a default finishing step; confirm explicitly with the jeweler that this piece skips the finish most fine-jewelry houses apply by default |
| Chain type | Cable or box chain, 1.3–1.5mm gauge | Confirm factory-standard gauge increments against this range |
| Chain length | 20–24" fixed length plus 2" extender | Extender chain and clasp in matched sterling silver finish |
| Clasp | Lobster clasp, sterling silver | Standard secure, one-handed clasp |
| Bail | Soldered directly to setting gallery rail | Not a separate applied/pinch bail — confirm as a single continuous solder join, not a mechanically attached component |

## 3. Stone Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Stone (primary option) | Heart-cut genuine or lab-grown emerald | Confirm sourcing and cost against Section 9 |
| Stone (fallback option) | Heart-cut fine emerald-adjacent stone (green sapphire, tsavorite) or heart-cut CZ/glass in matched hue | For cost-sensitive or faster-turnaround sampling only — see Section 9 |
| Stone color match | Match to Starlink One's own emerald as closely as sourcing allows | Confirm against the ring itself in person if possible, not a color swatch alone — see `../../color-system.md` Starlink Emerald reference (`#0F5C3D`) as a directional guide, not a literal stone-color spec (gemstone color doesn't map 1:1 to a hex value) |
| Stone height (with setting) | 10–13mm | Companion scale to the ring's stone, not a match to it |
| Cut alternative (Configuration 2) | Faceted round or oval, no heart cut | See Section 5 |

## 4. Setting & Construction

- **Setting type:** four-prong, hand-set and hand-filed — not cast-and-trimmed prongs. Confirm the jeweler's standard process for hand-finishing prongs after casting, since this is a durability and comfort detail (unfiled prongs catch on hair and fabric).
- **Gallery:** open beneath the stone, light passing through from behind — confirm this is achievable with the final chosen stone cut and setting depth; a heart cut in particular can complicate open-gallery depth and may need jeweler input on minimum metal thickness for structural integrity.
- **Casting method:** confirm jeweler's standard (lost-wax casting is expected default) — no unusual requirement here, flagged only for completeness.
- **Polish:** standard sterling silver polish on delivery is acceptable — the tarnish-over-time instruction (Section 2) applies to ongoing wear and care, not the as-delivered finish.

## 5. Two Configurations Under Consideration

| Spec | Configuration 1 (flagship) | Configuration 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Stone cut | Heart | Round or oval, faceted |
| Reads as | Direct, literal companion to Starlink One | Quieter color-only echo, no shape repeat |
| Setting | Four-prong, open gallery | Four-prong or bezel, open gallery where cut allows |
| Recommended for | Primary sample | Secondary sample, evaluate alongside Configuration 1 before choosing final production configuration |

**Recommended approach:** sample both configurations in the first prototype round rather than committing to one from spec alone — the "too on-the-nose" concern named in the vision doc is a real styling judgment call that's easier to make against a physical piece than a drawing.

## 6. Starlink One Reference Match

Unlike every other manufacturing brief in this system, there is no separate "hidden reference detail" section here — the entire pendant *is* the Starlink One reference (see `vision.md` Section 5). The one technical requirement that follows from that: stone color and metal finish should be confirmed against the actual ring, not a generic emerald reference or the color system's hex value alone, since the whole point of the piece is recognizability as a companion object.

## 7. Sizing & Chain Notes

- **Chain length:** 20–24" fixed, no full resizing mechanism — confirm target length against where the collarbone sits on a 64" frame before finalizing chain order length. A 2" extender covers minor adjustment only.
- **No ring-sizing-equivalent step here** — unlike Starlink One itself, this piece has no wearer-specific sizing beyond chain length, which simplifies the production run relative to a ring commission.

## 8. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Jeweler Conversation

1. **Stone sourcing and cost** — genuine heart-cut emerald at this scale (10–13mm) carries meaningful cost and clarity variability; confirm lab-grown emerald and fine emerald-adjacent alternatives as real options before committing to genuine stone sourcing, and get pricing across all three tiers (genuine, lab-grown, emerald-adjacent) before deciding.
2. **Heart-cut stone availability at small scale** — heart cuts are less standardized than round/oval cuts industry-wide; confirm the jeweler's stone supplier can source a consistent heart cut at 10–13mm, or whether this requires a custom-cut order with its own lead time.
3. **Open-gallery setting depth with a heart cut** — confirm minimum metal thickness the jeweler needs to keep the setting structurally sound with an open gallery under a heart-shaped stone, which sits differently than a round stone in a four-prong mount.
4. **Rhodium-skip confirmation** — many fine-jewelry production houses rhodium-plate sterling silver by default; confirm explicitly and in writing that this run skips that step, since it's easy for a standard production line to apply it automatically.
5. **Color match against the physical ring** — confirm the jeweler can do an in-person or physical-sample color match against Starlink One itself, rather than working from a written color description alone.
6. **Bail/solder joint durability testing** — confirm the soldered bail can handle repeated daily-wear stress (clasp on/off, chain tension) without joint failure; request a stress-test sample if the jeweler offers one.

## 9. Requested Next Step

A direct commission inquiry with Starlink One's own jeweler is the preferred first step, not a generic fine-jewelry production house — this guarantees the closest possible finish and stone-color consistency with the ring itself, which is the entire premise of the piece. If a custom commission isn't feasible on cost or timeline, Catbird or Mejuri fine jewelry lines are the named fallback (see `../06-heart-emerald-pendant-necklace.md`), but treat that as a real compromise on the "companion piece" premise, not an equivalent substitute — flag the tradeoff explicitly rather than treating a fallback vendor's stock heart pendant as interchangeable with a true Starlink One match.
