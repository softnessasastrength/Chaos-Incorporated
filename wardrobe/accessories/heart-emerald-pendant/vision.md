# The Heart-Emerald Pendant — Design Vision

*The fine-jewelry companion piece to Starlink One — Experimental tier (Phase 3 Statement), and the most literal single-object echo of the ring anywhere in the accessories category. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the accessories catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing design doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Heart-Emerald Pendant.**

Where the Anchor Heel borrows Starlink One's *logic* (soft shape, hard architecture) and the chain-link belt borrows its *hardware motif*, this piece borrows the ring's actual subject: a heart, in emerald, held in sterling silver. It is not a variation on a theme — it is the same design statement, moved from the hand to the collarbone. That directness is deliberate rather than lazy. Every other Starlink One reference in the wardrobe exists to be quiet or partial (a debossed insole mark, a single thread of emerald, a chain-link scaled down to a buckle) precisely because the ring itself is the one place that reference is allowed to be loud. This pendant is the second place. It only works because it's rare — one piece, worn deliberately, not a second ring's worth of look-at-me in daily rotation.

The brief is: a second heart, sized and placed so it reads as a companion to Starlink One rather than a competitor to it. Smaller stone, quieter setting, worn at a different point on the body — near enough in language to be unmistakably from the same design family, far enough in scale to know its place in the hierarchy.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Chain length | 20–24", adjustable via a 2" extender at the clasp | Sits at or just below the collarbone on a 64" frame — long enough to clear the layering necklace set worn underneath, short enough that the pendant doesn't disappear into a neckline |
| Pendant height (stone + setting) | 10–13mm | Small-to-medium; a companion scale to the ring's own stone, not a match to it — see Section 3 |
| Chain gauge | 1.3–1.5mm cable or box chain | Substantial enough to feel secure and to visually hold its own under the pendant's weight; not so heavy it reads as a choker-adjacent statement piece |
| Closure | Lobster clasp, sterling silver, matched finish to the chain | Standard, secure, one-handed operable |
| Bail | Fixed or soldered bail, not a pinch bail | A pinch bail is the most common point of jewelry failure under daily wear; soldering it removes that failure mode entirely |

## 3. Stone & Setting — Two Configurations, Ranked

Unlike the wardrobe's leather-goods items, there's no ranked leather palette here — the real decision is stone-and-setting configuration, since the pendant's entire identity rests on getting the heart-emerald relationship right.

### 1st — Heart-Cut Emerald or Emerald-Adjacent Green Stone (recommended, flagship configuration)
- **Stone:** a genuine heart-cut emerald (or, for cost/durability reasons, a heart-cut lab-grown emerald or fine emerald-adjacent green stone — green sapphire, tsavorite, or high-quality glass/CZ in an emerald-matched hue as a lower-cost fallback).
- **Cut:** heart-shaped, matching Starlink One's own stone shape directly — this is the single detail that makes the piece read as a companion rather than a generic green-stone necklace.
- **Setting:** four-prong setting, open gallery beneath the stone (light passes through, same as the ring) — the same "soft shape held by visible engineering" logic Starlink One itself is built on, restated at pendant scale.
- **Why it fits the brief:** this is the configuration explicitly named in [`../../build-plan.md`](../../build-plan.md)'s Phase 3 scope — "a second heart or emerald-adjacent piece" — built to be the obvious, deliberate companion rather than a subtle nod.

### 2nd — Faceted Round or Oval Emerald-Adjacent Stone, No Heart Cut
- **Stone:** a simple faceted round or oval stone in Starlink Emerald's color family, no heart shape.
- **Setting:** four-prong or bezel, same open-gallery preference where the cut allows it.
- **Why it's ranked second:** the vision doc for the layering set already noted a literal heart repeat can read as "too on-the-nose" depending on how it's styled day to day — this configuration keeps the color echo and the metal language while dropping the shape repeat, for a quieter version of the same idea. Worth sampling alongside the flagship configuration rather than choosing blind.

## 4. Metal & Construction

- **Metal: sterling silver (925), unrhodium-plated** — no rhodium flash, no protective coating. This is not a corner cut; it's the same deliberate choice the Anchor Heel's aniline leather makes. Rhodium plating keeps silver bright and unchanging, which is exactly wrong here — the whole system's rule is that the ring's finish (and everything that echoes it) is meant to visibly tarnish and soften with wear, then get worn back to brightness at the high points through actual handling. A plated pendant would look identical on day one and day one thousand; this one is supposed to change.
- **Prong setting, hand-set** — four prongs, filed and polished by hand rather than cast-and-trimmed, so the prongs sit flush and don't catch on fabric or hair. This matters more here than on a ring, since a pendant sits against skin and clothing constantly rather than being handled deliberately.
- **Open gallery beneath the stone** — light passes through from behind, matching Starlink One's own construction. A closed-back setting (foil-backed or solid-backed) is explicitly out of system; it would make the stone read as flatter and less alive than the ring it's meant to echo.
- **Bail:** soldered directly to the setting's gallery rail, not a separate applied bail — one fewer solder joint is one fewer failure point on a piece meant for regular wear.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Direct, Not Hidden

Every other item in the wardrobe treats its Starlink One reference as something to be discovered — a debossed insole mark, a single thread of color, a chain-link motif scaled down until it reads as ordinary hardware. This piece is the one deliberate exception to that rule, and the exception is named rather than smoothed over: the whole reason this pendant exists is to be recognized, immediately, as coming from the same design language as the ring. Hiding that reference here would undercut the piece's entire purpose. The system can afford exactly one loud echo of Starlink One in daily rotation, and this is it — reserved for a going-out or occasion context, never competing with the ring itself for attention on any single look (see Section 6, Styling).

## 6. Sizing, Chain & Styling Notes

- **Chain length is the primary fit variable** — no resizing mechanism beyond the 2" extender, so length should be confirmed against where the collarbone sits on a 64" frame before ordering, not assumed from a generic "princess length" chart.
- **Pairs with:** the ring statement dress, the ribbon-tie camisole, any sleeveless or open-collar top where the collarbone is visible — see [`../06-heart-emerald-pendant-necklace.md`](../06-heart-emerald-pendant-necklace.md) for the full styling and pairing notes carried over from the original concept note.
- **Does not pair with:** the layering necklace/bracelet set worn at full volume — this piece wants room to be the one statement at the neck, not a fourth voice in a stack. If worn together, this pendant sits longest and outermost, alone.
- **Care:** no polishing, no rhodium re-plating request at any jeweler visit — tarnish is intentional, matching the ring's own care instructions exactly.
