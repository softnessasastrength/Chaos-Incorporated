# Heart-Emerald Pendant Necklace

**Category:** Accessories
**Subcategory:** Jewelry — Statement Pendant (Fine Jewelry)
**Priority:** Experimental (Phase 3 Statement)
**Status:** Concept

## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- Unlike the everyday layering set (Phase 1), this is a single statement piece meant to be worn deliberately, not in daily rotation — a heart-motif or emerald-adjacent pendant on a longer chain (20–24") that sits at or just below the collarbone, giving a second, quieter echo of the ring at a different point on the body than the hand.
- Proportion notes: a single pendant, not a cluster — at a smaller frame, one deliberate statement piece reads more intentional than several competing for attention.
- Comfort priority: a lightweight setting, and a chain gauge substantial enough to feel secure without being heavy enough to notice after an hour of wear.

## Technical Specs
- Chain length: 20–24"
- Pendant size: Small-to-medium — should read as a companion piece to the ring, not compete with it
- Closure type: Lobster clasp or spring ring

## Fabric & Feel
- Material: Sterling silver setting with a genuine or lab-grown emerald or emerald-adjacent green stone; heart motif optional (a direct callback to Starlink One's own heart-emerald design) or a simple faceted stone if a literal heart repeat feels too on-the-nose
- Hand-feel / skin feel: Smooth setting, no rough edges at the clasp or bail
- Breathability: N/A
- Break-in behavior: N/A — sterling silver will tarnish and develop patina exactly like the ring, by design

## Colorways
- Sterling silver plus emerald/emerald-adjacent stone only — no gold setting, per the closed hardware-metal rule

## Sourcing
- Brand / product line examples: a custom commission from Starlink One's own jeweler is strongly preferred here (guarantees finish and stone-color consistency with the ring itself); Catbird or Mejuri fine jewelry as a non-custom fallback
- Approx. price range: $200–$600+ depending on stone (custom commission likely higher)
- Where to buy: direct commission preferred; Catbirdnyc.com or Mejuri.com as fallback

## Styling & Pairings
- Pairs well with: the ring-statement dress, the ribbon-tie camisole, any sleeveless or open-collar top where the collarbone is visible
- Occasion: going-out, evening — the accessories category's rare-occasion statement piece
- Shoe pairings: N/A

## Starlink One Pairing
- Hand/wrist visibility: N/A — worn at the neck.
- Hardware finish: Sterling silver exclusively, unpolished to match the ring's patina.
- Ring echo: Direct, and the most literal in the entire wardrobe — explicitly named in `../build-plan.md`'s Phase 3 scope as "a second heart or emerald-adjacent piece," designed to be an obvious companion to Starlink One rather than a subtle one.

## Chaos Incorporated Notes
- Aesthetic tie-in: the accessories category's highest-intentionality piece, alongside the chain-link belt and layering set as the third and final direct execution of the ring's design language — this time as genuine fine jewelry rather than an everyday accessory.
- Modifications wanted: commission from the same jeweler as Starlink One if at all possible.
- Care instructions: no polishing — tarnish is intentional; rinse with water only if visibly dirty.
