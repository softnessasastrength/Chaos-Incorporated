# Bottoms — Category Priority Ranking

Ranked using the tiers in [`../priority-system.md`](../priority-system.md). This is the map for the whole `bottoms/` build-out — individual item files live under each subfolder.

## Ranking

| Rank | Category | Priority | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Jeans** | Must-Have | The foundational bottom. Highest weekly wear, widest styling range, most forgiving of mixing with every top/shoe in rotation. Gets the deepest system (see `jeans/jeans-template.md`). |
| 2 | **Yoga Pants / Leggings** | Must-Have | Comfort core of the wardrobe — loungewear, transit, errands, low-effort days. Needs real stretch-% and waistband engineering, not an afterthought category. |
| 3 | **Shorts** | High | Seasonal but heavy rotation in warm months; also covers sleep/lounge overlap (bike shorts, sweat shorts). |
| 4 | **Trousers (tailored)** | Medium | Occasional-use — dressier days, structured looks. Fewer pieces needed, but fit precision matters more per-piece since there's less stretch to hide error. |
| 5 | **Joggers / Sweatpants** | Medium | Comfort-adjacent to leggings but with a different silhouette logic (tapered hem, drawstring waist). Build after leggings system is solid. |
| 6 | **Skirts** | Experimental | Untested silhouette direction for this system — worth one or two bets once the core categories are dialed in. |
| 7 | **Overalls / Jumpsuit Bottoms** | Experimental | High personality, low frequency. A fun bet, not a load-bearing category. |

## Build Order

1. Jeans system (this drop) — full sub-style breakdown + 8–10 item files.
2. Leggings/yoga pants — 6–8 item files.
3. Shorts — 6–8 item files.
4. Trousers, joggers, skirts, overalls — backlog, build opportunistically.

## Cross-Category Rule

Every bottom in this system must independently satisfy the manifesto's fit rule for a 64" frame: **rise before volume, inseam documented, no "figure it out" hemming.** No category gets a pass on this just because it's lower priority.
