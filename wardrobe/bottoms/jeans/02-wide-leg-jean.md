# Wide-Leg Jean

**Category:** Bottoms
**Subcategory:** Jeans — Wide Leg
**Priority:** High
**Status:** Sourcing

## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- High rise is non-negotiable here — it's the only thing preventing the leg volume from visually shortening both torso and legs at once.
- Proportion notes: crop to ankle-length or just-below-ankle. A full-length wide leg on a 5'4" frame reads as being swallowed; a cropped one reads as intentional.
- Comfort priority: the leg itself touches nothing, so this is a genuinely breezy, unrestricted fit — the waistband is where all the fit-precision work needs to happen.

## Technical Specs
- Rise: High (11–12" front rise)
- Inseam / length: 26–27" cropped (ankle-length on a 5'4" frame) — do not order a standard 30–32" expecting to just "not hem it"
- Waist range / sizing notes: True to waist; substantial waistband (1.5"+) to prevent back-gap
- Stretch level: 1–2% elastane, or rigid 100% cotton if the cut is already generous through the hip
- Fabric weight: 12–13oz
- Closure type: Button-fly, high-rise 5-pocket

## Fabric & Feel
- Fabric composition: 99% cotton / 1% elastane, or rigid selvedge-style cotton
- Hand-feel / skin feel: Wants body and structure, not a soft drape-y hand — a fabric that's too soft collapses into a shapeless silhouette instead of holding a clean wide line
- Breathability: Excellent — high cotton content, loose fit
- Break-in behavior: Rigid denim will crease naturally at the hip/thigh with wear, which is desirable here (adds shape without losing structure)

## Colorways
- Dark indigo, unwashed or lightly washed (holds structure best)
- Ecru/off-white for warm-weather rotation

## Styling & Pairings
- Pairs well with: fitted, tucked, or cropped tops only — pairing wide-leg with an oversized top overloads the silhouette
- Occasion: elevated-casual, gallery/dinner/weekend-going-out
- Shoe pairings: heeled boot or platform sneaker — flat, low shoes lose the leg-lengthening ratio

## Chaos Incorporated Notes
- Aesthetic tie-in: this is the "soft volume held by hard structure" piece — loose fabric, but only functions correctly with a rigid waistband and a heel underneath doing the engineering work
- Modifications wanted: crop hem precisely rather than relying on a fold/cuff, which adds bulk exactly where it shouldn't
- Care instructions: cold wash, hang dry — avoid tumble drying to prevent shrinkage that changes the crop length
