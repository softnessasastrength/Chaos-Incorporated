# Jeans System — Master Reference

Jeans are the Must-Have foundation of the entire wardrobe. This document is the master reference for every jean style in the system — it exists so that no individual jean file has to re-derive fit logic from scratch. Individual item files (in this same folder) fill in specific product recommendations and colorways; this file is the "why" behind every one of those choices.

**Frame constant for all styles below:** 63" (5'3") adult frame. Every rise/inseam/volume note is written against that number, not generic sizing advice.

---

## How to Read This Document

For each silhouette:
- **Ideal rise** — where the waistband should sit, and why, for a shorter torso.
- **Stretch level** — target elastane %, and where the stretch needs to live (waist vs. seat vs. through the leg).
- **Fabric recommendation** — weight (oz) and blend that gives the right drape at this height without going stiff or bulky.
- **Why it works for a 63" frame** — the actual proportion math, not vibes.
- **Comfort / skin-feel notes** — what the denim should feel like against skin, all day, in the crotch curve, waistband, and knee.
- **Pairing ideas** — what tops/shoes/outerwear complete the silhouette.

---

## Straight-Leg Jean

- **Ideal rise:** Mid-to-high rise (sits at or just above natural waist). This is the single most reliable rise for 5'3" — it lengthens the leg line without the "cut in half" effect of low-rise.
- **Stretch level:** 2–3% elastane (98/2 or 97/3 cotton-elastane). Enough to move with the body through the hip and seat; too much stretch in a straight leg makes the hem cling and lose its clean drop.
- **Fabric recommendation:** Mid-weight denim, 11–12.5oz. Heavy enough to hold a clean vertical line without stiffness that fights movement.
- **Why it works for a 63" frame:** The straight leg is the safest volume decision at this height — it doesn't add width the way wide-leg does, and it doesn't shorten the leg the way a cuffed or bootcut hem can. Hemmed to just-above-the-shoe-top, it reads as intentional rather than "kid's jeans."
- **Comfort / skin-feel:** Look for a brushed or stone-washed interior finish — raw unwashed denim against skin for 10+ hours reads stiff and scratchy at the inner thigh by hour three. A few washes (or pre-washed retail denim) softens the hand-feel significantly.
- **Pairing ideas:** Fitted or slightly cropped tops (tucking works well at this rise), ankle boots or clean sneakers, a structured jacket to add the "hard" counterpart per the manifesto's soft/structure rule.

## Wide-Leg Jean

- **Ideal rise:** High rise, non-negotiable. High rise is what keeps a wide leg from visually shortening the torso and legs both at once — a low- or mid-rise wide leg on a 5'3" frame reads as being swallowed by fabric.
- **Stretch level:** 1–2% elastane, or a rigid (0% stretch) mid-weight denim if the fit is cut generously through the hip already. Wide-leg doesn't need stretch for mobility since the leg opening isn't fighting the body — it needs structure to hold its shape instead of collapsing into a shapeless drape.
- **Fabric recommendation:** Mid-weight, 12–13oz, with body (not overly soft/fluid) so the leg holds a crisp line from knee to hem rather than pooling.
- **Why it works for a 63" frame:** Wide-leg is the riskiest silhouette at this height and needs an explicit "exit strategy" per the manifesto: crop the hem to ankle-length or just-below-ankle (never a full break/pool onto the floor), and pair with a heel or platform sole to keep the leg-to-fabric ratio in proportion. Cropped + heeled is what makes this work; full-length + flat is what makes it swallow the frame.
- **Comfort / skin-feel:** Since the leg itself is roomy, all the comfort engineering lives in the waistband — it needs to be substantial (1.5"+) and either curved or well-boned so it doesn't gap at the back on a smaller waist-to-hip ratio.
- **Pairing ideas:** Fitted, tucked, or cropped top to balance the leg volume; heeled boot or platform sneaker; a fitted jacket, not an oversized one (two oversized elements at once is where "controlled" chaos becomes just chaos).

## Skinny Jean

- **Ideal rise:** Mid-rise. High-rise skinny can feel restrictive at the natural waist for all-day wear; low-rise reintroduces the torso-shortening problem. Mid-rise is the comfort/proportion sweet spot for this specific silhouette.
- **Stretch level:** 8–12% elastane (or elastane + a recovery fiber like Lycra/EA). This is the one silhouette in the system where higher stretch is correct, not a compromise — the fit is close to the leg through the knee and calf, and without real recovery stretch it will bag at the knee within a few wears.
- **Fabric recommendation:** Lightweight-to-mid, 9–11oz, brushed or with a soft finish. Heavier denim in a skinny cut can feel restrictive at the knee when sitting.
- **Why it works for a 63" frame:** Skinny is naturally leg-lengthening on a shorter frame since it removes fabric volume entirely — no proportion trick required. The main risk is ankle-cropping too short, which can shorten the leg line; hem should hit at or just below the ankle bone.
- **Comfort / skin-feel:** Because this fit sits close to skin at the knee and calf for hours, prioritize a soft-brushed interior and confirm recovery stretch (does it bag out by end of day, or spring back?). A poor-recovery skinny jean is the single most common "why don't I wear this anymore" complaint in this category.
- **Pairing ideas:** Oversized or slightly cropped tops both work (skinny is the most pairing-flexible leg); ankle boots, sneakers, or heels all sit cleanly against the narrow hem.

## Baggy / Loose Jean

- **Ideal rise:** High rise. Same logic as wide-leg — high rise anchors the waist and prevents the loose volume from reading as "wearing something too big."
- **Stretch level:** 0–2% elastane. Baggy fits are cut with intentional excess room, so stretch fiber isn't needed for mobility; a slightly rigid hand actually helps the silhouette hold its shape (a stretchy baggy jean tends to droop and lose shape by midday).
- **Fabric recommendation:** Mid-weight, 12–13.5oz, classic indigo or washed finish. This is the style where a visible, characterful wash (whiskering, fading) does the most aesthetic work.
- **Why it works for a 63" frame:** The baggy leg needs the same "exit strategy" as wide-leg but more aggressively — crop at or above the ankle, cuff once, and keep the rise high and the waist visually defined (a belt helps) so the eye reads "waist-in, leg-out" instead of "shapeless." This is an Experimental-tier bet for a reason: it's easy to get wrong at this height.
- **Comfort / skin-feel:** The most all-day-comfortable silhouette in the system by design — nothing about it should touch the leg tightly at any point. Confirm the waistband still fits snugly even though the leg is loose; a baggy jean with a loose waistband will slide and need constant adjusting.
- **Pairing ideas:** Fitted, tucked, or cropped tops only (this is the one silhouette where pairing with another loose piece reads as sloppy rather than chaotic-on-purpose); chunky sneakers or boots to match the leg's proportions.

## Mom Jean

- **Ideal rise:** High rise, tapered leg. The defining trait of a mom jean is the high waist + relaxed hip + tapered ankle combination.
- **Stretch level:** 2–4% elastane. Enough for all-day seated comfort at the hip/thigh, low enough that the tapered leg holds its slightly boxy shape rather than clinging.
- **Fabric recommendation:** Mid-weight, 11–12oz, classic or vintage-style wash — this silhouette is meant to reference '80s/'90s denim, so a period-appropriate wash reinforces the shape.
- **Why it works for a 63" frame:** The tapered ankle is the key proportion tool here — it narrows the leg line right where it matters (the visible lower leg) while the roomier hip/thigh stays comfortable. High rise plus tapered ankle is one of the most universally flattering combinations for a shorter torso.
- **Comfort / skin-feel:** Relaxed through the seat and thigh means no crotch-curve tightness even sitting for long periods — this is a genuinely all-day-comfortable cut, closer to leggings-comfort than skinny-jean-comfort.
- **Pairing ideas:** Tucked-in fitted or cropped tops (the high rise wants to be shown, not hidden), sneakers for a casual look or boots for a slightly elevated one.

## Bootcut Jean

- **Ideal rise:** Mid rise. Bootcut's slight flare from the knee down does the leg-lengthening work, so the rise doesn't need to be as high as wide-leg/baggy to achieve the same effect.
- **Stretch level:** 2–3% elastane. Similar to straight-leg — enough for hip/seat mobility, not so much that the flare from the knee loses its shape.
- **Fabric recommendation:** Mid-weight, 11–12oz, with enough body to hold the flare's shape over a boot without collapsing.
- **Why it works for a 63" frame:** The slight flare from the knee is specifically designed to skim over a boot and create an unbroken vertical line from hip to floor — historically one of the most leg-lengthening cuts that exists, and one of the most reliable choices for a shorter frame. Hem length must be long enough to break just at the boot, or the leg-lengthening effect is lost.
- **Comfort / skin-feel:** Comfortable through hip and seat; the flare itself doesn't touch the leg, so there's no knee/calf tightness the way a skinny jean has.
- **Pairing ideas:** This is the one silhouette in the system built specifically around boots — heeled ankle boot or a low block heel; fitted or slightly tucked tops to keep the eye on the vertical line.

## Barrel / Balloon Jean

- **Ideal rise:** High rise, and the waist must be tightly fitted even though the leg is voluminous — barrel-leg is defined by a fitted waist that balloons out through the thigh and tapers back in at the ankle.
- **Stretch level:** 1–2% elastane. The volume in this cut comes from pattern-cutting, not stretch, so a slightly rigid hand holds the "balloon" shape better than a stretchy one (which sags rather than curves).
- **Fabric recommendation:** Mid-weight, 12–13oz, since the fabric needs enough structure to hold a curved silhouette rather than draping flat.
- **Why it works for a 63" frame:** This is genuinely the hardest silhouette in the system to get right at this height — the ankle taper helps (it reintroduces a defined leg line at the bottom), but the thigh volume needs to be proportionate to the frame, not borrowed at full scale from a taller runway sample. Flagged Experimental for exactly this reason: source a size run cut for petite proportions if at all possible, don't just size down a standard cut.
- **Comfort / skin-feel:** Genuinely excellent all-day comfort through the thigh (nothing touches the leg there at all); watch the ankle taper for tightness, since that's the one point of contact.
- **Pairing ideas:** Fitted, cropped top; the ankle taper wants to be seen, so an ankle boot or low sneaker that doesn't compete with the silhouette's own taper.

## Flare Jean

- **Ideal rise:** High rise. Flare is the most rise-sensitive silhouette after wide-leg — the waist-to-hip-to-flare line only reads correctly with a high, well-defined waistband.
- **Stretch level:** 2–4% elastane, concentrated through the seat/thigh (the fitted part of the leg above the flare) since the flare portion itself doesn't need stretch.
- **Fabric recommendation:** Mid-weight, 11–12.5oz, with a soft-but-structured hand so the flare moves (swings when walking) rather than hanging stiff.
- **Why it works for a 63" frame:** Flare is a leg-lengthening cut when the fitted-through-the-thigh-to-flare-from-the-knee ratio is right — the fitted portion should end at or just below the knee, giving the flare enough length to lengthen the leg without pooling on the ground. Hem must hit at the top of the shoe, exactly, or the leg-lengthening effect flips into looking like too much extra fabric.
- **Comfort / skin-feel:** Fitted through hip/thigh similarly to a straight leg, so comfort notes mirror straight-leg — brushed interior finish matters for all-day wear.
- **Pairing ideas:** Fitted, tucked tops (this silhouette wants a defined waist on top too); platform or heeled shoe to keep the hem breaking correctly at shoe-top.

## Cross-Style Rules (apply to every jean, every silhouette)

1. **Rise is decided before wash, distressing, or color.** Get the rise right first; everything else is decoration on top of a correct fit.
2. **Every jean gets a documented hem plan.** "Buy and wear as-is" is not acceptable for any silhouette that isn't already cropped/ankle-length by design — see each style's note above.
3. **Stretch-recovery matters more than stretch-%.** A jean with 2% elastane and good recovery fiber will outperform one with 5% elastane and poor recovery (bags out, loses shape). Check recovery claims, not just the % on the tag.
4. **Skin-feel is documented per item, not assumed.** "It's denim" is not a comfort note. Every item file specifies interior finish (raw, brushed, sanded) because that's what's actually touching skin for 10+ hours.
5. **Petite-specific size runs are preferred over "size down" as a workaround** wherever the brand offers them — sizing down a standard-length cut changes waist/hip proportions, not just length.
