# Bootcut Jean

**Category:** Bottoms
**Subcategory:** Jeans — Bootcut
**Priority:** Medium
**Status:** Concept

## Why It Works for Her Frame (63")
- The slight flare from the knee down is one of the most reliably leg-lengthening cuts that exists, historically built specifically to skim over a boot and create an unbroken vertical line from hip to floor.
- Proportion notes: hem length must break exactly at the top of a boot — too short loses the effect, too long drags and shortens the line.
- Comfort priority: comfortable through hip and seat like a straight leg; the flare itself doesn't contact the leg, so no knee/calf tightness.

## Technical Specs
- Rise: Mid-rise (10" front rise)
- Inseam / length: 29–30" (to break correctly over a boot heel)
- Waist range / sizing notes: True to waist
- Stretch level: 2–3% elastane
- Fabric weight: 11–12oz
- Closure type: Button-fly, standard 5-pocket

## Fabric & Feel
- Fabric composition: 97% cotton / 3% elastane
- Hand-feel / skin feel: Mid-weight with enough body to hold the flare's shape over a boot without collapsing flat
- Breathability: Good
- Break-in behavior: Softens with wash without losing the knee-down flare structure

## Colorways
- Dark indigo (most versatile with boots)
- Black for evening pairing

- Shoe pairings: this is the one silhouette in the system explicitly built around boots — heeled ankle boot or low block heel
