# Camp Collar Print Shirt (Hawaiian-Inspired)

**Category:** Tops
**Subcategory:** Shirts — Camp Collar Print
**Priority:** Experimental
**Status:** Concept

## Why It Works for Her Frame (63")
- The camp collar (open, no top button, flat lay) sits closer to the body than a standard point collar, which keeps the neckline from adding visual width at the shoulder — important since this is a boxier, straight-cut shirt overall.
- Proportion notes: body length is cropped to hip-length, not the longer, looser resort-shirt cut this style is usually sold in; can be worn open over a fitted base or buttoned with a front tuck/knot, same volume logic as the flannel shirt. Short sleeve (elbow or above) is the default length, which incidentally solves the ring-visibility question for free — no cuff to manage at all.
- Comfort priority: a lightweight, breathable woven (not the stiff rayon most stock Hawaiian shirts use) — meant to actually be worn, not just photographed once.

## Technical Specs
- Rise: N/A
- Inseam / length: Body length ~23–25" (hip-length — cropped from the standard longer resort-shirt cut)
- Waist range / sizing notes: Relaxed straight fit, true to size to one size up — less oversized than the flannel shirt, since this piece doesn't carry the same "borrowed from a boyfriend" volume story
- Stretch level: Minimal, 0–2% elastane
- Fabric weight: Light, 110–140 gsm
- Closure type: Button-front, camp collar (no collar-point button), silver-tone or matte pewter buttons

## Fabric & Feel
- Fabric composition: Cotton poplin or linen-cotton blend
- Hand-feel / skin feel: Soft, lightweight, slightly crisp but not stiff
- Breathability: Excellent
- Break-in behavior: Minimal — the linen blend softens slightly with wash

## Colorways
This is the one piece in the system given the most explicit print latitude, per the print exception in [`../../color-system.md`](../../color-system.md)'s "Breaking the Palette" section — ground color stays on-palette, secondary print colors range wider than the closed 9:
- **Cloud Ivory ground** with a reimagined tropical leaf/floral print in **Starlink Emerald, Sage, and Periwinkle** — the flagship colorway, a deliberately "cool-toned Hawaiian" rather than the traditional warm red/orange/gold register most stock resort shirts use.
- **Sage ground** with a tonal leaf print in Starlink Emerald and Cloud Ivory (quieter, tone-on-tone option).

**Explicitly avoid:** stock warm-toned Hawaiian prints — hibiscus reds, mango oranges, sunset golds. These fight the cool undertone the entire palette is built around. If a cool-direction print can't be sourced or custom-printed, this item should not be substituted with a warm stock print — that's a color-system violation, not a compromise.

## Starlink One Pairing
- Hand/wrist visibility: Short sleeve by default — wrist and hand fully visible with no spec risk at all, one of the lowest-risk pieces in the system for ring visibility, same "no sleeve to manage" logic as the quilted vest.
- Hardware finish: Silver-tone or matte pewter buttons, specified explicitly since stock resort shirts commonly use natural coconut-shell buttons (a warm brown tone) or gold-tone snaps, both off-system.
- Ring echo: Direct — the flagship Cloud Ivory colorway's print includes Starlink Emerald as a primary print color, making this one of the more overt (if playful) ring-color echoes in the tops system, in the same spirit as the ribbon-tie camisole's emerald colorway.

## Chaos Incorporated Notes
- Aesthetic tie-in: the system's one deliberate "fun bet" print direction — Experimental tier because the cool-toned tropical print is an unvalidated aesthetic direction, not because the camp-collar silhouette itself is risky (it's broadly flattering and low-risk on its own).
- Modifications wanted: crop to hip length from any longer stock resort-shirt cut; confirm the print direction is genuinely cool-toned before committing to a print run — "a Hawaiian shirt in green" isn't automatically on-palette if the greens/accents lean warm.
- Care instructions: cold wash, hang dry to preserve print vibrancy and prevent shrinkage in the cotton-poplin weave.
