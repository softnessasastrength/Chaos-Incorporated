# Oversized Flannel Shirt

**Category:** Tops
**Subcategory:** Shirts — Oversized Flannel
**Priority:** High
**Status:** Concept

## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- The boyfriend/oversized fit is intentionally roomy through the body, so it gets the same "volume needs an exit strategy" treatment as every other oversized piece in this system: worn open over a fitted basics tank, or half-tucked/knotted at the front hem to reintroduce a waistline. It's not meant to be worn buttoned-and-loose with nothing else doing proportion work.
- Proportion notes: body length hits mid-hip to high-thigh — long enough to read as an intentional "boyfriend shirt," short enough that on a 5'4" frame it doesn't run past where a dress would end. Sleeve length is the critical spec: a men's-inspired oversized cut runs long by default, so the cuff needs to land at or just above the wrist bone through petite sizing or a deliberate roll, not a sleeve that swallows the hand.
- Comfort priority: a wear-it-into-the-ground comfort piece — brushed, pre-washed flannel that's already soft, not stiff off-the-shelf flannel that takes months to break in.

## Technical Specs
- Rise: N/A
- Inseam / length: Body length ~27–29" (mid-hip to high-thigh on a 5'4" frame)
- Waist range / sizing notes: Intentionally oversized through the body — size up 1–2 sizes from her fitted-tee size for the boyfriend proportion, not true-to-size
- Stretch level: Minimal, 0–2% elastane in the cotton-flannel weave — a little give at the shoulder seam, not a stretch-forward fabric
- Fabric weight: Mid-weight brushed flannel, 5–6oz
- Closure type: Button-front, silver-tone snap or matte pewter button — not the gold-tone snap most stock flannels ship with

## Fabric & Feel
- Fabric composition: 100% cotton or cotton-poly brushed flannel
- Hand-feel / skin feel: Soft-brushed on both faces, not just the interior — worn open over bare arms as often as buttoned, so exterior hand-feel matters as much as interior
- Breathability: Good — mid-weight layering piece
- Break-in behavior: Pre-softened/pre-washed finish preferred at purchase; continues to soften with wash — one of the most literal "wear it in, don't varnish it" pieces in the whole system

## Styling & Pairings
- Pairs well with: open over a basics tank or the ribbon-tie camisole, half-tucked into straight or mom jeans, or knotted at the front hem over leggings
- Occasion: everyday, cool-weather, low-effort comfort with intention
- Shoe pairings: sneakers, ankle boots, slides

## Starlink One Pairing
- Hand/wrist visibility: Cuff must land at or above the wrist bone at the standard roll — flagged explicitly, since an oversized flannel sleeve is one of the higher sleeve-length risk items in the system, same category as the kimono duster and the chunky cardigan.
- Hardware finish: Silver-tone or matte pewter snap/button, specified explicitly since stock flannel defaults to gold-tone hardware.
- Ring echo: None direct — this is a quiet, comfort-first base layer. The plaid can optionally carry a thin Starlink Emerald windowpane line as a subtle print variant, tying to the anchor color without turning the whole shirt into a color statement.

## Chaos Incorporated Notes
- Aesthetic tie-in: the most literal "patina over polish" fabric in the entire wardrobe — flannel is designed to be worn in, and this piece leans all the way into that manifesto principle.
- Modifications wanted: source or custom-weave the plaid in an on-palette colorway rather than accepting stock red/gold/rust flannel, which would break the closed-palette rule for a High-priority piece.
- Care instructions: cold wash, tumble dry low — flannel actually improves with a few dryer cycles, one of the only pieces in the system where machine drying is the correct instruction rather than hang-dry.
