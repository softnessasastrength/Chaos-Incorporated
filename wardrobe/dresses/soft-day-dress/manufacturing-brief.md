# The Soft Day Dress — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for pattern development and factory quoting
**Prepared for:** Garment manufacturer producing washable cotton/linen everyday dresses — a standard cut-and-sew production house is appropriate here, unlike the fit-focused atelier recommended for the Ring Statement Dress, since this piece's whole brief runs against couture-level intervention
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending manufacturer confirmation of fabric shrinkage behavior and pocket construction)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into pattern, fabric, and construction specs for an initial quoting and sampling conversation. It is not a finalized production spec — Section 7 lists what needs manufacturer confirmation before pattern grading begins.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A fitted-or-semi-fitted bodice dress with an A-line, gathered, or lightly tiered skirt, in washable cotton, linen-cotton, or soft rayon crepe, 33–36" length, with in-seam pockets and a smocked, seamed, or tied waist. Multiple colorways (Section 5) share one pattern and construction method.

## 2. Pattern & Construction Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Length | 33–36" from waist | Confirm finished length on a fit sample against a 64" reference height, not flat pattern measurement alone |
| Bodice | Fitted or semi-fitted, princess or side-bust seaming | Lower structural tolerance than the Ring Statement Dress — no boning or shaping liner required |
| Skirt | A-line, gathered, or light tier | Confirm gather ratio at waist seam allows full range of everyday movement (sitting, bending) without waistband strain |
| Waist | Smocked back, seamed, or tie closure | Smocked back is the preferred default — confirm manufacturer's standard smocking method and stretch recovery over repeated wear/wash cycles |
| Pockets | In-seam, side seam placement | **Required feature, not optional** — confirm pocket bag depth and placement doesn't create visible bulge through the skirt fabric at the specified weight |
| Closure | Side zip, back zip, or pull-on (smocked) | Pull-on smocked preferred where the pattern allows — lowest-friction everyday closure |

## 3. Fabric Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fabric type | Cotton poplin, linen-cotton blend, soft rayon crepe, or smocked cotton | Confirm manufacturer's stock options against this list |
| Weight | 100–180 gsm | Light enough for warm-weather breathability, substantial enough to hold bodice shape without full lining |
| Shrinkage | Confirm pre-shrunk or accounted-for shrinkage in pattern grading | This is a machine-washable garment by design (Section 6) — shrinkage must be built into the pattern, not discovered after the first customer wash |
| Colorfastness | Confirm wash-fastness, especially for the on-palette floral print option | A print or solid that fades after repeated washing works directly against this piece's "wash-and-wear, improves with washing" brief |

## 4. Construction Detail

- **Bodice seaming:** princess or side-bust seams sufficient for shape — no boning channels, no shaping liner. Confirm the manufacturer isn't defaulting to a more structured build than this brief calls for; over-engineering this piece adds cost without adding value against its actual everyday-wear job.
- **Pocket construction:** in-seam pockets, confirm bag fabric (can be a lighter-weight lining scrap rather than shell fabric) and depth sufficient for a phone without distorting the skirt's outer silhouette.
- **Hem finish:** simple durable hem (clean-finished or narrow rolled) — no couture-level hand-finishing required; this is a wash-and-wear garment.
- **Smocking (if chosen for waist):** confirm manufacturer's standard smocking technique and stretch recovery across repeated wear/wash — smocking that loses elasticity after a season of washing undercuts the whole "engineered for daily wear" premise.

## 6. Care & Durability Requirements

- **Machine washable preferred, hang dry** — this is the lowest-maintenance care profile of any dress in the system; confirm final fabric and construction choices don't quietly push this toward a hand-wash-only or dry-clean-only care label, which would undercut the entire "worn constantly, not admired occasionally" design brief.
- **Wash-and-improve behavior expected on cotton/linen options** — unlike the Ring Statement Dress's "minimal break-in, fits correctly from first wear" requirement, this piece is expected to soften with washing over time; confirm this is communicated to the manufacturer as an intended behavior, not a defect to engineer against.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Manufacturer Conversation

1. **Shrinkage accounting** — confirm pre-shrunk fabric or built-in shrinkage allowance in the pattern, tested across a realistic number of wash cycles, not just one.
2. **Pocket bulge at skirt silhouette** — confirm pocket construction doesn't visibly distort the A-line/gather/tier silhouette from the outside at the specified fabric weight.
3. **Smocking durability** — confirm the smocked-waist option (if chosen) retains elasticity and shape across repeated washing, not just on a fresh sample.
4. **Print colorfastness** — confirm the floral print option (and the Emerald solid, if piece-dyed) holds color through repeated washing consistent with the "machine washable" care label.
5. **Colorway consistency across a washable base** — confirm the Starlink Emerald color reads consistently against the system's reference in a lighter, washable fabric base versus the heavier formal fabrics used on other dresses in this catalogue.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A single fit sample in Cloud Ivory, standard/reference size, put through at least three wash cycles before final pattern and fabric approval — this piece's entire value proposition depends on holding up to repeated washing without losing fit, shape, or color, so a single fresh sample isn't sufficient validation the way it might be for a dry-clean-only occasion piece. Confirm pocket construction and smocking durability (if used) specifically survive that wash-test process before moving to a full colorway run.
