# The Soft Formal Midi Dress — Design Vision

*The Soft Formal kit's workhorse — High tier within Soft Formal specifically (still not a wardrobe-wide Must-Have), and the piece meant to be sourced first in the formal kit because it covers the widest range of formal occasions on its own. Doctrine: [`../../formal-system.md`](../../formal-system.md). See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the dresses category and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the doctrine underneath it.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Soft Formal Midi Dress.**

This piece sits at the center of the Soft Formal doctrine's whole reason for existing: most formal occasions — a wedding guest invitation, a daytime ceremony, a "black-tie optional" event where a full gown would over-dress the room — call for something more considered than the Soft Day Dress but genuinely lower-commitment than the Long Formal Dress or even the Cocktail/Evening Dress's night-specific register. Tea-to-midi length is the answer: formal enough to read as dressed-for-the-occasion, versatile enough to actually get re-worn across multiple events rather than photographed once and retired. That re-wearability is the entire design brief, stated plainly in the original concept note as "source first in Soft Formal kit" — this is the piece the rest of the formal system assumes already exists in the closet before the cocktail dress or the long dress get added on top.

The frame-specific case for tea/midi length matters more here than it does for most silhouette decisions in this system: at 64", a floor-length formal piece risks real fabric pooling and requires the heel-height-first sequencing the Long Formal Dress's own vision doc names explicitly, while a too-short formal length can read as cocktail-register rather than ceremony-appropriate. Tea-to-midi threads that needle — below the knee, above the ankle — delivering genuine leg-lengthening without either failure mode.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |

## 3. Fabric & Feel

- **Fabric: stretch crepe, soft satin, or quality ponte formal-weight fabric** — explicitly "re-wearable, not crackly costume," a phrase carried directly from the original concept note because it captures the exact failure mode this piece is designed against. A lot of mass-market formal-wear defaults to stiff, shiny, single-wear-feeling fabric; this piece rejects that default the same way the rest of the system rejects "looks right in its first week" leather.
- **Weight:** mid, 160–240 gsm crepe/satin — substantial enough to hold formal structure through a ceremony and a full meal seated, without crossing into stiff or heavy.
- **Hand-feel:** smooth lining, no scratchy seams at the underarm — a small detail, but one worth naming explicitly because underarm seam irritation is a common real-world formal-wear complaint that a beautiful fabric choice alone doesn't solve.
- **Break-in behavior:** minimal, same rule as the Ring Statement Dress — this piece must fit correctly on the first wear, since "improves with age" isn't the relevant metric for an occasion garment worn a handful of times per year.

## 4. Waist & Fit Construction

- **Defined waist, true to size with stretch** — the elastane content (4–8%) should deliver comfort through a seated ceremony and meal without needing a separate structural intervention the way the Ring Statement Dress's higher-stakes fitted bodice does. This piece's fit tolerance is real but more forgiving; it doesn't need boning or a shaping liner to hit its brief.
- **Sit-test requirement:** confirmed comfort sitting through a ceremony and a meal is named explicitly as a fit requirement, not an afterthought — a formal dress that only looks right standing has failed a real use case this piece is specifically built around.
- **Petite-length options:** named in the original concept note's modification wishlist — worth flagging early given the frame-specific length sensitivity in Section 2.

## 5. Colorways

- **Cloud Ivory, Blush Mauve, Midnight Navy, Jet Black** — the core rotation, covering the full range of formal occasion tones from daytime-appropriate to evening-appropriate.
- **Starlink Emerald** — available, but with an explicit sequencing rule: only source in Emerald if not also planning to buy the Ring Statement Dress for the same season, since the two pieces would otherwise compete for the same "ring color lead" role across a single formal season's wardrobe.
- **Sage** — the quiet formal alternative, for occasions wanting a formal silhouette without a fully neutral or fully saturated color statement.

## 6. Starlink One Reference & Styling

- **Hand/wrist visibility:** sleeve at 3/4 or sleeveless — wrist stays clear, consistent with the system-wide rule.
- **Hardware finish:** silver-tone zip only — the quietest hardware footprint of any formal piece, appropriate to this dress's "workhorse," not "statement," role.
- **Ring echo:** the quiet colorways let the ring lead without competing; if the Emerald colorway is chosen, that look should skip Emerald shoes and the Heart-Emerald Pendant, same one-Emerald-lead-per-look rule applied everywhere else in the system.
- **Rejected hardware note carried from sourcing:** BHLDN guest midi options are named explicitly in the original concept note as a line to check carefully and reject if gold hardware is present — a useful reminder that formal-wear sourcing is exactly where the silver-only rule is most likely to get quietly violated by an otherwise well-fitting piece.

## 7. Sourcing & Occasion Notes

- **Reference lines:** Reformation formal midi, Doen elevated dress, Aritzia dressy midi, Sézane formal crepe — all already at the "considered but re-wearable" register this piece targets.
- **Shoe pairings:** block heel pump, the Anchor Heel, or the Barely There sandal's dressy slide in warm weather.
- **Layering:** the evening wrap or the wool coat (cold season) both extend this piece's occasion range without changing the base garment.
- **Occasion:** wedding guest, daytime formal, and black-tie-optional events where midi length is accepted — the widest occasion range of any Soft Formal piece, which is exactly why it's the kit's recommended first purchase.
- **Care:** dry clean or gentle wash depending on final fabric — hang to dry regardless of method chosen.
