# The Soft Formal Midi Dress — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for pattern development and factory quoting
**Prepared for:** Garment manufacturer producing re-wearable formal-weight dresses — a mid-tier formal cut-and-sew house is appropriate here, between the fit-focused atelier recommended for the Ring Statement Dress and the standard production house recommended for the Soft Day Dress
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending manufacturer confirmation of heel-dependent length verification process and sit-test protocol)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into pattern, fabric, and construction specs for an initial quoting and sampling conversation. It is not a finalized production spec — Section 7 lists what needs manufacturer confirmation before pattern grading begins.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A fitted-or-semi-fitted, tea-to-midi length dress in stretch crepe, soft satin, or quality ponte, with 3/4/cap/sleeveless sleeve options (optional wrap), invisible zip closure, and a defined stretch waist. Six colorways share one pattern and construction method.

## 2. Pattern & Construction Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Length | 38–42" typical tea/midi at 64" with intended heel | **Gating spec** — must be verified against a live fit sample wearing the actual intended shoe (or a matched heel-height mock), not measured against a flat pattern or a barefoot fit check |
| Bodice | Fitted or semi-fitted | True to size with 4–8% elastane — no boning or shaping liner required, unlike the Ring Statement Dress |
| Skirt | Soft A-line or gentle column | Explicitly not ballgown volume — confirm skirt pattern doesn't drift toward excess volume in translation from sketch to pattern |
| Sleeve | 3/4, cap, sleeveless (optional wrap) | Confirm wrap attachment method if offered as an add-on component rather than integrated into the base garment |
| Closure | Invisible zip | Standard |

## 3. Fabric Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fabric type | Stretch crepe, soft satin, or quality ponte | Confirm manufacturer's formal-weight stock against this list — reject fabric samples that read as stiff or "costume-like" per the vision doc's explicit rejection of that failure mode |
| Weight | 160–240 gsm | Substantial enough for formal structure through a seated ceremony and meal |
| Stretch content | 4–8% elastane | Lower stretch tolerance than the Ring Statement Dress's 6–10%, reflecting this piece's more forgiving (not higher-stakes) fit brief |
| Underarm seam finish | Smooth, non-scratchy | Confirm finishing method (bound seam, flat-felled, or seam tape) at the underarm specifically — a named comfort requirement, not a generic finishing note |

## 4. Fit & Sit-Test Protocol

- **Sit-test requirement:** confirm the manufacturer includes a seated-wear test (ceremony + meal duration, minimum 60–90 minutes seated) as part of fit-sample evaluation, not just a standing fit check. This is named explicitly because seated comfort is a real, distinct use case for this specific piece that a standing-only fitting can miss entirely.
- **Petite-length option:** confirm whether the manufacturer offers a petite length grading as a standard option or whether this requires a custom secondary pattern — flagged given the frame-specific length sensitivity in Section 2.
- **Heel-height-dependent length verification:** confirm the fit sample is evaluated with the actual intended shoe (block heel pump or Anchor Heel height) rather than barefoot or in a generic heel — length verification without a matched heel height risks approving a hem that reads differently once the actual shoe pairing is worn.

**Recommended build order:** sample Cloud Ivory or Midnight Navy first — both are the most broadly re-wearable colorways and the safest choice for validating the sit-test protocol and heel-dependent length verification before expanding to the full six-colorway run.

## 6. Starlink One Reference Match

Silver-tone zip pull only — the lightest hardware footprint of any formal piece in this catalogue, consistent with this dress's "workhorse," not "statement," role. No debossed, engraved, or chain-link detail required or wanted anywhere on the garment.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Manufacturer Conversation

1. **Heel-dependent length verification** — confirm the manufacturer's fit-sample process accounts for the intended heel height rather than evaluating length barefoot or with a generic reference heel.
2. **Sit-test protocol** — confirm a seated-wear fit evaluation (60–90 minutes) is part of standard fit-sample review, not solely a standing fit check.
3. **Petite-length grading availability** — confirm whether this exists as a standard secondary pattern option or requires custom development.
4. **Underarm seam finishing** — confirm the specific finishing method used at the underarm and that it meets the "smooth, non-scratchy" requirement across all six colorway fabric lots, since dye process can occasionally affect fabric hand-feel at the seam.
5. **Wrap attachment (if offered)** — confirm construction method for the optional wrap component and whether it's sold as an integrated design element or separate accessory piece.
6. **Emerald dye match** — confirm against the color system reference; flag the sequencing/styling note from `vision.md` Section 5 to whoever manages the purchasing plan, even though it isn't itself a manufacturing requirement.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A single fit sample in Cloud Ivory or Midnight Navy, standard/reference size, evaluated with the intended heel height and put through the sit-test protocol (Section 4), before any multi-colorway production commitment. This is named as the Soft Formal kit's recommended first purchase in `vision.md` Section 1 — the sample is worth prioritizing ahead of the Cocktail/Evening Dress and Long Formal Dress samples, since this piece is meant to already exist in the closet before the rest of the formal kit gets built out.
