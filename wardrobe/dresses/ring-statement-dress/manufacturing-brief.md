# The Ring Statement Dress — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for pattern development and factory quoting
**Prepared for:** Garment manufacturer or made-to-order atelier — a fit-focused small-batch or made-to-measure producer is preferred over a mass-production cut-and-sew house given the fitted-bodice tolerance this piece requires (see Section 8)
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending manufacturer confirmation of fabric sourcing, boning/liner method, and fit-sample process)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into pattern, fabric, and construction specs for an initial quoting and sampling conversation. It is not a finalized production spec — Section 8 lists what needs manufacturer confirmation before pattern grading begins.

## 1. Product Overview

A fitted, structured-bodice midi-to-knee-length dress in stretch crepe, scuba knit, or ponte, with a 3/4-length or cropped set-in sleeve, defined waist, and back or hidden-side zip closure. Three colorways share one pattern and construction method (Section 6).

## 2. Pattern & Fit Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Silhouette | Fitted bodice and waist, semi-fitted skirt | Defined waistline via princess seaming, not stretch fabric alone |
| Hem length | 33–35" from natural waist (knee) | Confirm finished length against a size-run fit sample, not flat pattern measurement alone |
| Sleeve length | 3/4-length or cropped (elbow-to-just-below-elbow) | **Gating spec** — confirm against a live fit model's actual arm movement (reaching, resting, gesturing) before finalizing the sleeve pattern; this is the one measurement in the brief that cannot be validated from a flat sketch |
| Sleeve construction | Set-in, not raglan | Holds sleeve-hem shape and length more precisely under movement than a raglan seam |
| Neckline | Crew, square, or gentle V | Moderate depth — confirm neckline doesn't require additional structure/interfacing beyond standard finishing |
| Closure | Back zip or hidden side zip | Silver-tone pull/hardware, standard concealed zip installation |

## 3. Fabric Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fabric type | Crepe, scuba knit, or structured ponte | Confirm manufacturer's standard stock options against this list before custom sourcing |
| Stretch content | 6–10% elastane | Confirm recovery performance (post-wash, post-wear) meets a fitted-bodice standard, not just initial stretch — poor recovery is the single most likely failure mode for this garment |
| Weight | 180–220 gsm | Heavy enough to hold bodice structure without full boning alone; light enough for skirt drape |
| Lining (bodice) | Full lining, shell-matched or complementary | Required |
| Lining (skirt) | Partial or none | Dependent on final fabric opacity — confirm against actual fabric swatch, not assumed from weight/gsm alone |

## 4. Bodice Structure — Two Methods, Manufacturer's Choice

Either method is acceptable; the manufacturer should recommend whichever they can execute most reliably at this fit tolerance, but raw compression fabric alone (no boning, no liner) is explicitly out of spec.

1. **Boning channels:** 2–4 lightweight spiral steel or plastic bones at side seams and princess seams. Confirm boning channel construction (topstitched casing vs. separate boning tape) and bone length/flexibility against the fabric's own stretch behavior — steel boning in a high-stretch fabric can create pressure points if not sized correctly.
2. **Built-in shaping liner:** power-mesh or structured knit underlayer bonded to the bodice lining. Confirm bonding method (fused vs. sewn) holds through repeated wear and dry cleaning without the liner separating from the shell.

**Princess seams are required through the bodice regardless of structure method** — front and back — since this is the seaming that delivers the fitted silhouette and (if boning is used) houses the boning channels.

## 5. Starlink One Reference Match

This is the one manufacturing brief in the system with the lightest hardware-reference footprint by design (see `vision.md` Section 6) — the silhouette itself, not an applied detail, is the reference. The only physical requirement: any exposed hardware (zip pull, closure) is silver-tone, matching the wardrobe-wide metal rule. No debossed, engraved, or chain-link hardware detail is required or wanted anywhere on this garment.

## 7. Sizing & Fit Process

- **Fit-sample-first process strongly recommended** over grading directly from a flat pattern — the sleeve-length gating spec (Section 2) and the boning/liner fit tolerance (Section 4) both need live confirmation on a fit model before committing to a size run.
- **Size run:** confirm manufacturer's standard range and grading increments; no unusual sizing requirement beyond the standard "smaller frame" fit notes already established elsewhere in this system (see the Anchor Heel brief's last-grading note for the equivalent footwear-side principle — proportions don't scale linearly, and a fitted bodice is exactly the kind of piece where that matters most).

## 8. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Manufacturer Conversation

1. **Sleeve-length fit validation** — the single highest-priority open item. Requires a live fit sample and physical arm-movement test (reaching, resting, gesturing) before the sleeve pattern is finalized; this cannot be confirmed from spec or flat sketch.
2. **Fabric recovery performance** — confirm the manufacturer can source or test crepe/scuba/ponte stock at 6–10% elastane with proven recovery after repeated wear and cleaning, not just off-the-roll stretch.
3. **Boning vs. liner decision** — confirm which structure method the manufacturer executes more reliably at this fit tolerance, and get a fit-sample comparison of both before committing to production method.
4. **Starlink Emerald color match** — confirm dye-lot matching process against the ring itself (or the color system's hex reference at minimum) and whether this requires a custom dye order vs. manufacturer stock color.
5. **Bodice lining bond durability (if liner method chosen)** — confirm the liner-to-shell bond holds through the garment's expected cleaning method (hand wash or dry clean) without separating.
6. **Zip closure placement** — confirm back zip vs. hidden side zip based on which the fitted bodice's princess seaming makes easier to conceal cleanly; either is acceptable per Section 2, but the manufacturer's construction should drive the final call, not an arbitrary choice made before pattern development.

## 9. Requested Next Step

A single fit-sample garment in the Starlink Emerald colorway, standard/reference size only, before any multi-size grading or multi-colorway production commitment — the sleeve-length validation (Section 8, item 1) and the boning-vs-liner decision (item 3) both need to be resolved against a physical sample before this piece moves toward a real production plan. Per the sequencing note in `vision.md` Section 7, this remains the aspirational Phase 3 centerpiece rather than a near-term sourcing priority — the fit sample is worth commissioning to de-risk the design, not to move immediately into full production.
