# The Long Formal Dress — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for pattern development and tailoring
**Prepared for:** Formal-wear manufacturer or bridal/formal atelier with in-house or partnered hemming/alterations capability — this piece cannot be finished as a standard off-the-rack production run given the heel-dependent hemming requirement (Section 2)
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending confirmation of tailoring partnership and Emerald custom-match feasibility)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec. **Read `vision.md` Section 7 before proceeding with any part of this brief** — this garment has a hard sourcing gate that precedes any manufacturing conversation.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into pattern, fabric, and construction specs for an initial quoting and sampling conversation. It is not a finalized production spec — Section 7 lists what needs manufacturer/tailor confirmation before this piece moves toward any commitment. **This brief should not be acted on until the sourcing gate in `vision.md` Section 7 is satisfied** (a real calendar event exists, and the intended heel is already owned).

---

## 1. Product Overview

A floor-length column or soft-hip-flare formal dress in stretch crepe (optional chiffon overlay over a fully opaque lined base), with a defined waist, invisible zip, and hemming performed after final heel selection rather than to a standard length. Four colorways.

## 2. Pattern & Hemming Specification — Read First

**This is the one manufacturing brief in the system where hemming is explicitly not part of the initial production run.** The garment is patterned and constructed to an intentionally long, untailored length, then hemmed as a final, separate step once the customer's intended heel is confirmed and in-hand. Do not treat this as a standard "finish to floor length" instruction — floor length is heel-height-relative, not a fixed measurement, and finishing to a generic floor length before the heel is confirmed risks a hem that pools or, worse, trips on the actual event heel.

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Base construction length | Intentionally long/untailored, hemmed last | Confirm manufacturer or partnered tailor can perform final hemming as a distinct post-delivery step |
| Silhouette | Column or soft hip flare | Explicitly not ballgown volume — confirm skirt pattern doesn't drift wide in translation from sketch to pattern |
| Waist | Defined | Standard defined-waist construction, no unusual requirement |
| Walking ease | Slit or built-in ease if column is narrow | Confirm placement and depth of any slit against the final silhouette — a slit added as an afterthought can look inconsistent with a column silhouette's clean line |
| Closure | Invisible zip | Standard |

## 3. Fabric Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fabric type | Stretch crepe (preferred) | Optional chiffon overlay permitted only with full opaque lining underneath — see Section 4 |
| Weight | Light to mid formal weight | Must hold column silhouette line while remaining wearable 4+ hours |
| Lining | Full-length, soft | Required throughout — no unlined sections |

## 4. Opacity Requirement (If Chiffon Overlay Used)

Same non-negotiable opacity rule as [`../sheer-overlay-dress/manufacturing-brief.md`](../sheer-overlay-dress/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 3 — the base garment must be fully opaque at bust, seat, and torso under both direct sunlight and event-lighting conditions before any chiffon overlay is added as an accent layer. This piece is not treated as a second translucent-system statement dress; if a chiffon overlay is requested, apply the same opacity testing protocol used for the Sheer Overlay Dress rather than a lighter standard just because the overlay here is described as an "accent" rather than the whole garment's premise.

## 6. Starlink One Reference Match

Silver-tone hardware only, wherever it appears (zip pull, any closures). No debossed, engraved, or chain-link detail. See `vision.md` Section 6 for the styling-side note that, in the Emerald colorway specifically, the ring functions as the garment's own metal accent rather than needing a separate hardware echo — a styling framing, not an additional manufacturing requirement.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Manufacturer/Tailor Conversation

1. **Post-delivery hemming partnership** — confirm the manufacturer either has in-house tailoring capability or can recommend/coordinate with a trusted external tailor for the heel-dependent final hem, since this is not a standard part of most formal-wear production processes.
2. **Walking-ease placement** — confirm slit or ease construction is evaluated against an actual walking test once the base garment exists, not just against a static silhouette sketch.
3. **Emerald custom color match** — confirm feasibility and cost of a custom dye lot matched against the physical ring or the color system's hex reference; treat this as a likely custom-commission item rather than standard production.
4. **Chiffon overlay opacity testing (if used)** — confirm the same dual-lighting-condition opacity test used for the Sheer Overlay Dress is applied here, not a lighter standard.
5. **4+ hour wearability** — confirm fabric weight and lining combination has been evaluated for extended wear comfort, not just a brief fit-check try-on.

## 8. Requested Next Step

**Do not commission a sample or begin sourcing until the gate in `vision.md` Section 7 is satisfied** — a real calendar event exists, and the intended block heel pump (or Dress Anchor variation) is already owned, so the hem can eventually be tailored against a known heel height. Once that gate is met, the recommended first step is a single base-garment fit sample in Jet Black or Midnight Navy, intentionally unhemmed, to validate silhouette and fit before any hemming or Emerald custom-match conversation begins. Consistent with the original concept note's "source last" placement in the Soft Formal kit, this remains the most aspirational and least near-term piece in the entire dresses catalogue.
