## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Cocktail / Evening Dress.**

This piece occupies a genuinely awkward, and explicitly named, position in the catalogue: it can overlap in role with the Ring Statement Dress, and the original concept note says so directly rather than pretending the two pieces are cleanly distinct. Both are knee-or-above-knee, fitted, going-out-register dresses. The honest difference is intent, not silhouette: the Ring Statement Dress's entire brief runs backward from ring visibility (see its own vision doc's sleeve-length engineering); this piece's brief runs backward from cocktail-hour formality — a lined, structured, sit-through-dinner dress that doesn't have "showcase the ring" as its organizing principle, even though it can absolutely be worn with the ring and often will be. The system's own guidance, carried straight from the original concept note, is not to buy both pieces in the same week without a specific reason — a reasonable instruction given how much real overlap exists here, but one worth stating plainly rather than resolving by pretending the overlap isn't there.

Where this piece earns its own place rather than becoming a redundant near-copy of the Ring Statement Dress: Medium priority tier (lower urgency, not a piece to rush toward), a broader colorway range including winter-appropriate soft velvet, and a design brief centered on formal sit-through-dinner comfort rather than sleeve-length ring engineering. It's the more flexible, less singularly-purposed of the two — useful across a wider range of cocktail/evening occasions precisely because it isn't optimized around one specific showcase job.

## 3. Fabric & Feel

- **Fabric: stretch crepe, soft satin, or quality velvet** (velvet reserved for the Jet Black winter colorway specifically) — the velvet option is unique to this piece in the dresses catalogue, appropriate to its role as the one piece explicitly built for evening/winter formal occasions rather than daytime-inclusive formal wear.
- **Weight:** mid — lined and smooth is the priority over any specific gsm target, since lining quality matters more to this piece's comfort brief than raw fabric weight does.
- **Hand-feel:** lined, smooth — no unlined sections at bust or seat given the going-out, closely-observed occasion this piece is worn for.
- **Breathability:** low to moderate — an honest tradeoff named directly rather than glossed over; this is an evening-occasion piece, not an everyday one, so lower breathability is an acceptable cost for the structure and formality it delivers.
- **Break-in behavior:** none expected — fits correctly from first wear, same rule as every other formal/statement piece in this catalogue.

## 4. Fit & Comfort Priorities

- **Stretch content:** 5–10%, the highest stretch range of any dress in the catalogue, reflecting the "Spanx-free comfort stretch" instruction carried directly from the original concept note — the fabric itself should do the shaping work a shapewear layer would otherwise need to do.
- **Boning rule:** soft spiral boning only if boning is used at all, and only if it demonstrably doesn't dig after an hour of wear — this is a harder requirement than it sounds, since rigid boning is the industry-standard default for structured cocktail dresses, and this system is explicitly rejecting that default in favor of comfort even at the cost of somewhat less rigid shaping.
- **Sit test:** confirm the dress doesn't ride up excessively when seated through a dinner-length event — named explicitly in the original concept note's modification wishlist as a fit check to run before finalizing any pattern.

## 5. Colorways

- **Jet Black, Midnight Navy** — the core evening-neutral rotation.
- **Starlink Emerald** — available, functioning here (as on the Soft Formal midi) as a full-field stone echo when chosen; quiet accessories are the styling rule for this colorway (see Section 6).
- **Blush Mauve, Lavender Mist** — the softer, more romantic-evening colorway options, extending this piece's range beyond a purely grounding-neutral cocktail palette.

## 6. Starlink One Reference & Styling

- **Hand/wrist visibility:** sleeveless or 3/4 preferred — consistent with the system-wide wrist-visibility rule, though (per Section 1) not engineered around ring-showcase as its primary purpose the way the Ring Statement Dress is.
- **Hardware finish:** silver-tone.
- **Ring echo:** an Emerald cocktail dress functions as a full-field stone echo — style with quiet accessories in that colorway, same discipline applied to the Soft Formal midi's Emerald option.
- **Heart pendant pairing note:** the original concept note specifically calls out the heart-emerald pendant as an optional addition when the dress itself is in Jet Black or Midnight Navy — i.e., when the dress isn't already carrying the Emerald color statement, the pendant becomes available as the look's ring echo instead. This is a useful, explicit example of the system's "one Emerald lead per look" rule resolving cleanly rather than becoming a restriction.

## 7. Sourcing & Occasion Notes

- **Reference lines:** Reformation cocktail, Reformation's 90s slip-formal styles if lined appropriately, Aritzia evening, & Other Stories formal, Nordstrom Rack formal mid-range — a genuinely broad price/formality range compared to the other formal pieces, appropriate to this piece's Medium (lower-urgency) tier.
- **Shoe pairings:** block heel pump as the primary pairing; the Statement Mule as a secondary option if an open-back silhouette fits the specific dress code.
- **Occasion:** cocktail hour, evening weddings, dinner formal — narrower than the Soft Formal midi's daytime-inclusive range, by design.
- **Care:** per individual fabric label — no single care instruction applies cleanly across the crepe/satin/velvet fabric range this piece spans.
