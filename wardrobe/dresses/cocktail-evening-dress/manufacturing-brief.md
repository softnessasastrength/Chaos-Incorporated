**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for pattern development and factory quoting
**Prepared for:** Garment manufacturer producing lined formal cocktail dresses, including a velvet colorway option
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending manufacturer confirmation of soft-boning sourcing and velvet colorway feasibility)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into pattern, fabric, and construction specs for an initial quoting and sampling conversation. It is not a finalized production spec — Section 7 lists what needs manufacturer confirmation before pattern grading begins.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A fitted, fully-lined cocktail-length dress (33–37") with a defined waist, open-enough-for-jewelry neckline, optional soft spiral boning, and invisible back zip. Five colorways, one of which (Jet Black) is available in soft velvet as a winter-specific option.

## 2. Pattern & Construction Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Length | 33–37" (above-knee to knee) | Confirm on fit sample against 64" reference height |
| Bodice | Fitted, defined waist | Bust fully lined regardless of neckline depth |
| Neckline | Open enough for formal jewelry to be worn, without unlined risk | Confirm lining coverage extends fully behind any open neckline design |
| Boning | Soft spiral boning only, if used | **Do not substitute rigid/plastic boning as a "close enough" alternative** — confirm the manufacturer's standard boning stock and specify soft spiral explicitly, since rigid boning is the more common default in cocktail-dress production |
| Closure | Invisible back zip | Standard |

## 3. Fabric Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fabric type (primary) | Stretch crepe or soft satin | Applies to Midnight Navy, Starlink Emerald, Blush Mauve, Lavender Mist colorways |
| Fabric type (Jet Black option) | Soft velvet | Winter-specific colorway — confirm velvet stock has adequate stretch recovery (Section 4) since velvet behaves differently than crepe/satin under stretch |
| Stretch content | 5–10% elastane | Highest stretch range of any dress in this catalogue — confirm fabric delivers "Spanx-free" comfort stretch per the vision doc's brief, meaning the fabric itself does shaping work rather than requiring an external shapewear layer |
| Lining | Full lining throughout bust and seat | Non-negotiable per Section 2 |

## 4. Fit & Comfort Testing

- **Soft spiral boning sourcing:** confirm the manufacturer's standard boning supplier offers soft spiral boning (not just rigid steel or plastic) and that it holds shape through a full evening's wear without losing structure — this is a real sourcing question, since soft spiral boning is a less universal default than rigid boning in cocktail dress production.
- **Sit-test requirement:** confirm the fit sample is evaluated seated through a dinner-length event (60–90+ minutes) specifically checking for hem ride-up — named explicitly in the original concept note and carried forward here as a required fit-sample test, not an optional check.
- **Velvet stretch recovery (Jet Black option only):** confirm the specific velvet stock maintains the 5–10% stretch target and its recovery over repeated wear — velvet's pile construction can behave differently under stretch than a woven crepe or satin, and this needs its own confirmation separate from the other four colorways.

## 5. Colorway Spec Summary

| Colorway | Fabric | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Jet Black | Stretch crepe/satin **or** soft velvet (winter option) | Core evening neutral |
| Midnight Navy | Stretch crepe or soft satin | Core evening neutral |
| Starlink Emerald | Stretch crepe or soft satin | Full-field stone echo colorway — confirm dye match against `../../color-system.md`'s `#0F5C3D` reference |
| Blush Mauve | Stretch crepe or soft satin | Softer romantic-evening option |
| Lavender Mist | Stretch crepe or soft satin | Softer romantic-evening option |

**Recommended build order:** sample Jet Black in stretch crepe/satin first (broadest re-wearability, simplest fabric sourcing), then the Jet Black velvet variant as a distinct second sample given its different stretch-recovery profile, before expanding to the remaining four colorways.

## 6. Starlink One Reference Match

Silver-tone hardware only, wherever it appears (zip pull, any closures). No debossed, engraved, or chain-link detail required. See `vision.md` Section 6 for the styling-side note on how this piece pairs with the Heart-Emerald Pendant when worn in a non-Emerald colorway — a purchasing/styling note, not a manufacturing spec.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Manufacturer Conversation

1. **Soft spiral boning sourcing** — confirm availability and comfort performance against rigid boning as the manufacturer's likely default; this is the single most important open item, since substituting rigid boning would violate the piece's core comfort brief without necessarily being flagged as a deviation.
2. **Sit-test / hem ride-up evaluation** — confirm this is built into the standard fit-sample review process, not treated as optional.
3. **Velvet stretch-recovery testing** — confirm the Jet Black velvet variant is tested separately from the crepe/satin colorways given velvet's distinct behavior under stretch.
4. **Neckline-to-lining coverage** — confirm the open neckline design doesn't create an unlined gap at the bust when the garment moves (reaching, bending), not just in a static fit-check pose.
5. **Emerald dye match** — confirm against the color system reference across both the crepe/satin base (this piece) and note any difference from the Ring Statement Dress or Soft Formal Midi's own Emerald dye lots, since customers may own more than one Emerald formal piece despite the system's sequencing guidance to avoid it.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A single fit sample in Jet Black stretch crepe/satin, standard/reference size, evaluated through the sit-test protocol (Section 4) and confirmed on soft spiral boning sourcing, before any multi-colorway or velvet-variant production commitment. Given this piece's Medium (lower-urgency) priority tier, this sample is worth commissioning after — not ahead of — the Soft Formal Midi Dress's own first sample, consistent with the formal kit's stated build-order sequencing.
