# The Sheer Overlay Dress — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for pattern development and factory quoting
**Prepared for:** Garment manufacturer producing two-layer or integrated sheer-over-opaque dress construction
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending manufacturer confirmation of opacity testing method and layer-attachment approach)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into pattern, fabric, and construction specs for an initial quoting and sampling conversation. It is not a finalized production spec — Section 7 lists what needs manufacturer confirmation before pattern grading begins. **Section 3 (opacity testing) is the gating requirement for this entire brief** — no other section matters if the underlayer fails opacity testing.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A two-part dress: an opaque or solid underdress/slip (structural, coverage-bearing) plus a fully sheer chiffon or organza overlay (visual, non-structural). Available as true two-piece construction or as an integrated single garment with the sheer layer permanently attached to the opaque inner. Four colorway pairings share one pattern and construction method (Section 5).

## 2. Pattern & Construction Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Construction type | True two-piece **or** integrated single-garment | Confirm which the manufacturer executes more reliably — integrated construction reduces the risk of a customer wearing the overlay without its underlayer, which is a real consideration given the opacity rule in Section 3 |
| Underlayer length | Knee to midi | Confirm finished length on a fit sample |
| Overlay length | Same as underlayer, or 1–2" longer | The extra length is a deliberate float allowance, not a pattern-grading error — do not trim to match the underlayer exactly |
| Underlayer waist | Defined — smocked, seamed, or slip-with-clip | Structural waist definition lives entirely on this layer |
| Overlay waist | No independent structure required | Overlay is permitted to float |
| Closures | Underlayer: zip or pull-on. Overlay: open-front or shared closure | Confirm based on construction type chosen |

## 3. Opacity Testing — Gating Requirement

**This is the single most important section in this document.** The underlayer must pass opacity testing at the bust, seat, and crotch under two conditions before any production commitment:

1. **Direct sunlight test** — thin fabrics that read as opaque under standard indoor lighting can become translucent in direct sun; test the actual underlayer fabric and weight outdoors, not just on the cutting-room floor.
2. **Event/stage lighting test** — the inverse case: strong backlighting (common at evening events, photography) can reveal seams or thin spots not visible in flat retail lighting.

**Do not approve this garment for production based on indoor daylight inspection alone.** If the underlayer fabric fails either test at the specified weight (120–180 gsm), increase weight or add a discreet lining panel at the failing area before resampling — do not ship a version that passes only one of the two lighting conditions.

## 4. Fabric Specification

| Layer | Fabric | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Underlayer | Stretch crepe, satin, or ponte | 120–180 gsm | Must independently pass Section 3 opacity testing |
| Overlay | Chiffon or organza | 30–70 gsm | Confirm manufacturer's stock options against this weight range — too heavy and the overlay stops floating independently of the underlayer, undercutting the veil effect |

**Weight ratio note:** the underlayer-to-overlay weight ratio (roughly 2–4x) is a deliberate design target, not a loose guideline — confirm final fabric choices maintain this ratio so the two layers read as structurally distinct rather than as one thick combined garment.

## 5. Colorway Spec Summary

| Underlayer | Overlay | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Cloud Ivory or Blush Mauve | Cloud Ivory | Quietest, tonal/near-tonal |
| Sterling Grey or Ivory | Lavender Mist | Cool-toned romantic |
| Cloud Ivory | Starlink Emerald | Ring-forward vacation statement — confirm Emerald overlay color match against `../../color-system.md`'s `#0F5C3D` reference |
| Jet Black | Jet Black | Evening-only, tonal |

**Recommended build order:** sample the Cloud Ivory/Cloud Ivory pairing first — it's the lowest-risk combination for validating both the opacity testing (Section 3) and the two-layer construction method before committing to the more visually complex Emerald or Lavender Mist pairings.

## 6. Starlink One Reference Match

No debossed, engraved, or chain-link hardware detail required anywhere on this garment. Any exposed hardware (overlay closure, underlayer zip) stays silver-tone per the wardrobe-wide metal rule. No further reference-matching requirement beyond that — see `vision.md` Section 6 for the styling-side rule (Emerald overlay functions as a full-dress ring-color statement, which is a wear-time styling note, not a manufacturing spec).

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Manufacturer Conversation

1. **Opacity testing protocol (Section 3)** — confirm the manufacturer will test the underlayer under both lighting conditions before approving fabric/weight for production, not just standard QC inspection.
2. **Construction type decision (two-piece vs. integrated)** — confirm which method the manufacturer executes more reliably, and get a cost/complexity comparison of both before choosing.
3. **Overlay-to-underlayer attachment (if integrated construction chosen)** — confirm attachment method (shared waist seam, internal tacking points) allows the overlay to float per Section 2's length spec rather than pulling taut against the underlayer.
4. **Weight-ratio sourcing** — confirm manufacturer's stock fabric options can hit both the underlayer opacity target and the overlay's independent-float weight target simultaneously; these may require sourcing from two different mills rather than one shared supplier.
5. **Emerald color match (overlay)** — confirm dye-lot matching process for the sheer chiffon/organza against the color system's reference, noting that sheer fabrics can read differently in color than opaque fabrics at the same nominal dye formula.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A single fit-and-opacity sample in the Cloud Ivory/Cloud Ivory pairing, standard/reference size, tested under both lighting conditions specified in Section 3, before any multi-colorway or multi-size production commitment. Per the sequencing note in `vision.md` Section 1, this piece is built to be sourced last in the translucent kit — this sample is worth commissioning to validate the opacity map at full-garment scale, not to move toward near-term production ahead of the rest of the Soft Light kit proving out first.
