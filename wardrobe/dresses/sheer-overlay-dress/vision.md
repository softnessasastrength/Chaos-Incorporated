# The Sheer Overlay Dress — Design Vision

*The Soft Light kit's statement dress — Experimental tier (Phase 3 Statement-adjacent), and the piece that pushes the translucent system's opacity map to its most literal, full-garment scale. Governed by [`../../translucent-system.md`](../../translucent-system.md). See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the dresses category and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the doctrine underneath both.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Sheer Overlay Dress.**

Every Soft Light piece in this system runs on the same rule: sheer is a veil over engineered opacity, never a replacement for it. This dress is where that rule gets tested at the highest stakes, because a dress carries more surface area — and more of the body — than a sleeve or a skirt panel. The piece is explicitly two garments functioning as one: an opaque or solid **underdress** doing the actual coverage and structure work, and a sheer **overlay** doing the soft-veil visual work on top of it. Neither half is optional, and neither half is interchangeable with the other's job. A sheer dress sold and worn without its opaque underlayer is, by this system's own rule, simply out of system — no exception, no judgment call, because there is no version of "just wear it carefully" that holds up across a full day or evening of actual movement.

This is also, deliberately, the *last* piece built in the translucent kit rather than the first. The Soft Light doctrine's opacity map — what's allowed to be sheer, what must stay opaque, how the two halves relate — gets proven on lower-stakes pieces first (the sheer long-sleeve base, the organza blouse, the lace panel camisole) before it's asked to carry a full dress silhouette. Building this one last isn't caution for its own sake; it's the same "prove the core, then expand the bet" logic the priority system applies everywhere else, applied here to a design rule instead of a single silhouette.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Structure | Two-part: opaque/solid underdress (or slip) + sheer overlay | Integrated single-garment construction (sheer outer permanently attached to opaque inner) is acceptable as an alternative to a true two-piece set — see Section 4 |
| Underlayer length | Knee to midi (knee-to-mid-calf) | Not floor-length by default — a floor-length underlayer belongs to the Long Formal Dress's register, not this piece's |
| Overlay length | Same as underlayer, or 1–2" longer | The extra length lets the sheer layer float slightly past the underdress hem rather than terminating exactly at it — this is what reads as "veil" rather than "two identical dresses stacked" |
| Waist definition | Defined on the underlayer (smocked, seamed, or a slip with a waist clip) | The overlay is permitted to float without its own waist definition — the underlayer is doing the structural job for both layers |
| Closure | Underlayer: zip or pull-on. Overlay: open-front, or shared closure with the underlayer | Overlay closure method depends on final construction (true two-piece vs. integrated) |

## 3. Fabric & Layer Construction

- **Underlayer fabric:** stretch crepe, satin, or ponte, 120–180 gsm — a genuine dress-weight fabric doing real structural and coverage work, not a thin slip-dress placeholder. This layer needs to function as a complete, wearable dress entirely on its own; the sheer layer is an addition to it, not a component it depends on for coverage.
- **Overlay fabric:** chiffon or organza, 30–70 gsm — light enough to float and move independently of the underlayer's silhouette. The weight gap between the two layers (roughly 2–4x) is deliberate: it's what produces the visual effect of a soft veil moving over a fixed architecture, rather than two fabrics of similar weight reading as one thick garment.
- **Hand-feel:** soft against the skin on the underlayer (this is the layer in constant bodily contact all day), airy and barely-there on the overlay.
- **Breathability:** good across both layers — this is a warm-weather-leaning piece by role (see Section 6), so a stuffy or heat-trapping underlayer fabric works against the piece's actual occasions.

## 4. Opacity Map — The Non-Negotiable Core Rule

This is the section that matters most in this document, more than any single measurement above it:

- **Underlayer: fully opaque at bust, seat, and crotch.** Not "mostly opaque," not "opaque enough with the right lighting" — fully opaque, checked in direct sunlight and under event lighting both, since the two produce different results with thin fabrics.
- **Overlay: fully translucent.** No partial-lining trick on the overlay itself — the translucency is the entire point of that layer, and any attempt to make it "a little more modest" by lining it just recreates a second, worse-fitting underdress.
- **If sold or sourced as sheer-only, with no underlayer:** out of system, full stop, unless a specific matching underlayer is sourced as part of the same purchase. This is stated as a hard rule rather than a guideline because it's the one place in the entire wardrobe where a styling mistake has a real, non-cosmetic consequence — everywhere else in the system, "wrong choice" means a look that doesn't quite work; here it means an accidental wardrobe malfunction.

## 5. Colorways

- **Cloud Ivory sheer over Cloud Ivory or Blush Mauve under** — the quietest version, tonal or near-tonal layering.
- **Lavender Mist sheer over Sterling Grey or Ivory under** — a cooler-toned romantic pairing.
- **Starlink Emerald sheer over Cloud Ivory under** — the ring-forward vacation statement version; see Section 6 for the styling rule this colorway triggers.
- **Jet Black sheer over Jet Black under** — evening-only, the one colorway pairing that reads as formal rather than romantic-daytime.

## 6. Starlink One Reference & Styling Rules

- **Hand/wrist visibility:** overlay sleeve at 3/4 length or sleeveless underlayer — the wrist stays clear either way, consistent with every other statement piece in the system's ring-visibility rule.
- **Hardware finish:** silver-tone only, wherever hardware appears (closures, any trim).
- **Ring echo — the Emerald colorway is a full-dress stone filter.** When the overlay is Starlink Emerald, the entire dress is already functioning as a ring-color statement — the Emerald pendant and the Emerald jelly sandal should both sit out of that specific look. This is the same "one Emerald lead per look" logic already applied to the Soft Formal midi and cocktail dresses; stated here for the piece where the Emerald color covers the most surface area of any single item in the system.
- **Accessory minimalism:** minimal jewelry generally, layering bracelet plus the ring itself is the recommended ceiling; an organza duster is an acceptable outer layer only when the dress's own overlay isn't already Emerald (two sheer Emerald-adjacent layers stacked would over-state the same color note twice).

## 7. Sourcing & Occasion Notes

- **Reference lines:** LoveShackFancy overlay dresses, Doen sheer-over-solid pairings, Reformation two-layer midi, Free People slip-plus-sheer sets — all names already establish two-piece or integrated sheer-over-opaque construction as a normal, sourceable category rather than a custom-only ask.
- **Shoe pairings:** block heel pump, the Anchor Heel, the jelly line's low-block mule, or the Barely There sandal's dressy slide — not sneakers; this piece's whole silhouette logic assumes a slightly elevated occasion register.
- **Occasion:** elevated vacation, soft evening, the Soft Light kit's own statement occasion — not an everyday piece, and not competing with the Ring Statement Dress's going-out role even in the Emerald colorway, since the two pieces answer different design briefs (one showcases the ring directly; this one showcases the translucent system at full scale).
- **Care:** follow individual fiber care instructions; the overlay in particular is likely hang-dry or dry-clean-only given typical chiffon/organza care requirements — confirm per final fabric choice rather than assuming standard machine wash care across both layers.
