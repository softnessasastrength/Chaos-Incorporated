# The Kimono Duster — Design Vision

*The purest "soft" register in the outerwear category — High tier, and the piece that needs a fitted or structured layer underneath to stay on-brand rather than reading as shapeless. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the outerwear catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Kimono Duster.**

Most of the outerwear catalogue carries its own internal soft-hard tension — a belted trench, a self-tie cardigan, a structured shoulder over a soft body. This piece doesn't try to resolve that tension on its own at all; it's the one deliberately, entirely soft register in the category, and it depends completely on whatever's worn underneath (a fitted camisole, a corset top) to supply the structure the manifesto requires somewhere in the full look. That's a legitimate design choice, not an omission — an open, unstructured layer is naturally forgiving on a shorter frame precisely because it isn't trying to hold a shape of its own.

The one hard limit this forgiveness runs into: length. A full-length duster, sized for a taller frame, drags or pools at 64" — this piece crops hard from the standard duster's 42"+ length down to knee-max, non-negotiable. And the sleeve is the other real risk: a full kimono sleeve is the single highest hand-visibility risk piece in the entire outerwear category, closer to the chunky cardigan's cuff problem than to any other duster-adjacent piece, because a kimono sleeve by genre convention runs wide and long by default.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Length | 34–38" (knee to lower-calf) | Deliberately cropped from standard duster lengths (42"+) — non-negotiable at this height |
| Sleeve | 3/4-length or short enough to push up | The highest hand/wrist-visibility risk item in outerwear — a full kimono sleeve is genre-standard and directly wrong for this system |
| Closure | Open-front, or a single tie at the empire waist | No structure of its own by design |

## 3. Fabric & Feel

- **Fabric: rayon, viscose, or lightweight cotton voile** — floral print or solid, 80–110 gsm. This should feel like almost nothing, an airy layer rather than a heavy coat.
- **Hand-feel:** soft, fluid, almost weightless drape.
- **Break-in behavior:** none — drapes correctly from the first wear, since there's no structure to break in.

## 4. Colorways

## 5. Starlink One Reference & Styling

- **Hand/wrist visibility:** sleeve must be 3/4-length or shorter, or hemmed/rolled to clear the wrist — the single highest hand-visibility risk item in the entire outerwear category, named explicitly rather than treated as a routine spec.
- **Hardware finish:** none by design.
- **Ring echo:** indirect — the floaty, unstructured silhouette is this piece's entire aesthetic contribution. It's meant to be the purely "soft" half of an outfit that gets its structure from whatever's worn underneath, not from the duster itself.
- **Pairing:** over any `basics/` or `intricate/` top, especially the ribbon-tie camisole; layers over straight jean, wide-leg jean, or shorts. Needs a fitted or structured piece underneath (camisole, fitted tee, corset top) to stay on-brand per the manifesto rather than reading as shapeless.
- **Occasion:** warm-weather evening, romantic-casual, festival-adjacent.
- **Care:** hand wash or gentle cycle, hang dry.
