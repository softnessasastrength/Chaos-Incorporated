# The Kimono Duster — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for pattern development and factory quoting
**Prepared for:** Garment manufacturer producing lightweight woven dusters/kimono silhouettes

## 1. Product Overview

An open-front, unstructured duster in lightweight rayon/viscose/cotton voile, 34–38" length, with a 3/4-or-shorter kimono-style sleeve, no internal structure. Three colorways share one pattern.

## 2. Pattern & Construction Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Length | 34–38" (knee to lower-calf) | **Reject any sample at standard duster length (42"+)** — confirm the manufacturer's base pattern is cropped, not just hemmed shorter from a longer base, since a hem-only crop can leave the proportions (sleeve width, body volume) calibrated for the longer length |
| Sleeve | 3/4-length or short, or a wide sleeve engineered to push/roll and stay clear of the wrist | **Gating spec** — confirm against a live fit model in motion (reaching, arms at rest), since kimono sleeves by genre convention run wide and this is the highest hand-visibility risk item in the category |
| Closure | Open-front or single empire-waist tie | No boning, lining structure, or internal support — this piece has no structure of its own by design |

## 3. Fabric Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fabric | Rayon, viscose, or lightweight cotton voile | 80–110 gsm |
| Drape | Fluid, almost weightless | Confirm fabric doesn't read as stiff or "crackly" at this weight — a common failure mode with lower-quality voile |
| Print (if floral) | On-palette per `../../color-system.md`'s print exception | Confirm print colorway approval before production run |

## 4. Construction Detail

- **Crop verification:** confirm the pattern itself (not just the finishing hem) is proportioned for a knee-max length — body width, sleeve width, and overall drape should be recalculated for the shorter length, not simply trimmed from a standard-length duster pattern.
- **Sleeve engineering:** if a wide kimono sleeve is used rather than a narrower 3/4 sleeve, confirm it includes a functional push-up/roll mechanism (an internal tab, elastic, or button-tab) that reliably keeps it clear of the wrist during normal arm movement, not just when deliberately held up.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Manufacturer Conversation

1. **Sleeve-clears-wrist validation in motion** — the single highest-priority open item; confirm on a live fit model, not a static garment lay-flat.
2. **True cropped-pattern proportions** — confirm the shorter length isn't just a hemmed-down standard duster pattern.
3. **Fabric hand-feel at low gsm** — confirm the specific fabric lot doesn't read as stiff or crackly, since lightweight voile quality varies significantly between suppliers.
4. **Floral print palette approval** — confirm print colorway before committing to a production run.

## 8. Requested Next Step
