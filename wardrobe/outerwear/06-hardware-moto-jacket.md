# Hardware-Detailed Moto Jacket

**Category:** Outerwear
**Subcategory:** Statement — Hardware-Detailed Moto Jacket
**Priority:** Experimental (Phase 3 Statement)
**Status:** Concept

## Why It Works for Her Frame (63")
- A cropped moto silhouette (hits at or above the natural waist) keeps this piece from adding length to a shorter torso the way a longer motorcycle jacket would; a structured shoulder and fitted body read as intentional rather than borrowed from a taller frame's proportions.
- Proportion notes: sleeve hemmed to the wrist bone, the same mandatory spec as every other outerwear piece in the system; cropped body length, fitted rather than oversized through the shoulder.
- Comfort priority: soft, already-broken-in leather, or a structured ponte/scuba moto silhouette as a non-leather option — stiff leather against skin at the collar and cuff is a real comfort cost for a piece meant to be worn, not just photographed.

## Technical Specs
- Rise: N/A
- Inseam / length: ~19–20" (cropped)
- Waist range / sizing notes: Fitted through the body
- Stretch level: Minimal (leather), or 4–8% (ponte moto option)
- Fabric weight: Mid-heavy
- Closure type: Asymmetric zip front, chain-link trim detail at the collar or pocket, silver-tone hardware throughout

## Fabric & Feel
- Fabric composition: Soft lambskin or vegan leather, or a structured ponte/scuba knit moto silhouette
- Hand-feel / skin feel: Soft, pre-broken-in leather hand, or smooth structured knit for the non-leather option
- Breathability: Low (leather) to moderate (knit option)
- Break-in behavior: Leather softens further at the elbow/shoulder with wear; the knit option should fit correctly from the first wear

## Colorways
- Jet Black
- Midnight Navy (leather or ponte)

## Sourcing
- Brand / product line examples: AllSaints moto jacket (leather), a custom hardware commission for chain-link trim matched to Starlink One, or a vegan leather moto as a budget option
- Approx. price range: $150–$600 (leather/custom runs higher, vegan leather lower)
- Where to buy: AllSaints.com, or direct commission for the chain-link trim detail

## Styling & Pairings
- Pairs well with: any `basics/` or `intricate/` top, the ribbon-tie camisole for a soft/hard contrast, or over the ring-statement dress for a layered going-out look
- Occasion: going-out, evening — the system's most overtly hardware-forward piece
- Shoe pairings: heeled ankle boot, knee-high boot

## Starlink One Pairing
- Hand/wrist visibility: Sleeve hemmed to the wrist bone — mandatory.
- Hardware finish: Chain-link trim at the collar or pocket, commissioned or selected to match the ring's own chain-link scale as closely as possible — the same "match, don't compete" logic as the chain-link belt and layering bracelet, at outerwear scale.
- Ring echo: Direct, and the most hardware-literal execution of the ring's design language anywhere in the wardrobe — the outerwear equivalent of the layering bracelet.
