# The Cropped Denim Jacket — Design Vision

*The structured half of the "leaving the house" outerwear pair — Must-Have tier, and the direct outerwear equivalent of the straight-leg jean's role in bottoms. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the outerwear catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Cropped Denim Jacket.**

Every wardrobe needs a piece that does nothing more ambitious than work, every time, without a second thought — this is that piece for outerwear. Its entire design brief is "quiet structure," the exact register the manifesto assigns to items that need to recede so a romantic top or a statement piece elsewhere in the look can lead. A standard-length denim jacket already does this job on a taller frame; at 64" it fails immediately, running to mid-hip and visually shortening the leg line underneath instead of framing it. The crop to natural waist isn't a style preference layered on top of a classic silhouette — it's the one change that makes an otherwise-correct piece actually correct for this specific body.

The wash-and-wear philosophy here directly mirrors the ring's own unprotected finish: a pre-broken-in soft wash rather than a stiff rigid rinse, because this piece is worn constantly, and day-one board-like stiffness at the shoulder is a real, repeated comfort cost rather than a one-time break-in inconvenience.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Body length | ~19–20", cropped to the natural waist | Standard-length denim jackets run to mid-hip on this frame — explicitly rejected |
| Sleeve length | Ends exactly at the wrist bone | **The single non-negotiable spec on this piece** — worn this often, a long sleeve silently hides the ring on most days regardless of the rest of the outfit |
| Fit | Boxy-relaxed through the body, fitted at the shoulder seam | Do not size up for a looser body fit — size up on sleeve length only if needed, and hem separately |
| Closure | Button-front, silver-tone buttons | Chest pockets with silver rivets |

## 3. Fabric & Feel

- **Fabric: 98% cotton / 2% elastane, 10–11oz mid-weight denim.** Enough stretch for arm mobility without becoming a stretch-forward piece — this is structure with just enough give, not a soft knit pretending to be denim.
- **Wash:** pre-broken-in soft wash with visible texture, not a stiff dark rigid rinse. Buying a wash that already has character is the deliberate choice over buying raw rigid denim and hoping it breaks in fast enough to matter for a piece worn constantly.
- **Break-in behavior:** softens further with wear, same patina-forward logic as the rest of the system's leather goods, applied here to denim instead.

## 4. Construction & Hardware

- **Silver-tone buttons and rivets throughout** — specified explicitly because most retail denim jackets default to antique brass hardware, and this is the one detail most likely to get silently substituted if not called out directly to a manufacturer or buyer.
- **Sleeve hem as a tailoring point, not a size-chart assumption** — crop and hem the sleeve to petite proportions rather than buying standard length and rolling the cuff. A rolled cuff adds bulk exactly at the wrist, which works against the one spec this piece can't compromise on.

## 5. Colorways

- **Classic mid-blue indigo** — ties directly into the jeans system's workhorse wash, keeping this jacket from ever wash-clashing with whatever jean is worn underneath.
- **Black denim** — the second colorway, pairing with the black jean/legging rotation.

## 6. Starlink One Reference & Styling

- **Hand/wrist visibility:** the highest-stakes spec in this document — see Section 2.
- **Hardware finish:** silver-tone buttons and rivets, no exceptions.
- **Ring echo:** indirect. The wash/patina philosophy mirrors the ring's own unprotected finish rather than any literal motif — this sits in the "quiet structure" register alongside the straight-leg jean, not a hardware-forward piece like the moto jacket.
- **Pairing:** any basics tee underneath, leggings or jeans below — denim-on-denim needs tonal contrast (dark jacket with mid-wash jean, or the reverse). This is the structured counterpart every intricate/romantic top needs somewhere in rotation per the manifesto.
- **Care:** cold wash inside-out, hang dry, wash sparingly to preserve the wash character.
