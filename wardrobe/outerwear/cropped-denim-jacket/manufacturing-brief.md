# The Cropped Denim Jacket — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for pattern development and factory quoting
**Prepared for:** Denim/casual-wear cut-and-sew manufacturer
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending manufacturer confirmation of hardware sourcing and wash treatment)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A cropped (~19–20" body length) denim jacket, boxy-relaxed through the body, fitted at the shoulder, sleeve hemmed to the wrist bone, button-front closure with silver-tone hardware. Two colorways share one pattern and construction method.

## 2. Pattern & Construction Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Body length | 19–20", cropped at natural waist | Confirm finished length on a fit sample against a 64" reference height |
| Sleeve length | Ends at wrist bone | **Gating spec** — confirm against a live fit model, arms both hanging and in motion, not a flat pattern measurement |
| Body fit | Boxy-relaxed, fitted shoulder seam | Confirm shoulder seam placement doesn't drift wide — this is what keeps the boxy body from reading as oversized |
| Closure | Button-front | Silver-tone buttons, chest pockets with silver rivets |

## 3. Fabric Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fabric | 98% cotton / 2% elastane denim | 10–11oz mid-weight |
| Wash | Pre-broken-in soft wash | Confirm manufacturer's wash program can deliver visible texture/softness at the shoulder and collar specifically, not just an overall soft-hand finish — a jacket that's soft in the body but stiff at the collar fails the comfort brief |
| Stretch | Enough for arm mobility, not stretch-forward | Confirm recovery holds through repeated wear |

## 4. Hardware Specification

- **Buttons and rivets: silver-tone exclusively.** Confirm explicitly with the manufacturer — antique brass is the more common default finish for denim hardware, and this needs to be called out as a deviation from that default, not assumed.
- **Rivet placement:** chest pockets, standard denim-jacket rivet reinforcement points.

## 5. Colorway Spec Summary

| Colorway | Notes |
|---|---|
| Classic mid-blue indigo | Confirm wash matches the jeans system's workhorse wash reference so the jacket doesn't clash with the denim worn underneath it |
| Black denim | Standard black wash |

## 6. Starlink One Reference Match

No debossed, engraved, or chain-link detail required. The single hardware requirement is silver-tone buttons/rivets throughout — see Section 4.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Manufacturer Conversation

1. **Sleeve-length fit validation** — confirm against a live fit model with arms both hanging and in motion before finalizing the sleeve pattern.
2. **Wash softness at collar/shoulder specifically** — confirm the wash program softens these high-skin-contact points, not just the body panels.
3. **Silver-tone hardware sourcing** — confirm the manufacturer's standard hardware supplier stocks silver-tone denim buttons/rivets, since antique brass is the more common default.
4. **Indigo wash match to jeans system** — confirm dye/wash lot consistency against the jeans system's reference wash.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A single fit sample in the mid-blue indigo colorway, standard/reference size, before any multi-colorway production commitment — confirm sleeve length and collar softness on this sample before expanding to the black denim colorway.
