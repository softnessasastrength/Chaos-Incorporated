# Chunky Knit Cardigan

**Category:** Outerwear
**Subcategory:** Soft — Chunky Cardigan
**Priority:** Must-Have
**Status:** Concept

## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- Oversized/chunky knits swallow a 64" frame fast if left unchecked — the fix is a defined hip-length hem (not thigh-length) plus an optional self-tie belt that cinches the waist back in on demand, giving the piece the manifesto's required "exit strategy" from pure volume.
- Proportion notes: sleeve length must still end at or before the wrist. Chunky knits run long in the sleeve by default and need a turn-back cuff or petite-specific sizing — this is the single highest-risk item in the system for accidentally covering the ring, since knit bulk visually swallows the hand even at a technically "correct" length.
- Comfort priority: this is a pure comfort piece — soft, non-itchy yarn is non-negotiable at the collar and cuff specifically, since those are the two highest skin-contact points on an open cardigan.

## Technical Specs
- Rise: N/A
- Inseam / length: Hip-length, ~26–28"
- Waist range / sizing notes: Oversized/relaxed fit with an optional self-tie belt to cinch; the belt is what keeps this piece from reading as shapeless rather than intentionally soft
- Stretch level: Minimal — knit give only (~10–15%), no elastane
- Fabric weight: Heavy-gauge chunky knit
- Closure type: Open-front or single self-tie belt closure — no buttons, since the denim jacket already carries the hardware role in this outerwear pairing

## Fabric & Feel
- Fabric composition: Merino-acrylic or cotton-blend chunky knit — merino blend over 100% wool specifically, since raw wool at this gauge is likely to feel scratchy at the neck and wrist for a soft/stretchy preference
- Hand-feel / skin feel: Soft-brushed, not scratchy — verify collar and cuff texture specifically before buying, since gauge and softness don't always correlate
- Breathability: Moderate — a cool-weather layering piece, not built for insulation alone
- Break-in behavior: Minimal; should feel soft from the first wear — if it doesn't, it won't improve enough to matter

## Colorways
- Sterling Grey (the workhorse neutral)
- Cloud Ivory
- Sage (closest on-palette secondary green — a soft tonal echo of Starlink Emerald without repeating it directly)

All three are quiet/neutral-adjacent by design, so this piece can layer over any `intricate/` top without competing with a print or color statement underneath.

## Styling & Pairings
- Pairs well with: basics tees/tanks, leggings, denim — layers over any `intricate/` top for the soft-on-soft version of the manifesto's pairing rule (needs a structured jacket or a fitted bottom elsewhere in the same outfit to stay balanced)
- Occasion: everyday, cool-weather, low-effort comfort layer
- Shoe pairings: sneakers, flats, boots

## Starlink One Pairing
- Hand/wrist visibility: Sleeve needs a turn-back cuff or a length hemmed short enough to clear the wrist — flagged above as the top comfort/fit risk on this piece, and it's equally the top ring-visibility risk for the same reason.
- Hardware finish: None by design. If a tie-belt is used, keep it a simple fabric or thread tie rather than a buckle; if a buckle version is ever sourced, silver-tone only.
- Ring echo: Indirect — the Sage colorway option is the closest on-palette secondary green, giving a soft tonal echo to Starlink Emerald without repeating it directly.

## Chaos Incorporated Notes
- Aesthetic tie-in: the soft half of the outerwear pairing rule — needs the structured denim jacket (or a fitted bottom) elsewhere in the outfit to stay on-brand rather than reading as pure loungewear.
- Modifications wanted: turn-back cuff tailoring is close to mandatory given the wrist-visibility risk noted above, not an optional nice-to-have.
- Care instructions: hand wash or gentle cycle, lay flat to dry — chunky knits lose shape if hung wet.
