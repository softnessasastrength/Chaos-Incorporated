# The Chunky Knit Cardigan — Design Vision

*The soft half of the "leaving the house" outerwear pair — Must-Have tier, and a pure comfort piece that needs a structured jacket or fitted bottom elsewhere in the outfit to stay on-brand rather than reading as loungewear. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the outerwear catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Chunky Knit Cardigan.**

Where the cropped denim jacket carries the structured half of the manifesto's soft-top/hard-jacket pairing rule, this piece is the soft half — and that means its whole design challenge runs in the opposite direction from the denim jacket's. An oversized chunky knit swallows a 64" frame fast if left unchecked; the fix isn't fitting the knit (that would defeat the point of a chunky, cozy piece) but giving it an exit strategy from pure volume: a defined hip-length hem, not thigh-length, plus an optional self-tie belt that cinches the waist back in on demand. That belt is doing real structural work, not decorative work — it's the single detail standing between "intentionally soft" and "shapeless."

This is also, quietly, the single highest-risk item in the entire system for accidentally covering the ring. Chunky knit bulk visually swallows the hand even at a technically correct sleeve length in a way that a thinner fabric like the denim jacket's never does — a turn-back cuff isn't optional tailoring here the way it might be treated as a nice-to-have elsewhere; it's close to mandatory given how much is at stake.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Length | Hip-length, ~26–28" | Defined hem, not thigh-length — the volume's exit strategy |
| Fit | Oversized/relaxed with an optional self-tie belt | The belt is what keeps this piece from reading as shapeless |
| Sleeve length | Ends at or before the wrist, via turn-back cuff or petite-specific sizing | The highest ring-visibility risk item in the system — chunky knits run long by default and bulk visually swallows the hand even at a "correct" length |
| Closure | Open-front or single self-tie belt | No buttons — the denim jacket already carries the hardware role in this pairing |

## 3. Fabric & Feel

- **Fabric: merino-acrylic or cotton-blend chunky knit** — merino blend over 100% wool specifically, since raw wool at this gauge is likely to feel scratchy at the neck and wrist for a soft/stretchy preference.
- **Hand-feel:** soft-brushed, not scratchy — verify collar and cuff texture specifically before buying, since gauge and softness don't always correlate at this weight of knit.
- **Break-in behavior:** minimal; should feel soft from the first wear. If it doesn't, it won't improve enough with wear to matter — this is one of the few pieces in the system where "it'll soften with time" isn't an acceptable answer to an uncomfortable first impression.

## 4. Construction & Detail

- **Turn-back cuff tailoring:** close to mandatory, not an optional nice-to-have, given the wrist-visibility risk named in Section 1. This is the single highest-priority modification on this piece.
- **Belt construction:** if a tie-belt is used, keep it a simple fabric or thread tie rather than a buckle. If a buckle version is ever sourced instead, silver-tone only.

## 5. Colorways

- **Sterling Grey** — the workhorse neutral.
- **Cloud Ivory.**
- **Sage** — the closest on-palette secondary green, a soft tonal echo of Starlink Emerald without repeating it directly.

All three are quiet/neutral-adjacent by design, so this piece layers over any `intricate/` top without competing with a print or color statement underneath.

## 6. Starlink One Reference & Styling

- **Hand/wrist visibility:** the top comfort risk and the top ring-visibility risk on this piece, for the same underlying reason — see Section 1 and Section 4.
- **Hardware finish:** none by design; silver-tone only if a buckle is ever used.
- **Ring echo:** indirect — the Sage colorway is the closest on-palette secondary green, a soft tonal echo rather than a repeat of Starlink Emerald.
- **Pairing:** basics tees/tanks, leggings, denim — layers over any `intricate/` top for the soft-on-soft version of the manifesto's pairing rule, but needs a structured jacket or fitted bottom elsewhere in the same outfit to stay balanced.
- **Care:** hand wash or gentle cycle, lay flat to dry — chunky knits lose shape if hung wet.
