# The Chunky Knit Cardigan — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for pattern development and factory quoting
**Prepared for:** Knitwear manufacturer producing heavy-gauge chunky knits
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending manufacturer confirmation of yarn softness and cuff construction)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A hip-length (~26–28"), oversized-relaxed chunky knit cardigan with an optional self-tie belt, open-front closure, and a turn-back or petite-graded cuff to keep the sleeve clear of the wrist. Three colorways share one pattern and yarn.

## 2. Pattern & Construction Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Length | Hip-length, ~26–28" | Confirm finished length on a fit sample |
| Fit | Oversized/relaxed | With optional self-tie belt |
| Sleeve/cuff | Turn-back cuff or petite-graded sleeve length | **Gating spec** — confirm against a live fit model that the cuff genuinely clears the wrist at the knit's actual gauge/bulk, not just at the nominal sleeve length; chunky knit bulk visually shortens effective sleeve clearance compared to a woven fabric at the same measurement |
| Closure | Open-front or self-tie belt | No buttons |

## 3. Fabric Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Yarn | Merino-acrylic or cotton-blend chunky knit | Merino blend preferred over 100% wool — confirm supplier can hit a soft-brushed hand at this heavy gauge, not just a lighter-gauge softness spec |
| Gauge | Heavy | Confirm collar and cuff texture specifically, not just overall body swatch — gauge and softness don't reliably correlate |
| Softness testing | Should read as soft from first touch, no break-in expected | If a yarn sample doesn't pass a first-touch softness check, reject rather than assume wear will improve it |

## 4. Construction Detail

- **Cuff construction:** confirm the manufacturer's standard turn-back cuff method holds its folded shape through repeated wear (a turn-back cuff that unrolls on its own defeats the purpose). If petite-graded sleeve length is used instead, confirm grading is genuinely re-proportioned for a shorter arm, not linearly scaled from a standard-length pattern.
- **Belt construction:** simple fabric or thread tie, matched to shell yarn or a coordinating solid — confirm tie length allows a functional cinch at the waist, not just a decorative drape.

## 6. Starlink One Reference Match

No hardware required by default. If a belt buckle is used instead of a fabric tie, silver-tone only — flag this explicitly if buckle sourcing is considered at any point.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Manufacturer Conversation

1. **Cuff-clears-wrist validation at actual knit bulk** — the single highest-priority open item; confirm on a live fit model with the actual yarn gauge, not a thinner mockup fabric.
2. **Collar/cuff softness at heavy gauge** — confirm supplier can deliver soft-brushed hand-feel at these specific high-skin-contact points.
3. **Cuff-fold retention** — confirm the turn-back cuff holds its shape through repeated wear and washing.
4. **Belt tie length and attachment** — confirm functional cinch, not purely decorative drape.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A single fit sample in Sterling Grey, standard/reference size, with explicit sign-off on cuff-clears-wrist testing before any multi-colorway production commitment — this is the one item in the outerwear catalogue where a manufacturing shortcut on the cuff spec directly creates the system's single highest ring-visibility risk.
