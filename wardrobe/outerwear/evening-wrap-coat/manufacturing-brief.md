# The Evening Wrap / Dressy Coat — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for pattern development and factory quoting
**Prepared for:** Formal outerwear manufacturer producing lightweight dressy wraps/coats
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending manufacturer confirmation of fabric weight against the indoor-carry comfort test)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

## 1. Product Overview

A hip-to-mid-thigh length (28–36") formal wrap or dressy coat in crepe, soft wool-cashmere blend, or satin-back crepe, with a soft shawl collar or clean wrap silhouette, wrap tie or single silver clasp closure. Five colorways.

## 2. Pattern & Construction Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Length | 28–36" (hip to mid-thigh) | Confirm on fit sample — reject long opera-coat proportions |
| Structure | Soft shawl collar or clean wrap | Confirm no hardware-forward detailing (studs, heavy zips, chain trim) gets added by a manufacturer defaulting toward a more "outerwear statement" construction |
| Sleeve | 3/4 or pushable to clear the wrist | Confirm against live fit model |
| Closure | Wrap tie, single silver-tone clasp, or open | |

## 3. Fabric Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fabric | Crepe, soft wool-cashmere blend, or dressy satin-back | Light to mid formal weight |
| Weight test | Must be comfortable to carry draped over an arm indoors for an extended period | **Named comfort requirement, not a cosmetic preference** — confirm final fabric choice against this practical test, not just a formal-weight gsm target on paper |
| Hand-feel | Soft at neck and cuff | Confirm collar fabric specifically doesn't require an undergarment buffer |

## 4. Hardware Specification

Silver-tone clasp only, if a clasp closure is used. **Explicitly exclude chain-heavy or biker-style trim** — confirm this with the manufacturer directly, since a coat/wrap manufacturer with moto-jacket-adjacent production lines may default toward harder hardware unless told otherwise.

## 6. Starlink One Reference Match

Silver-tone clasp only, if present. No chain-link or debossed detail — this is the deliberate inverse of the hardware moto jacket's hardware-forward approach (see `vision.md` Section 1).

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Manufacturer Conversation

1. **Indoor-carry weight test** — the single most important open item; confirm the manufacturer evaluates the finished garment against actual arm-carry comfort, not just a formal-weight fabric spec on paper.
2. **No hardware-forward drift** — confirm explicitly that chain trim or heavy hardware isn't defaulted into the design by a manufacturer more accustomed to statement-outerwear production.
3. **Sleeve-clears-wrist validation** — confirm against a live fit model.
4. **Emerald dye match** — confirm against the color system reference if that colorway is pursued.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A single fit sample in Midnight Navy or Jet Black, standard/reference size, evaluated specifically against the indoor-carry weight test, before any multi-colorway production commitment.
