# The Evening Wrap / Dressy Coat — Design Vision

*Soft Formal's "hard" outer half without the Chaos moto language — Medium tier, the formal arrival shell. Doctrine: [`../../formal-system.md`](../../formal-system.md). Distinct from [`../wool-wrap-coat/`](../wool-wrap-coat/) (utility cold) and [`../hardware-moto-jacket/`](../hardware-moto-jacket/) (Chaos night). See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the outerwear catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the doctrine underneath it.*

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Evening Wrap / Dressy Coat.**

Every formal outfit needs an arrival-and-departure layer, and this piece exists specifically so the hardware moto jacket doesn't have to do double duty as both the system's Chaos-night statement piece and its formal-event outer layer — those are two different registers, and forcing one piece to cover both would blur a distinction this system otherwise draws carefully everywhere else. This is Soft Formal's own "hard" half: soft structure (a shawl collar, a clean wrap) rather than biker hardware, open front or a single silver clasp rather than an asymmetric zip and chain-link trim.

Length follows the same rule as every coat in this catalogue: long opera coats drag and shorten a 64" frame unless carefully hemmed, so this piece stays hip to mid-thigh — long enough to function as a real arrival layer, short enough not to fight the formal dress or suit worn underneath. And critically, this is explicitly not a winter parka pretending to be evening-wear — light enough to carry over the arm indoors, which a genuine cold-weather coat generally isn't.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Length | 28–36" (hip to mid-thigh) | Long opera-coat lengths are explicitly rejected |
| Closure | Wrap tie, single silver-tone clasp, or open | |
| Structure | Soft — shawl collar, clean wrap | Not biker hardware; this piece is deliberately not the moto jacket's language |

## 3. Fabric & Feel

- **Fabric: crepe, soft wool-cashmere blend, or dressy satin-back** — light to mid formal weight.
- **Hand-feel:** soft at the neck and cuff.
- **Break-in behavior:** none — fits and drapes correctly from the first wear.
- **Weight test:** light enough for indoor carry on the arm — a real, practical test this piece needs to pass, since an evening coat that's too heavy to comfortably drape over an arm at a dinner table has failed its actual use case.

## 4. Colorways

- **Jet Black, Midnight Navy, Sterling Grey** — the core rotation.
- **Cloud Ivory** — summer evening option.
- **Starlink Emerald** — only if the dress worn underneath is quiet, keeping to the system's one-Emerald-field-per-look rule.

## 5. Starlink One Reference & Styling

- **Hand/wrist visibility:** sleeve 3/4 or pushable to clear the wrist.
- **Hardware finish:** silver-tone clasp only.
- **Ring echo:** none required.
- **Pairing:** the Soft Formal midi, the Cocktail/Evening Dress, the Ring Statement Dress, the Soft Formal Suit — this piece is the shared arrival layer across every formal piece in the system, not tied to one specific dress or suit.
- **Occasion:** arrival and departure for Soft Formal events specifically — not a piece meant to be worn seated through the event itself.
- **Rejected detail:** chain-heavy biker trims — keep this piece ceremony-quiet, the opposite instinct from the hardware moto jacket.
- **Cold-climate note:** the wool wrap coat may replace this piece for genuine outdoor transit in cold weather; this piece is built for indoor-to-car mild-weather use, not a substitute for real cold-weather protection.
- **Care:** dry clean or steam.
