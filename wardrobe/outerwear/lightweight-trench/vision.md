# The Lightweight Trench — Design Vision

*The most literal garment-scale execution of the ring's own design philosophy in the whole outerwear category — Medium tier. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the outerwear catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the doctrine underneath it.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Lightweight Trench.**

A trench coat already carries the manifesto's "soft shape, hard architecture" logic built into its own genre history — structured shoulder, defined waist belt, a loose body given shape by a cinch rather than by fitted seaming. That's exactly the ring's own construction, restated at coat scale: a soft heart shape held secure by visible engineering. The belt here isn't a styling detail layered onto a classic silhouette; it's the functional mechanism doing the same job Starlink One's prongs do, just in fabric and hardware instead of metal and stone.

The frame-specific problem: trenches are designed, as a genre, for taller frames, and a standard full-length trench hits well past the knee at 64" — the fix is a hard shorten to knee-max, not mid-calf, paired with the same wrist-clearing sleeve hem every other structured piece in this catalogue requires. The fabric choice matters just as much as the length: a lightweight cotton-blend gabardine rather than the heavier wool-blend trench fabric more common in the genre, so this stays a genuine three-season piece rather than reading as overly warm outside of true cold weather (which is the wool wrap coat's job instead).

## 3. Fabric & Feel

- **Fabric: cotton-poly-elastane gabardine**, 200–260 gsm — lightweight compared to a traditional wool-blend trench, deliberately, so this stays wearable across three seasons rather than reading as heavy or overly formal.
- **Hand-feel:** smooth, structured but not stiff, slightly cool to the touch.
- **Break-in behavior:** minimal — the structured shoulder should hold its shape from the first wear, same rule as every other structured piece in the system.

## 5. Starlink One Reference & Styling

- **Hand/wrist visibility:** sleeve hemmed to the wrist bone — the same mandatory spec as the cropped denim jacket.
- **Hardware finish:** buttons and belt buckle, silver-tone throughout.
- **Ring echo:** indirect but the most structurally literal in the entire outerwear category — the belted waist (loose coat body, cinched by a defined belt) is this piece's own version of the ring's heart held by engineered prongs. Worth naming explicitly, since it's easy to file this piece as "just a trench" without recognizing how directly its own construction logic mirrors the ring's.
- **Pairing:** over any tops category — belts naturally over the ribbon-tie camisole or a basics tee for a structured-over-soft look, or over an `intricate/` top for a defined-waist romantic-structured combination.
- **Occasion:** transitional weather, elevated-casual to dressier daytime.
- **Care:** dry clean or gentle machine wash depending on the exact blend — check the label.
