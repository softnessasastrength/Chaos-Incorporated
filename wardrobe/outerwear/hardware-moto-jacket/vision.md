## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Hardware-Detailed Moto Jacket.**

This is the system's most overtly hardware-forward outerwear piece, and it's meant to be — where the cropped denim jacket and lightweight trench carry the ring's design logic indirectly (patina, belted structure), this piece carries it directly, the same way the chain-link belt and the layering bracelet do at accessory scale. Soft, fitted body; hard, heavy hardware; structured shoulder. That's the manifesto's central pairing rule, executed as loudly as this system ever gets in outerwear.

A cropped moto silhouette (hitting at or above the natural waist) is what keeps this from reading as borrowed from a taller frame's proportions — a longer motorcycle jacket adds length in exactly the wrong place on a 64" frame. The fitted body and structured shoulder need to read as intentional, not oversized-for-effect the way moto jackets sometimes trend.

## 3. Material Palette — Two Options

- **Soft lambskin or vegan leather (recommended, flagship)** — pre-broken-in leather hand, not stiff-off-the-rack. Stiff leather at the collar and cuff is a real comfort cost for a piece meant to be worn, not just photographed — the same "wear it, don't varnish it" logic the Anchor Heel's aniline leather runs on.
- **Structured ponte/scuba knit moto silhouette (non-leather option)** — 4–8% stretch, smooth structured knit, fits correctly from the first wear rather than needing a leather break-in period.

## 4. Construction & Hardware Detail

- **Chain-link trim:** commissioned or selected to match the ring's own chain-link scale as closely as possible — the same "match, don't compete" logic already applied to the chain-link belt and layering bracelet, restated at outerwear scale. An off-scale chain-link won't read as intentional; this detail is worth commissioning custom rather than sourcing off-the-rack.
- **Hardware:** silver-tone throughout, no exceptions — asymmetric zip pull, any snaps or studs.
