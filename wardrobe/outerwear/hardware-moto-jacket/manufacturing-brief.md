## 1. Product Overview

A cropped (~19–20"), fitted moto jacket with a structured shoulder, asymmetric zip front, and custom chain-link trim matched to Starlink One's scale. Available in soft leather or a structured ponte/scuba knit. Two colorways share one pattern per material option.

## 3. Material Specification — Two Options

| Option | Material | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (flagship) | Soft lambskin or vegan leather, pre-broken-in hand | Confirm tannery/supplier can deliver a genuinely soft hand at collar and cuff, not stiff off-the-rack leather — this is a named comfort requirement, not a cosmetic preference |
| 2 (non-leather) | Structured ponte/scuba knit, 4–8% stretch | Confirm structured knit holds a defined shoulder line without the rigidity leather naturally provides |

## 4. Chain-Link Trim Specification — Custom Commission

- **This is the single most important sourcing item in this brief.** The chain-link trim must be commissioned or carefully selected to match Starlink One's own chain-link scale — an off-scale chain-link reads as generic hardware rather than an intentional design echo, per `vision.md` Section 4.
- **Recommend engaging a jeweler or specialty hardware fabricator directly** rather than sourcing from the garment manufacturer's standard trim catalog, since most off-the-rack moto jacket chain trims are sized for a generic aesthetic rather than matched to a specific reference piece.
- **Placement:** collar or pocket — confirm attachment method (sewn tab, riveted, or removable) with whichever fabricator produces the final trim.

## 6. Starlink One Reference Match

The chain-link trim (Section 4) is the primary and most literal reference match in this brief — confirm scale against the physical ring or a precise measurement reference, not a generic "chain-link style" description. All other hardware (zip pull, any snaps) stays silver-tone.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Manufacturer/Fabricator Conversation

1. **Chain-link scale match** — the highest-priority open item; confirm the commissioned trim's link size and finish against Starlink One directly, ideally with a physical side-by-side comparison rather than a written spec alone.
2. **Sleeve-length fit validation** — confirm against a live fit model, consistent with every other outerwear piece.
3. **Leather softness sourcing (Option 1)** — confirm supplier can deliver pre-broken-in hand-feel at the collar and cuff specifically.
4. **Shoulder structure across both material options** — confirm the structured-shoulder silhouette holds consistently whether the final material choice is leather or ponte.
5. **Chain-link attachment durability** — confirm the trim's attachment method (sewn, riveted, removable) holds up to regular wear without loosening or tearing the shell fabric/leather.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A single fit sample in Jet Black, leather option, standard/reference size, with the chain-link trim sourced as a separate, dedicated commission conversation run in parallel — do not wait on the trim's full production timeline to validate the base garment's fit and sleeve length.
