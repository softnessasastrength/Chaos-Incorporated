# The Soft Formal Suit — Design Vision

*Hard architecture for Soft Formal — Medium tier, the non-dress path through the formal system. Doctrine: [`../../formal-system.md`](../../formal-system.md). Distinct from the everyday [`../tailored-blazer/`](../tailored-blazer/) and any wide-leg casual trouser. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the outerwear catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the doctrine underneath it.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Soft Formal Suit.**

Filed in outerwear rather than split across categories, because this is meant to be sourced and worn as one system piece — a matching blazer plus a trouser or midi skirt, not a mix-and-match separates exercise. This is the wardrobe's answer to formal occasions where a dress isn't the right lifestyle choice: black-tie-optional events, formal work-evenings, non-dress wedding-guest situations. The original concept note names the real tradeoff directly — pick the suit or the Cocktail/Evening Dress first, as a lifestyle choice, not both on day one, since they answer the same occasion need in two different silhouette languages.

The frame math here is the same cropped-blazer logic as the everyday tailored blazer, just at formal-fabric weight: a long boyfriend suit jacket cuts a 64" frame in half, so the blazer stays cropped or standard-short (hip bone max), paired with a high-rise trouser or midi skirt to keep the vertical line intact. A matching fabric set is what reads as genuinely formal here — mismatched separates in the same silhouette wouldn't deliver the same occasion-appropriate signal.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Blazer length | 22–26" (high hip to hip) | Same crop logic as the everyday tailored blazer |
| Bottom | High-rise trouser (straight or soft wide-leg) with a heel-height assumption of 2–2.5", or a midi pencil / soft A-line skirt in matching fabric | Trouser needs a proportion exit strategy the same way any wide-leg piece does elsewhere in the system |
| Stretch | 2–5% elastane throughout | |
| Closure | Single button, silver-tone (blazer); zip or hook, silver-tone (trouser) | |

## 3. Fabric & Feel

- **Fabric: stretch wool blend, crepe suiting, or a formal ponte set** — soft enough for a silk camisole worn underneath, no itchy wool at the neck.
- **Lining:** lined or half-lined jacket — needs to hold up through a full evening sitting for dinner.
- **Break-in behavior:** minimal — should fit correctly from first wear, same as every other formal piece in the system.

## 4. Colorways

- **Jet Black, Midnight Navy, Sterling Grey** — the core formal-neutral rotation.
- **Cloud Ivory** — day formal / summer option.

## 5. Starlink One Reference & Styling

- **Hand/wrist visibility:** blazer sleeve to the wrist bone, pushable.
- **Hardware finish:** silver buttons only — no gold crest buttons, the same explicit rejection named in the everyday blazer's own brief.
- **Ring echo:** none required — a quiet suit lets the ring lead.
- **Pairing:** the ribbon-tie or lace panel camisole (lined), a fitted turtleneck for winter, the block pump, the evening clutch, the evening wrap.
- **Occasion:** black-tie optional, formal work-evening, non-dress wedding guest.
- **Sequencing note:** pick the suit or the Cocktail/Evening Dress first — a lifestyle choice, not both on day one.
- **Care:** dry clean or steam; hang the suit together as a set.
