# The Lightweight Tailored Blazer — Design Vision

*The missing "hard architecture" layer for Soft Light and soft day dresses — High tier, a named backlog gap filled deliberately. Soft Heat stack: [`../../warm-season-system.md`](../../warm-season-system.md). See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the outerwear catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the doctrine underneath it.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Lightweight Tailored Blazer.**

This piece exists to answer a specific gap the outerwear category otherwise leaves open: dressier structure without the hardware moto jacket's Chaos-night heat or the trench's full coat-length formality. A sheer organza blouse or a soft day dress both need a hard-architecture layer somewhere in the outfit per the manifesto's pairing rule, and neither the moto jacket (wrong occasion register) nor the trench (too much coat) is the right answer most of the time. This is that answer — a cropped or standard-short blazer, hip-bone max, that adds tailored structure without adding length or heat.

The comfort brief here runs directly against the genre's own worst instinct: a fully canvassed wool blazer, built the traditional tailoring way, fails both the warm-season requirement and the comfort rule this system runs on everywhere. Unlined or half-lined summer-weight construction is the deliberate choice instead — real tailored shoulder structure without the stiffness a fully canvassed jacket would add.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Body length | 22–26" (high hip to hip bone) | Cropped or standard-short — a long boyfriend blazer cuts a 5'4" frame in half |
| Shoulder | Slight structure, soft pad or natural | Not linebacker width — single-breasted, not oversized boxy |
| Sleeve | Hemmed to the wrist bone or slightly above | Mandatory, same rule as the denim jacket |
| Closure | Single button or open; two-button max | |

## 3. Fabric & Feel

- **Fabric: tropical wool blend, linen-blend suiting with structure, or soft ponte blazer cloth** — 180–260 gsm, light-to-mid weight.
- **Lining: unlined or half-lined** — a fully canvassed construction is explicitly rejected for this role; it fails both the warm-season brief and the comfort rule.
- **Hand-feel:** soft enough for bare arms under a sheer sleeve or camisole; no scratchy horsehair against the neck if unlined.
- **Break-in behavior:** minimal — should drape correctly from day one.

## 4. Colorways

- **Cloud Ivory, Sterling Grey, Midnight Navy** — the core rotation.
- **Jet Black** — elevated/evening-adjacent.
- **Blush Mauve** — one Experimental colorway only, a Soft Light romantic option.

## 5. Starlink One Reference & Styling

- **Hand/wrist visibility:** sleeve hemmed to the wrist bone or slightly above — mandatory, same rule as the cropped denim jacket.
- **Hardware finish:** silver-tone buttons/crest hardware only — no gold buttons.
- **Ring echo:** none required — the structure recedes so the ring and other jewelry lead. This is a quiet-structure piece, not a motif piece.
- **Pairing:** the soft day dress, the organza blouse, a ribbed tank plus trouser, the straight jean, the translucent midi with a solid top.
- **Occasion:** elevated daytime, dinner-casual, work-adjacent, "put together" travel days.
- **Care:** steam preferred; dry clean or gentle wash per fabric.
