# The Lightweight Tailored Blazer — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for pattern development and factory quoting
**Prepared for:** Tailored outerwear manufacturer with unlined/half-lined summer suiting capability
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending manufacturer confirmation of unlined shoulder structure method)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A cropped-to-standard-short (22–26") single-breasted blazer, slight soft shoulder structure, unlined or half-lined, sleeve hemmed to the wrist bone. Five colorways share one pattern.

## 2. Pattern & Construction Specification

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Body length | 22–26" (high hip to hip bone) | Confirm on fit sample — reject long boyfriend-blazer proportions |
| Shoulder | Slight structure, soft pad or natural | Confirm shoulder pad (if used) is genuinely soft, not a standard-weight tailoring pad that reads as too structured for this piece's brief |
| Sleeve | Hemmed to wrist bone or slightly above | **Gating spec** — confirm against a live fit model, same protocol as the cropped denim jacket |
| Closure | Single button or open, two-button max | |

## 3. Fabric & Construction — Unlined/Half-Lined Requirement

| Parameter | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fabric | Tropical wool blend, linen-blend suiting, or soft ponte blazer cloth | 180–260 gsm |
| Stretch | 2–5% elastane preferred | Confirm shoulder structure holds with this stretch content |
| Lining | Unlined or half-lined only | **Explicitly reject a fully canvassed construction** — confirm the manufacturer's standard tailoring process for this fabric weight doesn't default to full canvas/lining, which is the more traditional (and heavier) approach most blazer manufacturers default to |
| Interior finish (if unlined) | No scratchy horsehair or raw canvas against the neck | Confirm interior seam and collar finishing specifically, since this is the point most likely to feel unfinished on an unlined jacket |

## 4. Hardware Specification

Buttons: silver-tone or crest hardware only — confirm explicitly that gold buttons are not the manufacturer's default, since gold is a common blazer-hardware default across the industry.

## 5. Colorway Spec Summary

| Colorway | Role |
|---|---|
| Cloud Ivory | Core |
| Sterling Grey | Core |
| Midnight Navy | Core |
| Jet Black | Elevated/evening-adjacent |
| Blush Mauve | Experimental, one colorway only — Soft Light romantic |

**Recommended build order:** sample Sterling Grey or Midnight Navy first (broadest re-wearability), confirming the unlined shoulder-structure method before expanding to the full five-colorway run.

## 6. Starlink One Reference Match

Silver-tone or crest-style buttons only. No debossed or chain-link detail required — this piece is designed to recede so the ring leads (see `vision.md` Section 5).

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Manufacturer Conversation

1. **Unlined/half-lined shoulder structure** — the single highest-priority open item; confirm the manufacturer can deliver genuine tailored shoulder shape without full canvas construction, since this is a real tailoring-technique question, not just a fabric choice.
2. **Sleeve-length fit validation** — confirm against a live fit model.
3. **Interior finishing on unlined option** — confirm no scratchy or unfinished interior seams at the neck/collar.
4. **Gold-button default risk** — confirm silver-tone/crest hardware explicitly, since gold is the more common industry default.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A single fit sample in Sterling Grey or Midnight Navy, standard/reference size, in the unlined or half-lined construction, before any multi-colorway production commitment — the shoulder-structure-without-canvas question needs to be resolved on a physical sample before this piece's full colorway range is worth pursuing.
