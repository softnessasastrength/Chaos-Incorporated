# The Wool Wrap / Winter Coat — Design Vision

*Hard architecture for Soft Cold — High tier, the true cold-weather shell. Soft Cold doctrine: [`../../cold-season-system.md`](../../cold-season-system.md). Distinct from the trench (mild/rain-light) and the denim jacket (year-round light structure). See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the outerwear catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the doctrine underneath it.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Wool Wrap / Winter Coat.**

Where the lightweight trench handles mild, transitional weather, this piece is the system's answer to genuine cold — and it needs to actually deliver warmth without becoming the "walking blanket" failure mode oversized winter coats fall into on a shorter frame. Mid-thigh to knee length is the balance point: long enough to warm the hip and upper leg, short enough not to pool or shorten the silhouette. A defined or belted waist (wrap coat, soft belt, or princess seam) gives the volume its exit strategy, same rule as everywhere else in the system, applied here at the highest-warmth-requirement piece in the whole wardrobe.

The real-warmth-without-armor-stiffness brief matters more here than almost anywhere else in outerwear, because cold-weather coats have the strongest genre pull toward stiff, heavy canvas construction — precisely the failure mode this system rejects everywhere. Soft lining at the collar and cuff, and a weight she can wear all day rather than only for a photoshoot exit, are both named directly as requirements rather than left implicit.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Length | 34–38" (mid-thigh to knee on 64") | Long enough to warm the hip/upper leg, short enough not to pool |
| Waist | Wrap or single-button with soft belt | Room for a turtleneck plus thin sweater underneath |
| Sleeve | Slightly cropped or easy-to-push cuff | So Starlink One can show when desired — a softer version of the mandatory wrist-clearing rule elsewhere, since a heavy winter coat sleeve genuinely needs to accommodate layered sweaters underneath |
| Closure | Wrap tie, toggle, or single/double button | Silver-tone hardware only |

## 3. Fabric & Feel

- **Fabric: wool or wool-blend shell**, ~400–600 gsm depending on climate; soft viscose/cupro or satin lining; optional light recycled insulation if climate demands it, keeping the silhouette slim rather than puffy.
- **Hand-feel:** soft at the collar; lining cool-smooth; no scratchy wool at the neck without a scarf as a buffer.
- **Break-in behavior:** wool softens slightly and hangs better after a few wears — a gentler version of the system's patina logic, applied to structured wool rather than leather or denim.

## 4. Colorways

- **Jet Black, Midnight Navy, Sterling Grey** — the core rotation.
- **Camel is explicitly off-palette (warm)** — do not source unless recolored to a cool greige inside the Sterling Grey family.
- **Cloud Ivory** — only if the fabric is practical for winter dirt, which usually rules it out as a first buy.

## 5. Starlink One Reference & Styling

- **Hand/wrist visibility:** sleeve must push above the wrist or stop at the wrist bone when arms hang — mandatory.
- **Hardware finish:** silver/gunmetal buttons, buckles, toggles only.
- **Ring echo:** none required — the coat recedes so the ring and silver jewelry lead once cuffs are clear.
- **Pairing:** turtleneck, straight/skinny jean, the soft day dress, ponte trouser, fleece legging, scarf.
- **Occasion:** cold everyday, travel, elevated cold-weather days.
- **Care:** wool brush plus steam; dry clean sparingly; store with cedar.
