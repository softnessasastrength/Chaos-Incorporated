# Wool Wrap / Winter Coat

**Category:** Outerwear  
**Subcategory:** Structured — Cold-Weather Coat  
**Priority:** High  
**Status:** Concept  

*True cold shell. Soft Cold: [`../cold-season-system.md`](../cold-season-system.md). Distinct from trench (mild/rain-light) and denim jacket (year-round light structure).*

## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- **Mid-thigh to knee length** — long enough to warm the hip and upper leg, short enough not to pool or shorten the silhouette into a walking blanket.
- Proportion notes: defined or belted waist preferred (wrap coat, soft belt, or princess seam) so volume has an exit strategy; slightly cropped sleeve or easy-to-push cuff so Starlink One can show when desired.
- Comfort priority: real warmth without armor-stiff canvas against the neck; soft lining at collar and cuff; weight she can wear all day, not only for a photoshoot exit.

## Technical Specs
- Rise: N/A  
- Inseam / length: 34–38" (mid-thigh to knee on 64")  
- Waist range / sizing notes: Wrap or single-button with soft belt; room for turtleneck + thin sweater underneath  
- Stretch level: 0–2% (woven wool)  
- Fabric weight: Mid–heavy wool blend, ~400–600 gsm shell range depending on climate  
- Closure type: Wrap tie, toggle, or single/double button — **silver-tone hardware only**  

## Fabric & Feel
- Fabric composition: Wool or wool-blend shell; soft viscose/cupro or satin lining; optional light recycled insulation if climate demands (keep silhouette slim)  
- Hand-feel / skin feel: Soft at collar; lining cool-smooth; no scratchy wool at neck without a scarf  
- Breathability: Moderate  
- Break-in behavior: Wool softens slightly; hangs better after a few wears  

## Colorways
- Jet Black  
- Midnight Navy  
- Sterling Grey  
- Camel is **off-palette** (warm) — do not source unless recolored to a cool greige inside Sterling Grey family  
- Cloud Ivory only if fabric is practical for winter dirt (usually not the first buy)  

## Sourcing
- Brand / product line examples: Aritzia Wilfred cocoon/wrap coat (check length), Everlane Italian wool coat, Uniqlo +J or blocktech wool alternatives, Sézane wool coat, Madewell belted coat  
- Approx. price range: $150–$450  
- Where to buy: brand sites, department stores, end-of-season sales for wool  

## Styling & Pairings
- Pairs well with: turtleneck, straight/skinny jean, soft day dress, ponte trouser, fleece legging, scarf  
- Occasion: cold everyday, travel, elevated cold day  
- Shoe pairings: rain/snow boot, Hug Boot, knee-high boot, Anchor Heel (dry cold)  

## Starlink One Pairing
- Hand/wrist visibility: sleeve must push above wrist or stop at wrist bone when arms hang — mandatory  
- Hardware finish: silver / gunmetal buttons, buckles, toggles only  
- Ring echo: none required; coat recedes so ring and silver jewelry lead when cuffs are clear  

## Chaos Incorporated Notes
- Aesthetic tie-in: hard architecture for Soft Cold — soft knits underneath, deliberate outer structure.  
- Modifications wanted: petite or short length; reject floor-length default; belt optional but waist definition preferred.  
- Care instructions: wool brush + steam; dry clean sparingly; store with cedar.
