# Kimono Duster

*Now has a full vision doc + manufacturing brief — see [`kimono-duster/`](kimono-duster/).*

**Category:** Outerwear
**Subcategory:** Romantic — Kimono Duster
**Priority:** High
**Status:** Concept

## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- An open, unstructured layer is naturally forgiving on a shorter frame as long as it avoids floor length — a full-length duster on a 5'4" frame will drag or pool, so hem should hit mid-calf at the longest, ideally closer to knee length.
- Proportion notes: sleeve should be 3/4-length or short enough to push up — a full kimono sleeve is one of the highest-risk pieces in the whole system for burying the hand and wrist entirely.
- Comfort priority: lightweight, flowing fabric — this should feel like almost nothing, an airy layer, not a heavy coat.

## Technical Specs
- Rise: N/A
- Inseam / length: 34–38" (knee to lower-calf — deliberately cropped from standard duster lengths, which run 42"+)
- Waist range / sizing notes: Open-front, no closure, or a single tie at the empire waist
- Stretch level: None — woven fabric, drapes by weight rather than stretch
- Fabric weight: Very light, 80–110 gsm
- Closure type: Open-front, or a single ribbon tie at the empire waist

## Fabric & Feel
- Fabric composition: Rayon, viscose, or lightweight cotton voile — floral print or solid
- Hand-feel / skin feel: Soft, fluid, almost weightless drape
- Breathability: Excellent
- Break-in behavior: None — drapes correctly from the first wear

## Colorways
- Cloud Ivory ground with floral print (on-palette print colors, per the print exception in `../color-system.md`)
- Sage solid
- Lavender Mist solid

## Sourcing
- Brand / product line examples: Free People kimono duster, LoveShackFancy duster, Spell & The Gypsy Collective kimono
- Approx. price range: $90–$200
- Where to buy: FreePeople.com, LoveShackFancy.com, SpellHollywood.com

## Styling & Pairings
- Pairs well with: over any `basics/` or `intricate/` top, especially the ribbon-tie camisole; layers over straight jean, wide-leg jean, or shorts
- Occasion: warm-weather evening, romantic-casual, festival-adjacent
- Shoe pairings: sandals, flats

## Starlink One Pairing
- Hand/wrist visibility: Sleeve **must** be 3/4-length or shorter, or hemmed/rolled to clear the wrist — full kimono sleeves are the single highest hand-visibility risk item in the entire outerwear category.
- Hardware finish: None by design.
- Ring echo: Indirect — the floaty, unstructured silhouette is this piece's entire aesthetic contribution; it's meant to be the purely "soft" half of an outfit that gets its structure from whatever's worn underneath.

## Chaos Incorporated Notes
- Aesthetic tie-in: the purest "soft" register in outerwear — needs a fitted or structured piece underneath (camisole, fitted tee, corset top) to stay on-brand per the manifesto rather than reading as shapeless.
- Modifications wanted: crop from standard duster length to knee-max — non-negotiable at this height.
- Care instructions: hand wash or gentle cycle, hang dry.
