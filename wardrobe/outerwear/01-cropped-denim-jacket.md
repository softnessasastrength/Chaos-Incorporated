# Cropped Denim Jacket

**Category:** Outerwear
**Subcategory:** Structured — Denim Jacket
**Priority:** Must-Have
**Status:** Concept

## Why It Works for Her Frame (63")
- Cropped length (hits at or just below the natural waist) avoids the boxy, swallowing effect a standard-length jacket creates on a shorter torso — a standard denim jacket runs to mid-hip on a 5'3" frame, which visually shortens the leg line underneath instead of framing it.
- Proportion notes: sleeve must end at the wrist bone, not past it. Standard sleeve lengths run long on this frame and will bunch or cover the hand — this is the one spec on this piece that isn't negotiable, since it's worn constantly and directly determines ring visibility on most days it's worn.
- Comfort priority: structured shoulder, but a soft, already-broken-in wash through the body so it doesn't sit stiff and board-like across the shoulders.

## Technical Specs
- Rise: N/A
- Inseam / length: Body length ~19–20" (cropped, hits at or just above the natural waist)
- Waist range / sizing notes: Boxy-relaxed through the body, fitted at the shoulder seam — do not size up for a looser body fit, size up on sleeve length only if needed and hem separately
- Stretch level: 1–2% elastane (98/2 cotton-elastane) — enough for arm mobility, not a stretch-forward piece
- Fabric weight: 10–11oz mid-weight denim
- Closure type: Button-front, silver-tone buttons, chest pockets with silver rivets

## Fabric & Feel
- Fabric composition: 98% cotton / 2% elastane
- Hand-feel / skin feel: Pre-broken-in soft wash, not a stiff dark rigid rinse — this is worn over other layers constantly, so day-one stiffness at the shoulder/collar is a real comfort cost
- Breathability: Good — layering piece, not meant for insulation
- Break-in behavior: Softens further with wear; buy a wash that already has some texture rather than raw rigid denim expecting it to break in fast enough to matter

## Colorways
- Classic mid-blue indigo (ties directly into the jeans system's workhorse wash — narrow denim-blue range across the system means this jacket never wash-clashes with whatever jean is worn under it)
- Black denim (second colorway, pairs with the black jean/legging rotation)

## Sourcing
- Brand / product line examples: Levi's "Cropped Trucker," Madewell "The Jean Jacket" (cropped), AGOLDE cropped denim jacket
- Approx. price range: $80–$160
- Where to buy: Levis.com, Madewell.com, AGOLDE.com

## Styling & Pairings
- Pairs well with: any basics tee underneath, leggings or jeans below — denim-on-denim needs a tonal contrast (dark jacket with mid-wash jean or the reverse), and it's the structured counterpart every intricate/romantic top needs somewhere in rotation per the manifesto
- Occasion: everyday — the structured half of the "leaving the house" outerwear pair
- Shoe pairings: sneakers, ankle boots

## Starlink One Pairing
- Hand/wrist visibility: Sleeve must be hemmed to end exactly at the wrist bone. This is the highest-stakes spec on this piece — worn this often, a sleeve that runs long silently hides the ring on most days regardless of what else is in the outfit.
- Hardware finish: Silver-tone buttons and rivets throughout — spec this explicitly, since most retail denim jackets default to antique brass hardware.
- Ring echo: Indirect — the wash/patina philosophy mirrors the ring's own unprotected, wear-in finish. This sits in the "quiet structure" register alongside the straight-leg jean, not a literal motif piece.

## Chaos Incorporated Notes
- Aesthetic tie-in: the structured half of the manifesto's soft-top/hard-jacket pairing rule — the direct outerwear equivalent of the straight-leg jean's role in bottoms.
- Modifications wanted: crop and hem the sleeve to petite proportions; do not buy standard length and roll the cuff — a rolled cuff adds bulk right at the wrist where a clean line matters most for the ring.
- Care instructions: cold wash inside-out, hang dry, wash sparingly to preserve the wash character.
