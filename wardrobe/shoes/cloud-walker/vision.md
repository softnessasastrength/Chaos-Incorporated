# The Cloud Walker — Design Vision

*The everyday sneaker — Must-Have tier, the highest-mileage shoe in the entire rotation. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the full shoe catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing design doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Cloud Walker.**

This piece exists to resolve a real tension, not paper over it: the catalogue brief calls for a "chunky dad-sneaker" silhouette, but a full dad-sneaker stack (often 2"+ ) works directly against the same leg-lengthening math every other shoe in this system is built around — excess sole height shortens a 64" frame's visual leg line exactly the way excess bottoms volume does. The Cloud Walker takes the *silhouette cues* of a chunky sneaker — a rounded, substantial midsole profile, visibly layered sole construction — without taking the full stack height that silhouette normally demands. It reads as "elegant chunky," a considered version of the trend rather than a rejection of it or an uncritical adoption of it. Where the Anchor Heel grounds the wearer through width, the Cloud Walker grounds her through cushioning — this is the shoe designed to disappear underfoot across the longest, most repetitive days, the sneaker equivalent of the fitted crew tee: a neutral base that lets everything else in the outfit lead.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Total sole stack height | 1.25–1.5" (32–38mm) | Elevated from a true low-profile sneaker (~1"/25mm) but well under true dad-sneaker trend proportions (2"+/50mm+) — the resolved middle point named above |
| Silhouette | Low-top | A high-top would visually cut the ankle and shorten the leg on a shorter frame — the "chunky" read lives entirely in the sole profile, not ankle height |
| Upper weight | 280–310gsm-equivalent | Target a noticeably soft, buttery hand rather than a stiff technical-sneaker feel |
| Closure | Lace-up | Standard, five-to-six-eyelet minimum for a secure midfoot lockdown given the elevated sole height |
| Toe shape | Rounded, moderate volume | Matches the "substantial but not bulky" brief carried through the whole silhouette |
| Waterproofing (select colorways) | Gore-Tex lining option | Practical upgrade, not a standard spec on every pair — see Section 4 |

All figures are target specs pending a physical sample — see `manufacturing-brief.md` Section 2 for the buildable tolerance table; the sole-height figure in particular is proportion-critical and should be confirmed against a real sample before finalizing, per the original concept note.

## 3. Material Palette — Two Options

### 1st — Soft Leather (flagship)
- **Weight/hand:** targets the 280–310gsm-equivalent softness spec above — noticeably softer than a typical leather sneaker upper, closer to the hand-feel of the Perfect Flat's nappa than a stiff technical-sneaker leather.
- **Why it fits:** the leather option carries the most direct "ages like the ring" story of the two options — a soft leather sneaker upper creases and softens into a personal, worn-in shape over real use, consistent with the system-wide "wear it in, don't varnish it" rule.

### 2nd — Premium Canvas
- **Construction:** heavyweight canvas, comfortable from first wear (no break-in period, unlike the leather option).
- **Why it's ranked second, not excluded:** doesn't develop the same visible character over time a leather upper does, but is the more breathable, lower-maintenance option and pairs better with the Gore-Tex waterproofing option in Section 4 (canvas-plus-membrane is a more established sneaker-manufacturing combination than leather-plus-membrane).

## 4. Construction Details

- **Construction method: cupsole construction.** A molded EVA or PU midsole/outsole unit that the upper is cemented into, rather than a minimalist cement-only construction (like a plain court sneaker) or a vulcanized rubber build. Cupsole construction is the standard, correct method for achieving a visually substantial, rounded midsole profile at a controlled, lighter-than-it-looks weight — it's the direct mechanical reason the "elegant chunky" resolution in Section 1 is achievable at all.
- **Visible layered sole detail:** a cosmetic tooling line separating the midsole into two visually distinct layers (rather than one uniform block) — this is what reads as "chunky-inspired" to the eye even though the actual stack height stays capped per Section 2. A pure single-block midsole at this same height would read flatter and less "elevated" than the layered version, despite identical stack height.
- **Insole:** removable memory foam insole, swappable for orthotics and replaceable as it compresses with heavy daily wear — the same "serviceable, not disposable" logic as the Anchor Heel's replaceable top-lift (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 4), applied here to the component that takes the most repetitive daily load in the whole catalogue.
- **Gore-Tex bootie construction:** offered as an option on select colorways — a waterproof membrane bonded between the upper and the interior lining, not a surface water-resistant spray treatment. This is a genuine practical upgrade for wet-weather everyday wear, not a standard spec on every pair, since it adds cost and slightly reduces breathability.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Hidden, With One Overt Option

- **Interior stitching:** hidden Starlink Emerald (`#0F5C3D`) stitching on the interior lining — invisible while worn, visible only when the shoe is off or being put on, matching the quiet-reference rule the Perfect Flat and Anchor Heel both follow.
- **Exterior accent colorway option:** Starlink Emerald available as accent detailing — sole, eyelets, or heel tab — not a full-emerald upper. This is a deliberately small, optional overt touch rather than a statement-tier treatment; the Cloud Walker's identity is a neutral base, and a full-emerald upper would work against that the same way it would on the Yoga/Recovery Shoe.

## 6. Colorway Notes

Cloud Ivory, Sage, and Sterling Grey as the core neutral rotation; Starlink Emerald as the accent-only option described in Section 5. No colorway introduces a hue outside [`../../color-system.md`](../../color-system.md).

## 7. Sizing & Last Notes

- **Fit varies by upper material more than most shoes in the catalogue:** leather styles run true to size, canvas styles sometimes run large — flag both explicitly to the wearer rather than assuming one sizing rule covers both upper options.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** same rule as every other shoe in this catalogue (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 7) — independent grading for US 5–7.
- **Collar padding:** padded collar required to prevent heel rub, called out explicitly rather than left to a generic "cushioned collar" assumption, since heel rub is the most common sneaker-specific fit complaint.
