# The Horsebit Loafer — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for factory quoting and prototyping
**Prepared for:** Footwear manufacturer, Shenzhen
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending factory confirmation of custom chain-link hardware tooling and heel-cup fit)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into dimensions, materials, and construction methods for an initial quoting and prototyping conversation. Section 7 lists what needs factory engineering confirmation before tooling begins. The custom chain-link horsebit hardware (Section 5) is the single most distinctive — and most tooling-intensive — spec in this brief.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A women's structured slip-on loafer, 0.5–0.75" mini stacked heel, moc-toe hand-sewn apron construction, aniline nappa or smooth calf upper, and a custom chain-link horsebit hardware detail across the vamp in place of a traditional oval snaffle.

## 2. Loafer Specification

| Parameter | Target | Tolerance |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 13–19mm (0.5–0.75") | ±0.5mm |
| Toe shape | Rounded, moderate volume | Confirm last profile against reference — a classic penny-loafer proportion, not the Perfect Flat's softer almond shape |
| Apron seam | Hand-sewn or hand-sewn-look moc-toe construction | Confirm factory's standard capability for this stitch detail — not all cement-construction loafer lines include a true hand-sewn apron as standard |
| Horsebit hardware footprint | Confirm exact dimensions against reference — a horsebit sits in a well-established position/size range on a vamp saddle; the chain-link redesign needs to occupy that same expected footprint | ±1mm on overall hardware width |

## 3. Construction Method

- **Construction: Blake stitch or cement construction**, chosen for the same slim, flexible-sole reasoning as the Anchor Heel (see [`../anchor-heel/manufacturing-brief.md`](../anchor-heel/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 3) — confirm the factory runs this construction as standard for a structured loafer silhouette specifically, since some loafer lines default to a stiffer cemented-only build.
- **Mini stacked heel:** confirm factory's stacked-leather-heel process at this smaller scale — the same construction family as the Hug Boot's heel (see [`../hug-boot/manufacturing-brief.md`](../hug-boot/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 3), scaled down.
- **Vamp saddle hardware mount:** confirm the mounting method for the horsebit piece (typically a strap loop or riveted mount through the vamp saddle leather) — the mount must hold up under the hardware's actual weight, since a custom chain-link piece may weigh more than a traditional flat oval snaffle stamping.

## 4. Materials

| Priority | Material | Tannage / Finish | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Aniline nappa calfskin | Vegetable-tanned or veg-chrome combination, full aniline dye, no corrected grain | Flagship, patina-forward option |
| 2 | Smooth calf leather | Semi-aniline or light corrected finish | More polished option on the same platform |

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Manufacturing Notes (Custom Hardware Tooling)

**This is the highest-tooling-risk item in the brief.** A traditional horsebit is a well-established, off-the-shelf hardware stamping in the footwear hardware supply chain; a custom interlocking chain-link redesign is not.

- Confirm whether the factory's hardware supplier can cast or stamp a genuine interlocking chain-link piece at horsebit scale, or whether this requires a jewelry-adjacent hardware manufacturer (similar in kind to how the ring's own chain-link detailing is sourced) rather than a standard footwear-hardware vendor.
- Confirm the chain-link piece's finish (silver-tone, satin/unpolished) matches the system-wide hardware rule — request a physical hardware sample before bulk tooling, since "chain-link horsebit" is enough of a departure from stock hardware that a spec sheet alone risks misinterpretation.
- Confirm weight and mounting durability per Section 3 — a heavier custom piece stresses the vamp mount differently than a lightweight stamped snaffle does.

## 6. Sizing & Last

- **Heel-cup fit — the primary fit risk for this genre:** confirm the last is graded to hold the heel snugly without a strap or lace to help, a known loafer-specific fit issue distinct from every other fit note in this catalogue. Request explicit heel-cup fit feedback during sample review, not just standard length/width confirmation.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** independently grade US 5–7, same rule as the rest of the catalogue.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Factory Conversation

1. **Custom chain-link hardware tooling (Section 5)** — the single highest-risk item; confirm sourcing path (footwear-hardware vendor vs. jewelry-adjacent manufacturer) before other conversation items.
2. **Hardware mount durability under custom-piece weight** — test against the specific chain-link piece's actual weight, not assumed equivalent to a stock snaffle.
3. **Hand-sewn apron seam capability** — confirm as standard vs. a line upgrade.
4. **Heel-cup fit/last grading for a strap-less slip-on** — get explicit fit feedback on the first sample given the genre's known fit risk.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A running prototype in the aniline nappa colorway, single size (US 7), with the chain-link hardware sourced and mounted as specified — approve the hardware sample independently before cutting the full shoe sample, given how much of this brief's risk and cost concentrates in that single custom component.
