# The Hug Boot — Design Vision

*The everyday-to-elevated ankle boot — High tier, and the one shoe in the catalogue that another category's fit math explicitly depends on. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the full shoe catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing design doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Hug Boot.**

This is the shoe `../../bottoms/jeans/jeans-template.md` already names as the pairing that completes wide-leg, flare, and bootcut jeans' leg-lengthening effect — without it, those silhouettes lose part of their intended proportion. That load-bearing role (functional, not just aesthetic) is why this piece sits at High rather than Medium priority despite not being a Phase 1 Must-Have: several other items in the system depend on it existing and fitting correctly. The design argument is a Chelsea silhouette's clean, close-fitting ankle line — no bulky buckles or laces breaking up the leg — crossed with a subtle Western-inspired stacked heel block, giving the boot more visual personality than a plain Chelsea while keeping the "moderate heel worn often" proportion logic the shoes README names as the better long-term investment over a high heel worn rarely.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 1.5" (38mm), stacked leather block | The "moderate heel worn often" target — more usable leg-lengthening across a week than a high heel worn rarely |
| Heel shape | Stacked leather block, Western-inspired | Subtle, not a full Western boot's pronounced angle — a personality detail layered onto a fundamentally stable, wide base |
| Ankle cuff height | Standard Chelsea — clears the ankle bone cleanly | Confirm exact height against reference silhouette; too high starts reading as a tall boot, too low loses the Chelsea identity |
| Toe shape | Rounded / soft almond | Consistent with the rest of the catalogue's comfort-first toe shape rule |
| Closure | Elastic side gussets (primary) + functional side zip (secondary, for easier on/off) | See Section 4 — the two mechanisms coexist by design, not in conflict |

All figures are target specs for prototype confirmation — see `manufacturing-brief.md` Section 2 for the buildable tolerance table.

## 3. Material Palette — Two Options

### 1st — Leather (flagship)
- **Finish:** smooth or lightly grained leather, warm patina-friendly finish consistent with the system-wide "ages, doesn't stay static" rule.
- **Why it fits:** the boot's most versatile, everyday-to-elevated option — pairs with the widest range of bottoms per Section 6.

### 2nd — Suede
- **Finish:** brushed suede, natural nap, light water/stain guard only.
- **Why it's ranked second:** genuinely warmer hand-feel for cool-weather wear, but more vulnerable to water/stain damage — positioned as the Midnight Navy colorway option (Section 6) for occasions where the leather option's higher daily-durability isn't the deciding factor.

## 4. Construction Details

- **Construction method:** Chelsea-style elastic side gussets as the primary entry mechanism, **plus a functional side zip retained for easier on/off.** These aren't in conflict — the elastic gusset delivers the clean Chelsea silhouette and flexes with the ankle rather than fighting it (itself a comfort feature, not just an aesthetic one), while the zip is a practical addition for a boot meant to go on and off constantly, not a design contradiction to resolve away. A leather pull tab at the back heel carries the Section 5 Starlink One detail.
- **Heel construction:** stacked leather heel block — a genuinely different construction from the Anchor Heel's molded/covered heel core (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 4); stacked leather is built from individual leather lifts compressed and shaped into the heel form, a traditional construction method that reads as more "Western-inspired" than a smooth molded heel would.
- **Lining:** shearling or ultra-soft microfiber — a real upgrade from a standard boot lining, chosen specifically for cool-weather warmth without the bulk of a heavier boot liner.
- **Footbed:** cushioned despite the heel — the same memory-foam-over-EVA logic used across the catalogue (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 4), since this boot is worn often enough that comfort can't be traded away for the heel or the Western detailing.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Two Distinct Hardware Moments

Unlike the Perfect Flat or Anchor Heel's single hidden detail, this boot carries two real hardware moments — still quiet relative to the Statement Mule's overt treatment, but the most literal chain-link callback of any everyday (non-statement) shoe in the system:

1. **Chain-link leather pull tab** at the back heel, matching the ring's own chain-link scale — doubles as the boot's actual pull-on tab, so the reference is functional hardware, not applied decoration, the same "structure earns its place" logic used elsewhere in the system.
2. **Emerald-tone zipper pull** on the side zip — a second, distinct touchpoint, small and functional (it's the literal zipper pull) rather than a separate decorative add-on.

Both stay silver-family/emerald-tone per the system-wide hardware rule — never gold. Still restrained relative to the Statement Mule's fully overt instep chain-link (see [`../statement-mule/vision.md`](../statement-mule/vision.md) Section 5) — this piece is an everyday-to-elevated boot, not a going-out statement piece, so the reference stays functional-scale rather than announced.

## 6. Colorway Notes

Jet Black (leather) and Midnight Navy (suede) — both quiet, versatile neutrals consistent with this boot's role as a proportion-critical pairing piece for the jeans system rather than a colorway-forward statement item.

## 7. Sizing & Last Notes

- **Break-in note applies at the ankle cuff, not the toe box** — unlike a flat or sneaker, fit issues on this boot show up at the cuff line first; call this out explicitly to whoever is testing samples.
- **Heel stability shouldn't need breaking in:** if the heel feels wobbly on day one, it's the wrong pair — a stacked leather heel block should read stable immediately, the same standard the Anchor Heel and Statement Mule both hold their heel construction to.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** same rule as the rest of the catalogue for US 5–7.
