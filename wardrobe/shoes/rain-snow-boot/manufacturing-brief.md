# The Rain/Snow Boot — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for factory quoting and prototyping
**Prepared for:** Footwear manufacturer, Shenzhen (technical/waterproof line — confirm this capability exists alongside the factory's standard leather-goods line before quoting; not every general footwear factory runs seam-sealing as a standard process)
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending factory confirmation of gusset seam-sealing feasibility)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into dimensions, materials, and construction methods for an initial quoting and prototyping conversation. Section 7 lists what needs factory engineering confirmation before tooling begins. Unlike every other shoe in this catalogue, real seam-sealed waterproofing (not a water-resistant coating) is a functional non-negotiable here — flag this explicitly and early in any factory conversation, since it may require a different production line or subcontractor than this brand's other footwear.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A women's waterproof Chelsea boot, 0.5–1" heel height, seam-sealed waterproof upper, insulated synthetic lining, rubber lug outsole with functional tread depth.

## 2. Boot Specification

| Parameter | Target | Tolerance |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 13–25mm (0.5–1") | ±1.0mm |
| Outsole | Rubber lug, real tread depth | Confirm minimum tread depth against a genuine technical winter-boot standard (not a fashion-boot lug pattern) — request the factory's existing technical-outsole options rather than a custom fashion tread |
| Upper construction | Seam-sealed throughout, including the elastic gusset | See Section 3 — this is the single gating requirement in this brief |
| Silhouette | Chelsea, matching [`../hug-boot/manufacturing-brief.md`](../hug-boot/manufacturing-brief.md) ankle-line target | Confirm cuff height parity with the Hug Boot for visual consistency across the boot category |

## 3. Waterproofing — The Gating Construction Requirement

**Confirm before any other conversation:** the factory must be able to deliver genuine seam-level waterproofing (taped or sealed seams throughout the upper), not a surface water-resistant spray or coating treatment. This is a functional non-negotiable, not a preference, and needs to be stated explicitly since a factory whose standard process is spray-based water resistance will likely propose that as a "close enough" substitute unless corrected early.

- **The elastic gusset is the hardest point to seal.** A Chelsea gusset is inherently a seam/junction point, and elastic materials are harder to bond a waterproof seal to than a rigid upper panel. Ask the factory directly whether they have existing tooling/process for sealing an elastic gusset specifically — this may be the single highest-risk item in the whole brief and could determine whether the Chelsea silhouette is achievable at all within a genuinely waterproof build, or whether a design compromise (e.g., a bellows-tongue construction instead of an elastic gusset) needs to be discussed.
- **Confirm seam-sealing method:** heat-taped seams (as used in technical outerwear) vs. a fully molded/vulcanized rubber upper (as used on classic rubber rain boots) are the two most likely paths — each has different aesthetic and cost implications, and both should be quoted separately.

## 4. Lining & Insulation

| Layer | Spec |
|---|---|
| Insulation | Synthetic technical insulation (e.g., a Thinsulate-equivalent or comparable synthetic fill) — **not shearling**, which doesn't pair reliably with a fully sealed waterproof build |
| Target profile | Slim, not bulky — confirm insulation thickness target with the factory against a "warm but not clunky" brief, the same tension named in the vision document |
| Interior lining (skin contact) | Soft interior against the ankle despite the technical exterior — confirm this doesn't get value-engineered away as a "technical boot doesn't need a soft interior" assumption |

## 5. Materials

| Component | Material | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Upper | Waterproof-treated leather or rubber-coated waterproof textile | Confirm which option the factory's seam-sealing process is actually built around — the two materials likely require different sealing methods |
| Outsole | Rubber lug, technical tread pattern | Sourced as a technical outdoor/winter-boot outsole, not a fashion lug |

**Note carried from the vision document:** this is the one shoe in the catalogue without a ranked, patina-focused material palette — the aniline leather used elsewhere in this brand's footwear is not compatible with the waterproofing requirement here, and that's a deliberate, accepted tradeoff, not an open question to revisit.

## 6. Sizing & Last

- **Confirm room for a slightly thicker sock** given the insulated lining — this may mean a slightly roomier last than the brand's other boots at the same nominal size; flag explicitly rather than assuming identical grading to the Hug Boot.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** same rule as the rest of the catalogue for US 5–7.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Factory Conversation

1. **Elastic gusset seam-sealing (Section 3)** — the single highest-risk item in this brief; confirm feasibility before any other conversation, and get a real answer on whether a design compromise (e.g., bellows tongue instead of gusset) is needed.
2. **Seam-sealing method selection** — heat-taped vs. molded/vulcanized rubber; get both quoted with cost and lead-time differences.
3. **Technical outsole sourcing** — confirm the factory can source or produce a genuine technical winter tread, not a fashion-boot lug pattern.
4. **Insulation thickness vs. "not clunky" target** — needs a physical sample to validate.
5. **Interior comfort lining** — confirm this isn't dropped as an unnecessary cost on a "purely functional" shoe; it's a stated requirement, not optional.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A running prototype confirming waterproofing first — specifically a seam-sealed sample of just the gusset/upper junction, before committing to a full boot sample — given how much of this brief's risk concentrates in that single construction detail. Once gusset sealing is confirmed achievable, proceed to a full single-size (US 7), single-colorway (Jet Black) prototype.
