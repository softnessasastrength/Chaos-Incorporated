# The Kitten Heel Mary Jane — Design Vision

*The romantic strapped heel — Medium tier, a classic LoveShackFancy-adjacent silhouette that bridges the Perfect Flat and the Anchor Heel. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the full shoe catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing design doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Kitten Heel Mary Jane.**

This piece sits at Medium rather than High or Must-Have priority for an honest reason: the Anchor Heel already delivers this system's "moderate heel worn often" argument at 2.0–2.25", so this shoe isn't filling an urgent functional gap the way the Espadrille Wedge or Horsebit Loafer do — it exists because a round-toe, single-strap kitten heel is a genre-defining LoveShackFancy silhouette the catalogue didn't have, and it's worth building once the Must-Have and High layers are solid, per the priority system's own sequencing rule. The real design problem here is a genuine tension worth naming directly: a "kitten heel" is thin and tapered by definition — that's what makes it a kitten heel rather than a block heel — but every other heeled piece in this catalogue (the Anchor Heel, the Block Heel Pump, the Statement Mule) explicitly avoids a stiletto-thin profile on stability grounds. This piece doesn't get to avoid that tension by switching to a block shape without stopping being a kitten heel; it resolves the tension instead, the same way the Cloud Walker resolves the chunky/proportion tension and the Horsebit Loafer resolves the overt-hardware/quiet-everyday-piece tension.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 1.0–1.5" (25–38mm) | Genuinely low for a heel category — the point is romantic detail and a small lift, not leg-lengthening at Anchor Heel scale |
| Heel shape | **Demi-kitten** — slim and tapered, but not pin-thin | See Section 4 for the resolved stability argument; this is narrower than the Anchor Heel's block but meaningfully wider than a true cocktail-stiletto kitten heel |
| Toe shape | Rounded, classic Mary Jane proportion | Consistent with the catalogue's comfort-first toe rule |
| Closure | Single instep strap, small buckle | The strap is doing real stabilizing work here, not just a styling signature — see Section 4 |

## 3. Material Palette — Two Options

### 1st — Aniline Nappa Calfskin (flagship)
- **Finish:** vegetable-tanned or veg-chrome combination, full aniline dye, no corrected grain — same flagship rule as the rest of the catalogue.

### 2nd — Grosgrain Ribbon Strap, Leather Body
- **Construction:** the shoe body in aniline nappa, with the instep strap itself built from the same hybrid leather/fabric ribbon construction as the Barely There Sandal and Espadrille Wedge (see [`../barely-there-sandal/vision.md`](../barely-there-sandal/vision.md) Section 4) — a soft grosgrain ribbon face over a structural core.
- **Why it's ranked second, not lesser:** the more overtly romantic, LoveShackFancy-coded version of this same platform — offered as a colorway/trim variant once the base last and heel are validated, the same "one platform, multiple upper treatments" logic used across this catalogue.

## 4. Construction Details — Resolving the Kitten Heel's Stability Tension

- **The core design problem, stated plainly:** a true stiletto-thin kitten heel is exactly the precarious profile every other heeled piece in this system exists to avoid. Solving it by simply widening the heel into a block would stop this from being a kitten heel at all — genre and stability are in real tension here, unlike anywhere else in the catalogue.
- **The resolution has two parts, working together, not one alone:**
  1. **A demi-kitten taper** — slimmer than the Anchor Heel's block, but with a wider base-to-height ratio than a true cocktail kitten heel, giving real ground contact without reading as a block.
  2. **A genuinely structural instep strap** — unlike a Mary Jane strap that's purely decorative, this strap is specified to hold the foot securely over the narrower heel base, doing some of the stabilizing work a wider heel base would otherwise do alone. This is the same "the strap is structural, not decorative" logic the Statement Mule's instep strap uses (see [`../statement-mule/vision.md`](../statement-mule/vision.md) Section 4), applied here to compensate for heel width instead of an open back.
- **Heel attachment:** heel nail/dowel-through-shank plus adhesive bonding, the same secure attachment method as the rest of the catalogue's heeled pieces — a thinner heel does not mean a lighter-duty attachment.
- **Footbed:** cushioned, memory foam over EVA, same build as the rest of the catalogue — comfort isn't suspended by this being a romantic, lower-volume piece.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Quiet, In the Buckle Itself

- **Buckle cabochon:** a small emerald cabochon set into the center of the Mary Jane strap's buckle — a genuinely quiet detail, since Mary Jane buckles already carry a small decorative element by genre convention, so this reference sits inside expected hardware rather than adding new hardware, the same "real, expected hardware carrying the reference" logic as the Horsebit Loafer's chain-link (see [`../horsebit-loafer/vision.md`](../horsebit-loafer/vision.md) Section 5) — except here the reference stays quiet rather than overt, appropriate to this piece's Medium tier and its role as a romantic-everyday piece rather than a genre-defining hardware statement.
- **Buckle finish:** silver-tone, satin — never gold, per the system-wide hardware rule.

## 6. Sizing & Last Notes

- **True to size.**
- **Instep strap fit must be correct from the first wear**, the same standard the Statement Mule and Barely There Sandal both hold their straps to — since the strap is doing real stabilizing work here (Section 4), a loose strap is a stability issue, not just a comfort one.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** same rule as the rest of the catalogue for US 5–7.
