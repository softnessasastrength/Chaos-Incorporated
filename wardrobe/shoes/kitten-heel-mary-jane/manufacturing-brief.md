# The Kitten Heel Mary Jane — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for factory quoting and prototyping
**Prepared for:** Footwear manufacturer, Shenzhen
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending factory confirmation of demi-kitten heel stability at the specified taper)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into dimensions, materials, and construction methods for an initial quoting and prototyping conversation. Section 7 lists what needs factory engineering confirmation before tooling begins. The demi-kitten heel taper (Section 2) is the single spec most worth validating physically before committing to tooling — it's a genre-defined shape with a real, named stability tradeoff.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A women's Mary Jane, 1.0–1.5" demi-kitten heel, round toe, single structural instep strap with a small silver-tone buckle carrying an emerald cabochon.

## 2. Heel & Shoe Specification

| Parameter | Target | Tolerance |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 25–38mm (1.0–1.5") | ±0.5mm |
| Heel shape | Demi-kitten — slimmer than a block heel, wider base-to-height ratio than a true stiletto kitten heel | Request a physical reference sample or comparison to a named commercial reference before tooling — "demi-kitten" needs a concrete shape reference, not a verbal description alone |
| Toe shape | Rounded, classic Mary Jane proportion | Confirm last profile against reference |
| Instep strap | Single strap, structural (see Section 3), small buckle | Confirm strap width and anchor points with the factory's pattern engineer |

## 3. Construction Method — Strap as Stability Compensation

- **Heel attachment:** heel nail/dowel-through-shank plus adhesive bonding — confirm this is the factory's standard secure attachment method for a heel at this height/width, not a lighter attachment sometimes used on thinner heels.
- **Instep strap — confirm structural spec, not just styling spec:** the strap needs a stiffer core or reinforcement at its anchor points, the same class of requirement as the Statement Mule's instep strap (see [`../statement-mule/manufacturing-brief.md`](../statement-mule/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 3) — here, the strap is compensating for the heel's narrower base rather than for a missing back strap, but the engineering requirement (a strap that actually holds the foot secure, not just decorative) is the same category of spec.
- **Footbed:** memory foam over EVA, same build as the rest of the catalogue.

## 4. Materials

| Priority | Material | Tannage / Finish | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Aniline nappa calfskin | Vegetable-tanned or veg-chrome combination, full aniline dye, no corrected grain | Flagship — shoe body and strap in the same material |
| 2 | Grosgrain ribbon strap (leather body) | Hybrid ribbon-face/structural-core strap, same construction as [`../barely-there-sandal/manufacturing-brief.md`](../barely-there-sandal/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 3 | Confirm the narrow-strap bonding process applies at this strap's specific width — likely narrower than the sandal's ankle-wrap strap |

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Manufacturing Notes

- **Buckle cabochon:** small emerald cabochon (synthetic or emerald-toned resin) set into the buckle center. Confirm setting method holds up under repeated buckle/unbuckle handling — the same durability question the Gallery One pen's piston-knob cabochon raises for a handled, moving component (see [`../../../objects/pen/manufacturing-brief.md`](../../../objects/pen/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 9) — a buckle is handled at every wear, so this needs a bonded or set method proven for that level of contact, not a static-mount assumption.
- **Buckle finish:** silver-tone, satin. Confirm plating/finish matches the system-wide hardware rule.

## 6. Sizing & Last

- **True to size.**
- **Instep strap fit correct from first wear:** confirm with the factory that strap length/anchor tolerance is tight enough that a loose strap isn't a realistic outcome of normal size-run variance — given the strap does real stability work here, a loose strap is more consequential on this shoe than a purely decorative strap elsewhere.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** independently grade US 5–7, same rule as the rest of the catalogue.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Factory Conversation

1. **Demi-kitten heel stability confirmation (Section 2)** — request a physical sample or named reference comparison before finalizing tooling; this shape sits in genuine tension between genre convention and this brand's stability standards.
2. **Instep strap structural spec vs. a purely decorative Mary Jane strap** — confirm the factory understands this as a functional stability component, not just a styling detail, since a standard Mary Jane strap spec from stock tooling may not meet this requirement.
3. **Cabochon setting durability under repeated buckle handling** — needs a physical wear-test, not a design-review sign-off.
4. **Ribbon-strap variant bonding at a narrower width than the Barely There Sandal's** — confirm separately, don't assume identical tooling transfers directly.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A running prototype in the aniline nappa colorway, single size (US 7), with the demi-kitten heel and structural instep strap as the two specs to validate most carefully in wear-testing before approving the sample or committing to the grosgrain ribbon variant.
