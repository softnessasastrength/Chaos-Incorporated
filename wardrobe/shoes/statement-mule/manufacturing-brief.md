# The Statement Mule — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for factory quoting and prototyping
**Prepared for:** Footwear manufacturer, Shenzhen
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending factory confirmation of instep-strap load-bearing capacity and sculptural heel tooling)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into dimensions, materials, and construction methods for an initial quoting and prototyping conversation. Section 8 lists what needs factory engineering confirmation before tooling begins. The instep strap (Section 3) is the single highest-priority item in this brief — it is the only component holding the shoe on the foot, and it also carries the visible chain-link hardware from Section 6.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A women's open-back mule, 1.5–2" sculptural curved block heel, single wide structural instep strap carrying a visible chain-link hardware detail, leather or suede upper, three colorways (Jet Black, Sterling Grey, Starlink Emerald).

## 2. Heel & Mule Specification

| Parameter | Target | Tolerance |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 38–50mm (1.5–2") | ±1.0mm |
| Heel shape | Sculptural, single continuous curve, low and wide base | **Requires a CAD/vector reference shape delivered to the heel-maker, not a verbal "curved and low" description** — the same class of tooling risk as a squircle corner or an asymmetric window boundary in this brand's other technical briefs |
| Heel base width (ground contact) | Wide relative to height, prioritizing stability given the open back offers no rear support | Confirm factory's recommended minimum for a backless silhouette at this height |
| Toe shape | Rounded or softly squared | Confirm last toe-box profile against reference |

## 3. Instep Strap — Structural Specification, Not a Styling Detail

**This is the gating engineering question for the entire product.** With no back strap or heel counter, the instep strap alone keeps the shoe on the foot — it must be specified and tested as a load-bearing structural component:

- **Width and anchor points:** confirm strap width and both anchor-point locations on the upper with the factory's pattern engineer — anchor placement affects how much of the foot's natural movement the strap needs to accommodate without slipping.
- **Core stiffness:** confirm the strap incorporates a stiffer core or reinforcement (not just a single layer of upper leather) at the anchor points specifically, since these are the highest-stress points on the entire shoe.
- **Added hardware load:** the Section 6 chain-link detail adds weight and rigidity on top of the strap — confirm the strap's structural spec accounts for the hardware's weight, not just the strap material alone; this needs a physical prototype test, not a design-review assumption.

## 4. Sole & Footbed

- **Sole:** leather sole with a rubber grip pad at forefoot and heel contact points — genuine functional requirement given the lack of rear stability backup.
- **Footbed:** cushioned specifically at the forefoot (mules concentrate more pressure there); confirm forefoot cushioning target with the factory against a comparable backless-shoe reference.

## 5. Materials

| Priority | Material | Finish | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Smooth leather | Warm, patina-friendly finish | Works across all three colorways; recommended for the first prototype given the higher durability demand at the instep-strap stress point |
| 2 | Brushed suede | Natural nap | Offer as a colorway-specific option once the leather build and strap engineering are validated — suede has lower durability at the strap anchor points |

## 6. Starlink One Reference — Manufacturing Notes

- **Chain-link instep detail:** confirm attachment method (woven through the strap material vs. laid/stitched on top) — the attachment method must reinforce, not compromise, the strap's Section 3 load-bearing spec. Confirm chain-link scale matches the ring's reference scale per [`../../color-system.md`](../../color-system.md) and existing scale-matching precedent (e.g., the Hug Boot's pull tab, see [`../hug-boot/manufacturing-brief.md`](../hug-boot/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 5).
- **Finish:** hardware left unpolished per the system-wide patina rule — confirm the factory's standard chain-link hardware isn't pre-polished or lacquered by default.

## 7. Sizing & Last

- **Half-size-down guidance:** confirm with the factory whether their standard mule last runs loose in the heel, and if so, document a half-size-down recommendation explicitly rather than leaving it to individual fit feedback after launch.
- **Instep strap fit correct from first wear:** unlike a closed shoe, there's no break-in period that fixes a loose mule strap — this needs to be right in the first sample, not iterated on after initial wear-testing.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** same rule as the rest of the catalogue for US 5–7.

## 8. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Factory Conversation

1. **Instep strap load-bearing engineering (Section 3)** — the gating question for the entire product; test with the full chain-link hardware weight attached, not the bare strap alone.
2. **Sculptural heel curve tooling (Section 2)** — requires CAD/vector delivery to the heel-maker, same risk class as other continuous-curve components in this brand's technical briefs.
3. **Chain-link attachment method vs. strap integrity (Section 6)** — confirm the attachment reinforces rather than weakens the strap.
4. **Mule-specific heel-fit looseness** — confirm and document a half-size-down recommendation if the factory's standard last runs loose in this silhouette.

## 9. Requested Next Step

A running prototype in the Jet Black leather colorway only, single size (US 7), with the instep strap tested under the full weight of the chain-link hardware attachment before approving the sample — this is the one component in the whole build where a design-review sign-off isn't sufficient and a physical wear-test is required before moving to the Sterling Grey or Starlink Emerald colorways.
