# The Statement Mule — Design Vision

*The going-out sculptural mule — Experimental tier, the overt counterpart to the Anchor Heel's everyday quietness. Sits alongside [`../block-heel-pump/vision.md`](../block-heel-pump/vision.md) in the going-out register — a different silhouette (open-back mule vs. closed pump), not a duplicate. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the full 8-shoe catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing design doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Statement Mule.**

Where the Anchor Heel's entire identity is quiet competence — hidden insole deboss, a single thread of color visible only on close inspection — this piece is its going-out counterpart, allowed to show hardware boldly because statement-tier pieces in this system (the hardware moto jacket, the chain-link belt) are the ones with permission to do that. Same "soft shape, hard architecture" logic underneath: a sculptural curved heel is the soft shape, visible chain-link hardware across the instep is the hard architecture, applied at the statement-tier register instead of the everyday one. An open back elongates the visible line of the foot and ankle in a way heel height alone doesn't — no back strap or heel counter breaking up the leg the way a closed shoe does — but that same openness means this is the one shoe in the catalogue with the least structure holding it onto the foot, which is why the instep strap in Section 4 has to do more real work than a strap on any other shoe here.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 1.5–2" (38–50mm), sculptural block | Curved, low, wide base — not a straight cylindrical block; conservative even for a statement piece, since a mule is inherently less secure on the foot than a closed shoe |
| Heel shape | Sculptural — a single continuous curve from breast to base | The taper/curve tooling question this raises is the direct mule-specific parallel to the Anchor Heel's own "soft, gradual taper, not a stiletto taper" spec (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 2) |
| Toe shape | Rounded or softly squared, not pointed | Same grounding logic as the rest of the catalogue, applied even though this silhouette otherwise reads as more of a statement piece |
| Closure | Open back (no strap), single wide instep strap | See Section 4 — the instep strap is structural, not decorative |

## 3. Material Palette — Two Options

### 1st — Leather
- **Finish:** smooth leather, warm patina-friendly finish, works across all three colorways (Section 6).

### 2nd — Suede
- **Finish:** brushed suede, natural nap.
- **Why it's ranked second:** genuinely elevated hand-feel for an evening piece, but lower durability at the instep-strap stress point (Section 4) than leather — worth offering as a colorway-specific option once the leather build is validated, not as the default first sample.

## 4. Construction Details — The Instep Strap Does the Job a Back Strap Normally Does

- **The core structural fact of this shoe:** a mule has no back strap or heel counter holding the heel in place — nothing does that job except the wearer's own grip and the instep strap. That makes the instep strap a load-bearing structural component, not a styling detail, and it needs genuine engineering (width, anchor points, material stiffness at the anchor) to keep the shoe secure on the foot, especially with the added weight of the Section 5 chain-link hardware sitting on top of it.
- **Heel construction:** the sculptural curved heel shape (Section 2) is a continuous-curve profile, not a simple radius — the same class of tooling question as the watch case's squircle corner or the Anchor Heel's soft taper, and it should be supplied to the last-maker/heel-maker as a precise reference shape, not a verbal "curved and low" description alone.
- **Sole:** leather sole with a rubber grip pad at the forefoot and heel contact points — a genuine functional requirement given the open back offers no rear stability backup the way a closed shoe's heel counter does.
- **Footbed:** cushioned specifically at the forefoot, since mules concentrate more pressure there than a closed shoe with full foot support.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Visible, Not Hidden

**A deliberate departure from the Anchor Heel's private-detail philosophy**, and the most literal chain-link callback of any shoe in the catalogue:

- **Chain-link detail woven or laid across the instep strap** — visible, not hidden, matched in scale to the ring's own chain-link detailing per the system-wide "match, don't compete" rule. This sits directly on top of the structural strap named in Section 4, so the hardware attachment method has to reinforce the strap's load-bearing job, not compromise it.
- **Consistent placement of an existing rule, not an exception to it:** statement-tier pieces (the hardware moto jacket, the chain-link belt) are the ones in this system allowed to show hardware boldly; the Anchor Heel and Perfect Flat keep it hidden because they're everyday pieces. This shoe follows the same rule the going-out register already established elsewhere — it just happens to be the most literal application of it in footwear.
- **No exterior emerald color required** on the Jet Black or Sterling Grey colorways — the Starlink Emerald option (Section 6) carries the color statement where wanted; the chain-link hardware carries the ring reference on every colorway regardless of upper color.

## 6. Colorway Notes

Jet Black, Sterling Grey, and Starlink Emerald (statement colorway, reserved for the going-out register alongside the Block Heel Pump) — all three carry the same visible chain-link instep detail from Section 5 regardless of upper color.

## 7. Sizing & Last Notes

- **Consider half-size-down guidance** if the sourced brand's mules run loose in the heel — a common, real mule-specific fit issue given the open back offers no strap to take up slack.
- **Instep strap fit must be correct from the first wear:** unlike a closed shoe that molds to the foot over time, a loose mule strap only gets looser — there's no break-in period that fixes this the way there is for a closed shoe's upper.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** same rule as the rest of the catalogue for US 5–7.
