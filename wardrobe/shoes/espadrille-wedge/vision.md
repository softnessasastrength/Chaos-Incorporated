# The Espadrille Wedge — Design Vision

*The warm-weather wedge — High tier, the most direct Mediterranean-romantic silhouette the catalogue didn't have until now. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the full shoe catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing design doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Espadrille Wedge.**

This piece fills a real, named gap: the Barely There Sandal covers flat-to-low-block warm-weather footwear, and the Block Heel Pump covers going-out height, but nothing in the system delivers genuine warm-weather elevation for daytime wear — the specific LoveShackFancy-adjacent register of a jute wedge under a ribbon-tied or espadrille-laced upper. A wedge is also the one way to add real height that's *more* stable than a block heel, not less — full ground contact along the wedge's length distributes weight differently than a heel-and-forefoot two-point stance does, which is why this piece can sit at a taller stack than the Anchor Heel while still following the same "grounding over height" argument. Where the Barely There Sandal borrows the ring's romantic detail language atmospherically (Section 5 of its own vision doc), the Espadrille Wedge borrows the same ribbon-tie construction language directly — this piece and that one are meant to read as a matched pair across a warm-weather wardrobe.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Wedge height | 2.0–2.75" (50–70mm) | Taller than the Anchor Heel's 2.0–2.25" block heel range — justified by the wedge's full-ground-contact stability, which a block heel of the same height doesn't have |
| Effective pitch angle | 6–8° | Lower than an equivalent-height block heel would produce, since a wedge's continuous ground contact naturally reduces effective pitch versus a point-contact heel at the same stack height |
| Toe shape | Rounded closed toe, or peep-toe on select colorways | Consistent with the catalogue's comfort-first toe shape rule |
| Closure | Ankle-tie ribbon lacing (primary) or slip-on strap (secondary) | See Section 6 |
| Upper coverage | Espadrille-style — vamp coverage similar to a low ballet flat, not a full sandal cutout | Distinguishes this from the Barely There Sandal's minimal strap coverage |

## 3. Material Palette — Two Options

### 1st — Canvas (flagship, classic espadrille identity)
- **Construction:** heavyweight canvas or cotton duck, the traditional espadrille upper material.
- **Why it fits:** the most authentic, most breathable warm-weather option, and the clearest visual signal of "espadrille" as a category — this is the option that reads correctly at a glance.

### 2nd — Aniline Nappa Leather
- **Tannage/finish:** vegetable-tanned or veg-chrome combination, full aniline dye, no corrected grain — the same flagship-material rule used across the rest of the catalogue (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 3).
- **Why it's ranked second, not lesser:** a more elevated, dressier version of the same silhouette for wearers who want the wedge's proportion argument without the canvas option's more casual read — this is the "Dress Anchor" equivalent for this category, a material upgrade on an identical platform.

## 4. Construction Details — The Wedge Itself

- **Wedge core: jute rope, wrapped and compressed.** This is the traditional espadrille wedge construction, chosen deliberately over a foam or cork wedge core (like the Perfect Flat's hidden wedge, see [`../perfect-flat/vision.md`](../perfect-flat/vision.md) Section 4) — the visible, textured jute wrap is a genre-defining visual signal for this silhouette, not just a structural choice, the same way the Anchor Heel's leather-wrapped block heel is both structural and visual.
- **Durability upgrade over a traditional pure-jute sole:** a thin rubber sole layer is bonded to the base of the jute wedge at the ground-contact surface — traditional all-jute-sole espadrilles wear through quickly with real daily use, and this upgrade is the direct "warm hug means built to actually be worn, not admired once" answer to that known weak point, the same durability-first instinct behind the Anchor Heel's replaceable top-lift.
- **Footbed:** memory foam over a firmer base layer, same build logic as the rest of the catalogue's footbeds (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 4) — a jute wedge alone is not cushioned, so this layer is doing real comfort work, not a redundant add-on.
- **Upper construction:** for the canvas option, a sewn-and-glued espadrille-standard construction; for the leather option, the upper is constructed separately and bonded to the jute wedge the same way a standard shoe upper attaches to a sole unit.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Hidden, Woven Into the Ribbon

Following the Barely There Sandal's ribbon-detail precedent rather than the Anchor Heel's insole-deboss precedent, since this piece's most natural private-detail location is the ribbon tie itself, not a hidden insole:

- **Ribbon tie ends:** a fine Starlink Emerald (`#0F5C3D`, see [`../../color-system.md`](../../color-system.md)) thread woven into the very end of each ribbon tie — visible only when the ribbon is untied or being tied, the same "discovered in a private moment" logic as the Anchor Heel's insole mark (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 5), relocated to the one component on this shoe that's actually handled directly during wear.
- **No jute-wrap color accent:** the natural jute texture stays undyed and untouched by any Starlink Emerald accent — introducing color into the wedge itself would compete with the jute's own natural, warm material story rather than complementing it.

## 6. The Two Closures

### Ankle-Tie Ribbon (primary)
Grosgrain ribbon ties that wrap the ankle and tie at the back or side — the direct construction and styling echo of the Barely There Sandal's Everyday Strap version (see [`../barely-there-sandal/vision.md`](../barely-there-sandal/vision.md) Section 6), using the same hybrid leather/fabric strap construction (soft ribbon face, structural core) to survive the repeated tying and untying this closure sees in real wear.

### Slip-On Strap (secondary)
A single fixed vamp strap, no ankle wrap — for wearers who want the wedge's height without the ribbon-tie's daily lacing ritual. Same platform, simpler upper.

## 7. Sizing & Last Notes

- **True to size** for the leather option; **canvas may run slightly large**, the same fit note the Cloud Walker's canvas option carries (see [`../cloud-walker/vision.md`](../cloud-walker/vision.md) Section 7) — canvas espadrille uppers are a known category where this variance is common.
- **Wedge height is more forgiving of minor last-grading imprecision than a block heel is,** since there's no thin heel breast/base geometry to get wrong — but last grading for US 5–7 should still be independently confirmed, not linearly scaled, per the catalogue-wide rule.
- **Ribbon-tie fit:** confirm the ankle-tie length accommodates a genuine range of ankle circumferences without excess ribbon length becoming a tripping hazard — a real, checkable fit-safety item, not just a styling detail.
