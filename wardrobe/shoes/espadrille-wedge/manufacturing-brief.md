## 1. Product Overview

A women's espadrille wedge, 2.0–2.75" jute-rope wedge core with a bonded rubber sole layer for durability, canvas or aniline leather upper, ankle-tie ribbon lacing (primary) or slip-on strap (secondary) closure.

## 2. Wedge & Shoe Specification

| Parameter | Target | Tolerance |
|---|---|---|
| Wedge height | 50–70mm (2.0–2.75") | ±1.5mm |
| Effective pitch angle | 6–8° | Confirm against a physical sample — this is a feel spec as much as a dimension, given the wedge's full-ground-contact stability claim |
| Toe shape | Rounded closed toe, peep-toe on select colorways | Confirm last toe-box profile against reference |
| Ribbon tie length | Confirm against a genuine range of ankle circumferences | Flag as a fit-safety item, not purely a styling spec — excess ribbon length is a real tripping-hazard consideration |

## 3. Construction Method — The Jute Wedge

- **Wedge core: jute rope, wrapped and compressed** — confirm the factory's standard espadrille-wedge process (rope-winding density, compression method) and whether this requires a distinct production line or subcontractor from the brand's standard leather-goods footwear line.
- **Durability upgrade — the single most important construction decision on this product:** a thin rubber sole layer bonded to the base of the jute wedge at the ground-contact surface. Confirm bonding method (typically vulcanized or cemented) holds up under repeated wear without delaminating from the jute — this is the direct answer to a well-known espadrille weak point (pure-jute soles wearing through quickly), so the bond quality here is not a cosmetic detail but the product's core durability claim.
- **Upper attachment:** canvas upper via standard sewn-and-glued espadrille construction; leather upper bonded to the wedge using the same attachment method this brand's other footwear uses for upper-to-sole attachment — confirm compatibility between a jute wedge surface and standard sole-attachment adhesives, since jute's texture differs from a standard rubber or leather sole surface.

## 4. Footbed

| Layer | Spec |
|---|---|
| Sock lining | Soft textile or leather, matching upper material |

## 6. Starlink One Reference — Manufacturing Notes

- **Ribbon tie end detail:** fine Starlink Emerald (`#0F5C3D`) thread woven into the ribbon tie ends. Confirm thread color-match process against [`../../color-system.md`](../../color-system.md) and whether this can be done inline during ribbon manufacturing or requires a separate finishing step per unit.
- **No dye/accent on the jute wedge itself** — confirm this is communicated clearly to the factory so a well-meaning "brand color accent" isn't added to the wedge wrap by default.

## 7. Sizing & Last

- **Canvas may run large; leather runs true to size** — confirm both explicitly and reflect in size-guide documentation, the same two-material sizing split the Cloud Walker brief documents (see [`../cloud-walker/manufacturing-brief.md`](../cloud-walker/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 7).
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** independently grade US 5–7, same rule as the rest of the catalogue.

## 8. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Factory Conversation

1. **Jute-wedge production line sourcing** — confirm whether this requires a specialist espadrille vendor distinct from the brand's standard footwear line.
2. **Rubber-to-jute sole bonding durability (Section 3)** — the core durability claim of the product; request a wear-test sample specifically at this bond line before approving.
3. **Ribbon-tie strap bonding at this specific width** — confirm against the Barely There Sandal's established process, since strap width may differ between the two products.
4. **Canvas-vs-leather sizing discrepancy** — get in writing for size-guide accuracy.
5. **Ribbon tie length / fit-safety confirmation** — confirm against a real range of ankle circumferences.

## 9. Requested Next Step

A running prototype in the canvas option, ankle-tie ribbon closure, single size (US 7), single colorway (Cloud Ivory) — with particular attention to the rubber-to-jute sole bond (Section 3), since that detail determines whether this piece meets the "built to actually be worn" durability standard the rest of the catalogue holds to, rather than reading as a delicate, occasion-only sandal.
