# The Anchor Heel — Design Vision

*The everyday heel — Must-Have tier, meant to be in almost every look in the wardrobe. Distinct from [`../06-block-heel-pump.md`](../06-block-heel-pump.md), the Phase 3 going-out statement heel — that piece is a rare-occasion bet; this one is the daily driver [`../README.md`](../README.md) already named as the better long-term investment: "a moderate heel worn often gives more usable leg-lengthening across a week than a high heel worn rarely." This is that heel, finally built.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**Anchor One.**

The naming deliberately echoes Starlink One — not a coincidence, a lineage. Where Starlink One is a soft shape (a heart) held secure by visible engineering (four prongs, an open gallery), Anchor One is the same idea run through footwear: a body's full weight, held stable and unafraid by a heel that's wide enough to trust and low enough to never think about. "Anchor" is literal, not just a mood word — this heel's entire engineering brief is grounding. It exists so that height can be gained without a single ounce of the precariousness heels usually cost. Soft leather, warm cushioning, and a wide, honest base holding it all up — the manifesto's "soft materials, deliberate structure" principle, worn on the foot, every day, without ceremony.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 2.0–2.25" (50–57mm) | The requested sweet spot — high enough to lengthen the leg line meaningfully at 63", low enough to never read as a "statement" heel |
| Heel shape | Softly tapered block — wide at the breast, narrowing gently (not sharply) toward the top-lift | Not a stiletto taper, not a stiff cylindrical block — a shape that reads refined but never precarious |
| Heel top width (at breast, where heel meets shoe) | ~34mm (front-to-back) × 30mm (side-to-side) | Provides the structural base the upper attaches to |
| Heel base width (at top-lift, ground contact) | ~28mm (front-to-back) × 24mm (side-to-side) | Roughly 3× the ground-contact footprint of a stiletto — this width, not the height, is what actually delivers "grounding, never precarious" |
| Heel pitch angle (effective) | 8–10° | Reduced from the 12–14° a true 2–2.25" heel would otherwise create, via the hidden forefoot platform below — see Stack Height |
| Stack height (forefoot) | 5–8mm hidden platform under the ball of the foot | Not visible as a "platform shoe" — this is the standard comfort-engineering trick that quietly reduces effective pitch angle without changing the silhouette |
| Top-lift material | Thermoplastic rubber, 60–65 Shore A hardness | Durable, grippy, and — critically — **replaceable** (see Construction) rather than fused permanently to the heel core |
| Toe shape | Rounded / soft almond, not pointed | Comfort and stability both prefer a rounded toe; a pointed toe at this heel height adds forefoot pressure the "warm hug" brief explicitly rejects |

All figures above are target specs for prototype confirmation, not locked tolerances — final numbers get set once a physical last and prototype exist (see `manufacturing-brief.md`).

## 3. Material Palette — Three Options, Ranked

Every option stays in the system's warm, patina-friendly material language — no corrected-grain, heavily pigmented, "will look the same in five years" leather anywhere on this list. That's a deliberate exclusion, not an oversight.

### 1st — Vegetable-Tanned Aniline Nappa Calfskin (recommended, flagship material)
- **Tannage:** vegetable-tanned or vegetable-chrome combination tanned, full-grain calfskin.
- **Finish:** aniline dye — the leather is dyed through with soluble dyes and left with its natural grain and surface visible, **no corrected/pigmented topcoat**. This is the single most important material decision on the whole product: aniline leather visibly softens, deepens in tone, and develops character with wear — it is the footwear equivalent of the ring's unprotected sterling silver. A corrected-grain leather would look identical on day 1 and day 1,000; this leather is supposed to look *better* on day 1,000.
- **Thickness:** 1.2–1.4mm.
- **Why it fits Warm Hug:** softest hand-feel of the three options, the most direct "wear it in, don't varnish it" material story in the whole palette, and the only one of the three that actually improves with the exact daily wear this shoe is designed for.

### 2nd — Brushed Suede
- **Tannage:** chrome or vegetable-tanned split or full-grain suede, brushed finish.
- **Finish:** natural brushed nap, no heavy protective spray coating (a light water/stain guard is acceptable, a heavy coating that stiffens the nap is not).
- **Thickness:** 1.0–1.3mm.
- **Why it fits Warm Hug:** the warmest possible hand-feel of the three — genuinely the "hug" material — but ranked second because suede doesn't patina with the same visible, graceful character aniline leather does, and it's more vulnerable to water/stain damage for a shoe meant to be worn constantly, not occasionally. Best reserved for the Dress Anchor variation (Section 6), where lower daily wear-and-tear exposure makes the tradeoff worth it.

### 3rd — Smooth Semi-Aniline Calf Leather
- **Tannage:** chrome-tanned calfskin, light semi-aniline finish (a thin protective topcoat over an aniline base — some patina capacity retained, more scuff/stain resistance than full aniline).
- **Finish:** semi-matte, light natural grain visible through the finish.
- **Thickness:** 1.2–1.5mm.
- **Why it fits Warm Hug:** the practical, durability-first option — still warm in hand-feel and still carries *some* patina character, but prioritizes daily resilience over the maximum "ages beautifully" story. This is the material of record for the Travel Anchor variation (Section 6), where durability against real travel wear matters more than romantic patina.

## 4. Construction Details

- **Construction method: Blake stitch** (also called Blake or Blake-Rapid construction) — the upper, insole, and outsole are stitched together in a single line run from inside the shoe. Chosen explicitly **over Goodyear welt construction**, which is more traditional and more durable but produces a stiffer, more rigid shoe — directly against the "supportive but not stiff" brief. Blake construction gives a slimmer profile and a sole that flexes closer to the way a foot actually moves, which is the mechanical basis of the "hug" feeling, not just the materials layered on top of it.
- **Insole / footbed build (top to bottom):**
  1. Soft napped leather or moisture-wicking textile sock lining (skin contact layer).
  2. **Memory foam layer**, 4–6mm thick, visco-elastic, target density 3–4 lb/ft³ — this is the layer doing the actual "hug" work, contouring to the foot rather than resisting it.
  3. Firmer EVA base layer, 6–8mm, providing structural support underneath the memory foam so the footbed doesn't compress flat under repeated daily wear.
  4. Contoured arch support, moderate (not rigid/orthotic-level) — arch support matters more at 2–2.25" than at flat height, since weight shifts forward onto the ball of the foot; the contour is gentle, built to feel like it's *there* without ever feeling corrective.
- **Heel attachment:** a reinforced wood or high-density polymer heel core, secured through the shank via a long heel nail/dowel plus adhesive bonding — the traditional, most secure heel-attachment method, chosen for genuine long-term stability rather than a lighter cemented-only attachment that could work loose with daily wear.
- **Top-lift:** replaceable — screw or nail-set, not permanently fused to the heel core. This means the one component that actually touches the ground and wears down fastest can be serviced and replaced without discarding the whole shoe — a direct, practical extension of the "wear it in, don't varnish it" philosophy applied to footwear's actual lifecycle, not just its finish.
- **Edge finishing:** hand-burnished sole and heel-breast edges, finished tone-matched to the upper leather (or in a quiet Sterling Grey/Midnight Navy contrast for the Dress Anchor — see Section 6).

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Hidden, Not Announced

Two details, both deliberately private rather than decorative-on-the-outside — discovered, not advertised, matching the same "quiet, felt more than seen" logic already used for the packaging box's blind deboss and the watch crown's engraving:

1. **Insole detail:** a small blind-debossed heart-and-chain-link mark on the sock lining, positioned where the ball of the foot rests — visible only when the shoe is off or being put on, never while worn or photographed on the shelf. The same "discovered in a private moment" logic as the note card tucked inside the wardrobe packaging.
2. **Heel-breast seam thread:** a single fine thread of **Starlink Emerald** run along the inside edge of the heel-breast stitch line — invisible from a normal viewing distance, visible only on close inspection. A quiet color echo of the ring, not a color *statement* the way the ribbon-tie camisole's full-emerald colorway is.

No exterior hardware, no visible chain-link trim on this shoe — that treatment is reserved for genuinely hardware-forward pieces elsewhere in the system (the chain-link belt, the hardware moto jacket). The Anchor Heel's whole identity is quiet competence; a loud ring reference on the outside would work against that.

## 6. Three Variations

All three share the core spec (Section 2) and construction method (Section 4) unless noted. Differences are material, exact height within the 2.0–2.25" range, and finishing.

### Everyday Anchor — Must-Have
*The default. The one meant to be in almost every look.*

| Spec | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 2.0" (low end of range — maximum all-day wearability) |
| Material | 1st choice: vegetable-tanned aniline nappa calfskin |
| Colorway | Cloud Ivory or Sterling Grey |
| Sole | Leather sole with a rubber half-sole insert at the forefoot and heel top-lift for grip without sacrificing the Blake-construction flexibility |
| Cushioning | Full spec per Section 4 (4–6mm memory foam + EVA base) |
| Occasion | Jeans, wide-leg trousers, everyday dresses and midi skirts — the whole wardrobe, most days |

### Dress Anchor — High
*A half-step more refined, for occasions that want slightly more polish without leaving the "everyday" family.*

| Spec | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 2.25" (top of range — a little extra lengthening for a dressier look) |
| Material | 2nd choice: brushed suede, or aniline nappa in a refined finish |
| Colorway | Blush Mauve or Jet Black; Starlink Emerald as a bolder seasonal option |
| Sole | Full leather sole, hand-burnished edge in a contrast tone |
| Heel shape | Very slightly more tapered than the Everyday Anchor while staying within the same stable base-width range from Section 2 — a visual refinement, not a stability compromise |
| Occasion | Dinner, gallery/dressier daytime, occasions one step up from everyday but still nowhere near the going-out statement register of `../06-block-heel-pump.md` |

### Travel Anchor — High
*Built for durability and cushioning under repeated real-world wear, not romance.*

| Spec | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 2.0" (low end — maximum stability for unfamiliar terrain, long days) |
| Material | 3rd choice: smooth semi-aniline calf leather — durability-first |
| Colorway | Midnight Navy or Sterling Grey — practical, forgiving of travel wear |
| Sole | Full rubber outsole (not leather) with added tread pattern for grip on varied surfaces |
| Cushioning | Upgraded — memory foam layer increased to 6–8mm for extra all-day comfort on travel days |
| Occasion | Travel days, extended standing/walking, anywhere durability matters more than patina |

## 7. Sizing & Last Notes

- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** the last must be independently graded for sizes US 5–7, not just linearly scaled down from a size 8–9 master last. This is a real, common footwear QC failure — toe box and vamp proportions that look correct at size 8–9 often look stubby or oddly oversized when a last is just shrunk uniformly rather than re-proportioned for a smaller foot. Flag this explicitly to the last-maker; it's the single highest-leverage fit note for a 63" frame's typically smaller foot size.
- **Width options:** offer three widths — Narrow (B), Medium (M, standard), Wide (W). Target ball-of-foot widths at a US size 7 reference: B ≈ 3.3", M ≈ 3.5", W ≈ 3.7" (confirm exact figures with the last-maker; these are target ranges, not locked measurements).
- **Heel counter padding:** regardless of width option, the heel counter (back of the shoe, around the Achilles) gets substantial soft padding — this is one of the most common real-world heel-fit complaints (heel slip, rubbing) and is treated here as a comfort-priority spec, not an afterthought.
- **Standard stock width:** Medium (M) as the default/standard-stock width, with B and W available as made-to-order or a smaller secondary production run once M is validated — same "prove the core, then expand" logic the wardrobe's own priority system already uses for colorways.
