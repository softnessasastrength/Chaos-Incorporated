## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- A wrap/ribbon-tie ankle strap (Everyday version) adds a small visual anchor at the ankle, which reads as intentional rather than a bare flat sandal disappearing against a shorter leg.
- Proportion notes: keep straps slim, not chunky, so they don't visually cut the leg short; a low block heel (0.5–1.5") adds some of the "moderate heel worn often" leg-lengthening the shoes category calls for.
- Comfort priority: a cushioned footbed is essential, since a minimal-strap sandal has very little structure around the foot itself — this is the one shoe category where "delicate-looking" and "durable" are most in tension, and the construction spec below exists specifically to resolve that tension rather than pick one side of it.

## The Two Versions
- **Everyday Strap:** the original ribbon-tie ankle wrap — ribbon-tie or buckle closure, worn with denim, shorts, everyday warm-weather looks.
- **Dressy Slide:** a strapless or single-vamp-strap slide, no ankle wrap, cleaner and more minimal — for occasions that want the same "barely there" footbed comfort without the ribbon detail's visual presence.

## Technical Specs
- Heel height: 0–1.5" (flat to low block), both versions
- Sizing notes: True to size
- Sole: Leather or rubber outsole
- **Footbed: full memory foam over a cork base layer** — an upgrade from a standard cushioned footbed; the cork layer adds structural support and shapes to the foot over time (cork sandal footbeds are a known, real comfort-footwear convention, not a marketing term), while the memory foam on top delivers the immediate "hug" softness
- Closure type: Ribbon-tie or buckle ankle wrap (Everyday); single vamp strap, no ankle wrap (Dressy Slide)

## Fabric & Feel
- Upper material: **hybrid leather/fabric straps** — a soft fabric or grosgrain ribbon face bonded to a leather or webbing core, rather than fabric alone. This is the direct construction answer to the delicate-vs-durable tension named above: the strap *looks* and *feels* like soft ribbon at the surface, but the bonded core carries the actual structural load, so it doesn't stretch out or fray at the stress points the way an unsupported fabric-only strap would.
- Hand-feel / skin feel: Soft strap lining where it touches skin — no raw-edge straps that can rub
- Breathability: Excellent (open sandal)
- Break-in behavior: Leather-cored straps soften within a few wears; if a strap rubs at the same point every time on day one, it's the wrong pair

## Chaos Incorporated Notes
- Aesthetic tie-in: the clearest warm-weather LoveShackFancy-DNA shoe in the system.
- Modifications wanted: confirm the hybrid strap construction (fabric face, leather/webbing core) is achievable at the ribbon-tie's original slim strap width — bonding two materials at a very narrow strap width is a real construction constraint worth checking with a sample before assuming it scales down cleanly.
- Care instructions: leather conditioner periodically, avoid wearing in rain — the fabric-faced straps still don't hold up to water even with the leather-cored construction underneath.
