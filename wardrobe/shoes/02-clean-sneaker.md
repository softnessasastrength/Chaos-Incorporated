## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- **The chunky/proportion tension, resolved:** a full dad-sneaker sole (often 2"+ stack) would work directly against the frame math the rest of this shoe category is built on. This piece takes the *silhouette cues* of a chunky sneaker (a more substantial, rounded midsole profile, visible layered sole construction) without taking the full stack height — capped at ~1.25–1.5" total sole height, elevated from the original ~1" low-profile spec but well under true chunky-trend proportions. Reads as "elegant chunky," not "platform sneaker."
- Proportion notes: low-top silhouette preserved (a high-top would visually cut the ankle and shorten the leg on a shorter frame) — the chunkiness lives in the sole profile, not the ankle height.
- Comfort priority: this is the highest-mileage shoe in the rotation — cushioning and break-in behavior matter more here than in any other shoe category.

## Technical Specs
- Sole height: ~1.25–1.5" total stack (elevated-but-proportion-safe — see reconciliation note above)
- Upper weight: target 280–310gsm-equivalent — a noticeably soft, buttery upper hand rather than a stiff technical-sneaker feel
- Sizing notes: true to size for leather styles; canvas styles sometimes run large, check reviews
- Sole: rubber outsole, cushioned midsole, removable insole (see Fabric & Feel)
- Closure type: lace-up
- Waterproofing: Gore-Tex lining offered as an option on select colorways (practical upgrade for wet-weather everyday wear, not a standard spec on every pair)

## Fabric & Feel
- Upper material: soft leather or premium canvas, targeting the 280–310gsm-equivalent softness spec above
- Hand-feel / skin feel: soft interior lining, padded collar to prevent heel rub
- Insole: **removable memory foam insole** — swappable for orthotics if needed, and replaceable as it compresses with heavy daily wear rather than the whole shoe needing replacement (same "serviceable, not disposable" logic as the Anchor Heel's replaceable top-lift)
- Interior detail: hidden Starlink Emerald stitching on the interior lining — not visible while worn, only seen when the shoe is off or being put on
- Breathability: good, especially canvas versions; excellent with the Gore-Tex option specifically for wet-weather breathability-vs-waterproofing balance
- Break-in behavior: leather styles need 1–2 wears to soften at the crease; canvas is comfortable from first wear
