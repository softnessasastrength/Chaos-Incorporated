## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- A near-flat adds almost no visible height, which means it stays the "neutral" variable in the leg-lengthening math established across the bottoms system — it works best paired with silhouettes that don't depend on a heel to complete their proportion (straight-leg, skinny, cropped-straight, mom jean's tapered ankle).
- Proportion notes: a rounded toe (softer, more in-scale on a smaller frame than a long pointed toe) plus a **0.5" hidden internal wedge** — invisible from the outside, so it still reads as a flat, but takes a small amount of pressure off the forefoot and adds a whisper of the leg-lengthening effect a true heel provides. The ballet-flat upper is cut slightly higher at the vamp than a pure ballet flat (closer to a loafer's coverage), which is what gives this piece its "hybrid" identity and a bit more structure holding the foot in place.
- Comfort priority: this is a walked-in-constantly piece — footbed cushioning is not optional, and the extremely soft upper (see Fabric & Feel) matters as much as the sole.

## Technical Specs
- Heel height: 0.5" hidden internal wedge (external silhouette still reads flat)
- Sizing notes: true to size; check if the brand runs narrow, since flats have the least room to compensate for a fit error
- Sole: rubber outsole, cushioned foam insole over the hidden wedge
- Closure type: slip-on

## Fabric & Feel
- Upper material: extremely soft nappa leather or high-quality leather-alternative — softer hand than a standard ballet flat upper, since this is meant to feel closer to a slipper than traditional stiff-vamped ballet flats
- Hand-feel / skin feel: soft interior lining, no rigid seams across the toe box
- Breathability: Good (leather), moderate (synthetic alternatives)
- Break-in behavior: quality leather flats soften at the vamp within the first few wears; if it's still painful after that, it's the wrong size
