## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- This is the shoe the jeans system's fit math explicitly assumes: `bottoms/jeans/jeans-template.md` names a heeled ankle boot as the pairing that completes wide-leg, flare, and bootcut jeans' leg-lengthening effect — without it, those silhouettes lose part of their intended proportion.
- Proportion notes: a 1.5" stacked leather heel is the "moderate heel worn often" the shoes README calls for — more usable leg-lengthening across a whole week than a high heel worn rarely. The Chelsea silhouette's clean, close-fitting ankle line (no bulky buckles or laces breaking up the leg) plus a subtle Western-inspired stacked-heel block gives this boot more visual personality than a plain Chelsea without sacrificing the low-drama proportion logic.
- Comfort priority: cushioned footbed and a stable (not stiletto-thin) heel base matter more at this height, since this shoe is meant to be worn often, not just for a single event. The elastic side-gusset construction (see Closure Type) is itself a comfort feature — it flexes with the ankle rather than fighting it the way a rigid zip-only construction can.

## Technical Specs
- Heel height: 1.5", stacked leather block
- Sizing notes: true to size; break-in note applies at the ankle cuff, not the toe box
- Sole: rubber outsole with a stable heel base
- Closure type: Chelsea-style elastic side gussets (no laces) as the primary construction, **plus a functional side zip retained for easier on/off** — the two aren't in conflict; the elastic gusset gives the clean Chelsea silhouette and flex, the zip is a practical add for a boot meant to go on and off constantly, not a design contradiction. A leather pull tab at the back heel carries the Starlink One detail (see Starlink One Pairing).

## Fabric & Feel
- Upper material: leather or suede
- Lining: **shearling or ultra-soft microfiber** — a real upgrade from a standard boot lining, chosen specifically for cool-weather warmth without the bulk of a heavier boot liner
- Hand-feel / skin feel: cushioned footbed despite the heel, soft ankle cuff lining
- Breathability: moderate (leather), better with a suede upper
- Break-in behavior: leather/suede softens at the ankle cuff within a few wears; heel stability shouldn't need "breaking in" — if it feels wobbly on day one, it's the wrong pair
