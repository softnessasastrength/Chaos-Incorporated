# The Barely There Sandal — Design Vision

*The warm-weather sandal — Medium tier, built as two named versions sharing one footbed and strap-construction philosophy. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the full 8-shoe catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing design doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Barely There Sandal**, built as two versions — **Everyday Strap** and **Dressy Slide** — off one shared construction platform.

This category holds the most direct tension in the whole shoe system: "delicate-looking" and "durable" are usually opposites in sandal construction, since a minimal-strap sandal has the least structure of any shoe category holding the foot in place. Rather than picking a side of that tension, this piece resolves it directly in the construction spec (Section 4) — the strap *looks* like soft ribbon at the surface while a bonded structural core underneath does the actual load-bearing work. This is the clearest warm-weather LoveShackFancy-DNA piece in the whole catalogue, and the only shoe whose Starlink One connection is atmospheric rather than a literal hardware detail (Section 5) — it borrows the ring's romantic detail language instead of quoting the ring directly.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 0–1.5" (flat to low block), both versions | Adds some of the "moderate heel worn often" leg-lengthening the shoes category calls for, without introducing instability to a minimal-strap silhouette |

## 3. Material Palette

### Straps — Hybrid Leather/Fabric (both versions)
A soft fabric or grosgrain ribbon face bonded to a leather or webbing core, rather than fabric alone — see Section 4 for why this is the construction answer to the delicate-vs-durable tension, not just a material choice.

### Footbed — Cork + Memory Foam (both versions)
A cork base layer under a memory foam top layer — cork sandal footbeds are an established, real comfort-footwear convention (not a marketing term), adding structural support and shaping to the foot over time, while the memory foam on top delivers the immediate "hug" softness. This is an upgrade from a standard cushioned footbed, appropriate to a category where the footbed is doing more of the total comfort work than in any closed shoe.

## 4. Construction Details — Resolving the Delicate/Durable Tension

- **The core design problem:** an unsupported fabric-only strap stretches out and frays at stress points — the ribbon-tie wrap and vamp strap are exactly the components carrying the most repeated flex and tension in normal wear. A construction that's "soft ribbon all the way through" fails exactly where it matters most.
- **The resolution:** the strap's visible face is soft fabric or grosgrain ribbon, bonded to a leather or webbing core that carries the actual structural load. The wearer sees and feels ribbon; the shoe holds up like a leather-strap sandal. This is a direct, checkable engineering answer to a stated design tension, the same "structure earns its place" logic used across the system — not a soft material chosen for its own sake.
- **Footbed construction:** cork base layer (structural, shapes to the foot over time) topped with memory foam (immediate cushioning) — see Section 3.
- **Sole:** leather or rubber outsole depending on version and colorway.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Atmospheric, Not Literal

Unlike every other shoe in the catalogue, this piece carries **no direct hardware or color echo** of the ring. Its connection is atmospheric: this sandal is the piece most likely to appear in the same outfit as the ribbon-tie camisole and tiered ruffle top, forming a ribbon-detail motif across the full look. That's not literally the ring, but it reinforces the same romantic detail language the ring's chain-link and heart motif sit within — a deliberate choice, not an oversight. Forcing a literal hardware reference onto this piece's minimal, all-ribbon identity would compete with the one detail (the ribbon itself) this shoe exists to carry. If a buckle is used on the Everyday Strap version, it stays silver-tone or pewter per the system-wide hardware rule, but this is incidental hardware, not a designed-in reference.

Both versions share the last, footbed, and strap-construction philosophy (Sections 3–4) — the difference is upper configuration only, the same "one validated platform, multiple upper treatments" logic the Anchor Heel applies across its own three variations.

## 7. Sizing & Last Notes

- **True to size**, both versions.
- **Strap fit is the primary fit risk in this category, not length/width sizing:** a strap that rubs at the same point on day one is the wrong pair — unlike a closed shoe, this category can't rely on break-in to fix a poorly placed strap.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** same rule as the rest of the catalogue for US 5–7.
