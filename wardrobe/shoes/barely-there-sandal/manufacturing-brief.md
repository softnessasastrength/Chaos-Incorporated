# The Barely There Sandal — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for factory quoting and prototyping
**Prepared for:** Footwear manufacturer, Shenzhen
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending factory confirmation of narrow-strap bonding feasibility)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into dimensions, materials, and construction methods for an initial quoting and prototyping conversation. Section 7 lists what needs factory engineering confirmation before tooling begins — the narrow hybrid-strap bonding process (Section 3) is the single highest-risk construction question in this brief.

---

## 1. Product Overview

Two sandal versions — Everyday Strap (ribbon-tie ankle wrap) and Dressy Slide (single vamp strap, no ankle wrap) — sharing one last, one cork-and-memory-foam footbed, and one hybrid leather/fabric strap construction.

## 2. Sandal Specification

| Parameter | Target | Tolerance |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 0–1.5" (0–38mm), both versions | ±1.0mm at the upper end of the range |
| Strap width (Everyday ankle wrap) | Slim — confirm exact width against reference silhouette before tooling | ±1mm |
| Strap width (Dressy vamp strap) | Slim-to-moderate, single strap | ±1mm |
| Toe shape | Open, no closed toe box | N/A |
| Footbed layer heights | Cork base (confirm factory-standard cork sandal footbed thickness) + memory foam top layer, 4–6mm | Confirm against factory's standard cork-footbed sandal line |

## 3. Strap Construction — The Gating Question

**This is the single most important construction question in this brief.** The strap must read and feel as soft fabric or grosgrain ribbon at the surface, while a bonded leather or webbing core underneath carries the actual structural/tension load — this is what allows the strap to survive real wear at the ribbon-tie wrap and vamp-strap stress points without stretching or fraying the way an unsupported fabric-only strap would.

- **Confirm bonding process at narrow strap width:** bonding two distinct materials (soft face + structural core) at a genuinely slim strap width is a real construction constraint, not just a materials-list item — request a strap-only sample swatch before committing to the full shoe sample, since this detail is easier and cheaper to validate in isolation first.
- **Confirm adhesive/stitch-bond method:** ask whether the factory's standard hybrid-strap process uses adhesive lamination, edge-stitching, or both, and whether either method holds up at the target strap width without a stiff, unribbon-like hand-feel — the whole point of this construction is that it shouldn't feel structural even though it is.

## 4. Footbed Construction

- **Cork base layer:** confirm factory's standard cork sandal footbed thickness and sourcing — cork sandal footbeds are an established construction in the existing sandal supply chain, not a custom material request.
- **Memory foam top layer:** 4–6mm, visco-elastic, matching the density target used elsewhere in the catalogue (3–4 lb/ft³ — see [`../anchor-heel/manufacturing-brief.md`](../anchor-heel/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 4).
- **Sole:** leather or rubber outsole depending on version/colorway — confirm per-version sole spec at the quoting stage, since the Dressy Slide's more formal positioning may call for a leather sole while the Everyday Strap's more casual daily use may call for rubber.

## 5. Materials

| Component | Material | Spec |
|---|---|---|
| Strap face | Soft fabric or grosgrain ribbon | Confirm colorfastness and fray resistance at cut edges before bonding |
| Strap core | Leather or webbing | Carries structural load — confirm tensile spec with factory against expected wear (daily ankle-wrap flex for Everyday Strap; lower-frequency wear for Dressy Slide) |
| Footbed | Cork + memory foam | Per Section 4 |
| Buckle hardware (Everyday Strap, if used) | Silver-tone or pewter metal | Per system-wide hardware rule — never gold |

| Spec | Everyday Strap | Dressy Slide |
|---|---|---|
| Closure | Ribbon-tie or buckle ankle wrap | Single vamp strap, no ankle wrap |
| Colorway | Cloud Ivory, Blush Mauve | Cloud Ivory, Sterling Grey (pewter metallic option) |

**Recommended build order:** sample the shared last and footbed once, then build both upper configurations off that validated base — the same "prove the shared platform first" logic the Anchor Heel brief applies to its own three variations (see [`../anchor-heel/manufacturing-brief.md`](../anchor-heel/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 7).

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Factory Conversation

1. **Narrow hybrid-strap bonding (Section 3)** — the gating construction question for this entire product; request an isolated strap swatch sample before the full shoe sample.
2. **Cork footbed sourcing** — confirm factory's standard cork sandal footbed spec and thickness.
3. **Per-version sole material decision** — confirm rubber (Everyday) vs. leather (Dressy) sole assignment at quoting, not left implicit.
4. **Strap fray resistance at cut edges** — confirm finishing method (edge-binding, hot-cut sealing, etc.) for the fabric/ribbon face, since an unfinished cut edge on a bonded strap is a common early-failure point.

## 8. Requested Next Step

An isolated strap-construction swatch sample first (validating the narrow hybrid bonding process in Section 3 before cutting a full shoe), followed by a running prototype of the Everyday Strap version, single size (US 7), single colorway (Cloud Ivory) — the Dressy Slide follows once the shared last and footbed are validated on the first version.
