# Soft Structure — The Jelly Line

*A seasonal, Experimental sub-line inside the shoes catalogue. One construction language, five silhouettes, shared last and footbed. Governed by [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) and the system palette in [`../../color-system.md`](../../color-system.md). Parent catalogue: [`../README.md`](../README.md).*

**Priority for the whole line:** Experimental  
**Phase:** Phase 2 seasonal expansion (docs built early; **do not source ahead of Phase 1 Must-Have shoes or the Barely There sandal**)  
**Status:** Concept  

---

## Why This Line Exists

The main shoe catalogue is leather, knit, and structured waterproof — materials that either patina or perform under full weather. That leaves a real gap:

- **Warm-weather water** (pool, beach, wet sidewalk after a storm) where the Barely There sandal’s leather/fabric straps are wrong
- **Wipeable play** where the Perfect Flat is too precious for the day
- **Color-as-the-whole-shoe** — translucent Starlink Emerald or Blush Mauve reading as a single soft object, not a neutral base with trim

Jelly fills that gap. It does **not** replace the Perfect Flat, Cloud Walker, Hug Boot, Anchor Heel, or Barely There sandal. Those stay the load-bearing rotation.

## The Honest Exception

The shoes catalogue rule is *materials chosen to patina.* Jelly **does not patina** — it yellows, can get sticky, and eventually cracks. That is not a failure of this line if we name it:

> **Soft Structure is the seasonal, wipeable, replaceable exception.** Soft upper, hard architecture underfoot, closed palette, silver-family hardware. Recolor and replace; do not pretend it improves with age the way denim or sterling silver does.

That is controlled chaos with a documented reason — not a silent contradiction of the manifesto.

## Thesis: Soft Structure

Same logic as Starlink One and the rest of the wardrobe: **soft shape held by hard architecture.**

| Soft | Hard |
|---|---|
| Flexible TPU/PVC upper | Molded anatomical footbed + real arch |
| Translucent color as the whole silhouette | Silver-tone chain-link buckle / charm |
| Barefoot-adjacent hand-feel | Stable heel cup, slim straps that actually hold |
| Seasonal, playful register | Closed 9-color palette only — no neon, no gold glitter |

## Shared Construction Rules (all five silhouettes)

These are non-negotiable. If a sourced product breaks them, it is off-line.

1. **Upper material:** soft TPU or flexible PVC blend — target Shore A hardness in a range that stays pliable at room temp and does not feel like hard candy against skin. Confirm on sample; factory sheet hardness is required before any multi-SKU order.
2. **Footbed:** molded anatomical bed with real arch support. Optional removable memory-foam insert for rinse/air-dry (preferred). Flat uncontoured jelly slabs are out of system.
3. **Heel height:** 0–1.5" depending on silhouette (see each item). No aggressive pitch. 64" frame: slim straps, no chunky kid Mary-Jane proportions.
4. **Hardware:** silver-family only (sterling look, gunmetal, or pewter-tone molded/metal buckle). Chain-link motif on buckle or as a small charm on the strap — match scale loosely to Starlink One / the chain-link belt, not oversized costume links.
5. **Hidden detail:** small heart + chain-link blind deboss under the footbed (or on the removable insert underside) — discovered when the shoe is off, same private-moment logic as Anchor Heel’s insole mark and the Perfect Flat’s emerald enamel dot.
6. **Colorways (line palette):**
   - **Hero:** translucent Starlink Emerald
   - **Romantic:** translucent Blush Mauve, translucent Periwinkle or Lavender Mist
   - **Quiet:** translucent Cloud Ivory, translucent Sage
   - **Grounding:** opaque Jet Black (only silhouette that may go fully opaque by default)
   - **Never:** gold glitter, warm amber, off-palette neon, multi-color rainbow packs
7. **Sizing:** true to size; document if the last runs narrow (classic jelly failure mode). Prefer a last with slight forefoot room over a squeeze.
8. **Care:** rinse, mild soap, air dry out of direct heat and long sun storage. No machine dry, no radiator. Expect multi-season life, not heirloom life.

### Sourcing references (line-level)

- Melissa (Brazilian jelly — elevated construction language)
- Steve Madden / Jeffrey Campbell translucent jellies (silhouette range)
- Old Navy / Target seasonal jellies (price-floor reality check — usually fail the footbed rule)
- Approx. elevated range: $40–$120 per pair; budget multi-packs are almost always wrong for this system

### Prototype order

1. **Hero first:** Everyday jelly flat (`01`) in translucent Starlink Emerald + Cloud Ivory only  
2. Wear-test one warm season (pool, errands, wet sidewalk)  
3. Only then open slide, ankle-strap, low-block mule  
4. Rain clog / chelsea mule (`05`) only if 01–04 prove the construction language works  

Same discipline as the barrel jean: do not scale an unproven silhouette into five SKUs on day one. The *docs* describe the whole line; *sourcing* is sequential.

## What This Line Pairs With

- Linen drawstring shorts, denim cutoffs, bike shorts, sweat shorts
