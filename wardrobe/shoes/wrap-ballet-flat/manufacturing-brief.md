# The Wrap Ballet Flat — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for factory quoting and prototyping
**Prepared for:** Footwear manufacturer, Shenzhen
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending factory confirmation of ribbon-loop anchor durability under repeated daily tying)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into dimensions, materials, and construction methods for an initial quoting and prototyping conversation. Section 7 lists what needs factory engineering confirmation before tooling begins. The ribbon-loop anchor points (Section 3) see more repeated handling than any other strap or tie detail in this catalogue and are the top durability question in this brief.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A women's true ballet flat, no internal wedge, thin flexible slip-lasted sole, aniline nappa leather or cotton twill/canvas upper, and long ribbon ties anchored through ankle-opening loops that wrap once or twice, wearer's choice, and tie.

## 2. Shoe Specification

| Parameter | Target | Tolerance |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | None (true flat) | N/A — confirm no internal wedge is added by factory default the way some "ballet flat" tooling assumes a small heel lift |
| Vamp height | Low, true ballet-flat cut | Confirm last profile against reference — explicitly lower than [`../perfect-flat/manufacturing-brief.md`](../perfect-flat/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 2's raised loafer-hybrid vamp target |
| Sole thickness | Thin, maximally flexible | Confirm against the thinnest sole spec anywhere else in this catalogue — this piece should flex closer to a true dance shoe than a standard flat |
| Ribbon length | Long enough for either a single or double ankle wrap plus tying | Confirm against a genuine range of ankle circumferences at both configurations |

## 3. Construction Method — Ribbon Loop Anchors

- **Construction: slip-lasted or cement construction**, the thinnest and most flexible sole build in the catalogue — confirm factory's lightest-weight flat construction option, likely a step below even the Perfect Flat's own spec (see [`../perfect-flat/manufacturing-brief.md`](../perfect-flat/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 3).
- **Ribbon anchor loops/D-rings:** small loops or flat D-rings set into the topline at the heel and both sides of the ankle opening — **this is the single highest-durability-risk detail in the brief.** Unlike a single ankle-wrap sandal strap (tied once, worn for the day), this ribbon is threaded and re-tied through these anchor points every single wear, potentially in two different wrap configurations (Section 6 of the vision document). Request a repeated-cycle wear test on the anchor points specifically — a loop or ring that loosens, wears through, or pulls away from the upper after repeated daily tying is a real failure mode this construction needs to be tested against, not just specified against.
- **Ribbon construction:** the same hybrid leather/fabric-core ribbon as [`../espadrille-wedge/manufacturing-brief.md`](../espadrille-wedge/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 5 and [`../barely-there-sandal/manufacturing-brief.md`](../barely-there-sandal/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 3 — confirm the same bonding process applies at this ribbon's specific width and length, and flag that this ribbon sees more handling cycles than either of those pieces' straps.
- **Footbed:** thin memory foam layer over a soft base — confirm minimum thickness that still delivers genuine cushioning without adding bulk that would work against this shoe's minimal silhouette.

## 4. Materials

| Priority | Material | Tannage / Finish | Notes |

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Manufacturing Notes

- **Ribbon tie end thread:** fine Starlink Emerald (`#0F5C3D`, see [`../../color-system.md`](../../color-system.md)) thread woven into the ribbon ends — confirm the same thread color-match process already used for the Espadrille Wedge (see [`../espadrille-wedge/manufacturing-brief.md`](../espadrille-wedge/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 6) applies directly, since this is the same detail reused rather than a new one.
- **No other exterior hardware or color detail** — confirm this stays a single-detail reference, consistent with the vision document's rationale.

## 6. Sizing & Last

- **True to size.**
- **Ribbon length confirmed at both wrap configurations** across a real range of ankle circumferences — flag as a fit-safety item (excess ribbon length at a single wrap) as well as a fit-sufficiency item (enough length for a double wrap on a larger ankle).
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** independently grade US 5–7, same rule as the rest of the catalogue.
- **Confirm the last-maker builds to this shoe's minimal-structure brief specifically** — a last reused from the Perfect Flat's tooling assumptions (which include the hidden wedge and raised vamp) would not produce the right shoe here; this needs its own last, not an adapted one.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Factory Conversation

1. **Ribbon anchor-loop durability under repeated daily tying (Section 3)** — the top risk in this brief; request a repeated-cycle wear test before approving tooling.
2. **Ribbon length sufficiency/safety across both wrap configurations** — confirm against a real range of ankle circumferences, not a single reference measurement.
3. **Minimal-structure last confirmation** — confirm this is built as its own last, not adapted from the Perfect Flat's tooling.
4. **Thinnest-sole-in-catalogue flexibility target** — confirm against a physical sample, since "thin and flexible" needs a concrete comparison point, not just a directional spec.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A running prototype in the aniline nappa colorway, single size (US 7), with the ribbon anchor loops tested through repeated tie/untie cycles in both wrap configurations before approving the sample — given how much more handling this detail sees than any comparable strap or tie elsewhere in the catalogue, this is the one component where a single static sample isn't sufficient validation on its own.
