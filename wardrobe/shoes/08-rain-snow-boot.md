# The Rain/Snow Boot

**Category:** Shoes
**Subcategory:** Boot — Waterproof Chelsea
**Priority:** High
**Status:** Concept

*Part of the full 8-shoe catalogue — see [`README.md`](README.md). Fills a genuine functional gap the rest of the shoe system doesn't cover: nothing else in the catalogue is built for actual wet/icy weather.*

## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- Chelsea silhouette keeps the clean, close-fitting ankle line consistent with the Hug Boot rather than introducing a bulky, clunky waterproof-boot shape that would look out of place next to the rest of the wardrobe.
- Proportion notes: this is the **one shoe in the catalogue where the "moderate heel worn often" rule is deliberately overridden.** A low or near-flat heel (0.5–1" max) is the correct choice here, not the usual 1.5–2" target — traction and stability on ice/wet surfaces matters more than leg-lengthening when the actual risk is falling, and no proportion argument outweighs that. Worth stating explicitly since it's a real, deliberate exception to a rule the rest of this category follows closely.
- Comfort priority: insulated lining for genuine cold-weather warmth, without the bulk that usually comes with insulation — the brief specifically calls for "still looks warm-hug, not clunky," which is a real design tension this spec addresses directly (see Fabric & Feel).

## Technical Specs
- Heel height: 0.5–1" (low/near-flat — see the proportion note above)
- Sizing notes: true to size; confirm room for a slightly thicker sock given the insulated lining
- Sole: **rubber lug outsole with real tread depth** — this is a genuine functional requirement, not a style choice; a smooth or minimal-tread sole defeats the purpose of a snow/ice boot regardless of how it looks
- Closure type: Chelsea-style elastic side gussets, fully seam-sealed for waterproofing

## Fabric & Feel
- Upper material: waterproof-treated leather or a rubber-coated waterproof textile, seam-sealed construction throughout — not just a water-resistant spray finish, genuine seam-level waterproofing
- Lining: insulated — a synthetic warm lining (not real shearling, which doesn't pair well with a fully waterproof/seam-sealed build) engineered specifically to stay slim rather than bulky, addressing the "warm-hug not clunky" brief directly
- Hand-feel / skin feel: soft interior against the ankle despite the technical/waterproof exterior — the interior comfort spec doesn't get compromised just because the exterior is functional
- Breathability: lower than the rest of the boot category by necessity (waterproofing trades off against breathability) — acceptable here since this is a specific-weather-condition shoe, not an everyday piece
- Break-in behavior: minimal — a technical waterproof boot shouldn't need breaking in the way a leather dress boot does
