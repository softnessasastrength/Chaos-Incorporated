# The Perfect Flat — Design Vision

*The everyday flat — Must-Have tier, the "neutral variable" shoe the rest of the bottoms system is built to pair against. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the full shoe catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing design doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Perfect Flat.**

Every other shoe in this catalogue makes its engineering visible in some way — the Anchor Heel's wide base, the Hug Boot's stacked block, the Statement Mule's overt chain-link. This piece inverts that rule on purpose: it's the one shoe in the system where the engineering has to disappear completely. The whole promise of a "flat" is that it reads as having no structure at all, and this piece has to deliver real structure — a hidden wedge, a loafer-grade vamp, genuine cushioning — without ever letting that structure show at the silhouette level. Where Starlink One argues "soft shape, visible hard architecture," the Perfect Flat argues the quieter companion case: soft shape, **invisible** hard architecture, engineering that only exists so the wearer never has to think about her feet across a full day on her feet. It plays the same "quiet workhorse" role in footwear that the straight-leg jean plays in bottoms — the piece that carries the most weekly mileage and asks for the least attention.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Heel-side stack height | 12–14mm (0.5") internal wedge | Full spec below in Section 4 — this is a wedge insert concealed inside the shoe, not a visible external heel |
| Forefoot stack height | 3–4mm | The wedge tapers from heel to forefoot; this taper, not the heel-side number alone, is what keeps the external silhouette reading flat |
| Effective internal pitch | ~4–5° | Gentle enough to be felt as "a little extra support," not perceived as a heel |
| Vamp height (front coverage) | Raised ~8–10mm above a classic low-cut ballet flat | The "loafer hybrid" identity — more foot coverage and structure holding the foot in place than a pure ballet flat offers |
| Toe shape | Rounded / soft almond | Softer, more in-scale on a smaller frame than a long pointed toe |
| Outsole | Rubber, thin profile | Thin enough to keep the flat silhouette; see Section 4 for the wedge-concealment construction that makes this possible |

All figures are target specs for prototype confirmation, not locked tolerances — see `manufacturing-brief.md` for the buildable version of this table.

## 3. Material Palette — Two Options, Ranked

### 1st — Aniline Nappa Calfskin (recommended, flagship material)
- **Tannage/finish:** vegetable-tanned or veg-chrome combination, full aniline dye, no corrected-grain topcoat — the same non-negotiable "ages, doesn't stay static" finish rule the Anchor Heel's flagship material follows (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 3).
- **Hand-feel:** extremely soft — softer than a standard ballet-flat upper, closer to a slipper's hand-feel, since this piece is worn constantly enough that upper softness matters as much as footbed cushioning.
- **Thickness:** 1.0–1.2mm — thinner than the Anchor Heel's upper leather (1.2–1.4mm), since a flat's upper doesn't need to support a heel-bearing structure the way a heeled shoe's upper does.

### 2nd — Microfiber Leather Alternative
- **Construction:** premium bonded microfiber, not a stiff PU/pleather substitute — chosen specifically to preserve the "extremely soft" hand-feel brief for a vegan-leather option, not as a lower-cost fallback that compromises feel.
- **Why it's ranked second, not excluded:** doesn't patina the way aniline leather does, so it's positioned as the accessibility/vegan option rather than a flagship substitute — offered in the same colorways, priced accordingly.

## 4. Construction Details — The Hidden Wedge

- **Construction method:** slip-lasted or cement construction (not Blake or Goodyear welt) — the correct, standard method for a lightweight flat; the Anchor Heel's Blake stitch is overkill here and would add unnecessary sole rigidity to a piece whose entire brief is flexibility.
- **Wedge insert:** a molded EVA or cork wedge sits between the sockliner and the outsole, tapering from the Section 2 heel-side stack to the forefoot stack. It's fully enclosed within the shoe's sole unit — no visible platform edge or step at the outsole perimeter — so the external silhouette reads as a standard thin-soled flat from any angle.
- **Footbed build (top to bottom):** soft napped leather sock lining → thin memory foam layer over the concealed wedge → the wedge/EVA structure itself → thin rubber outsole. Cushioning is real, not just a spec-sheet claim, despite the whole build staying thin enough to disappear visually.
- **Vamp construction:** cut higher than a pure ballet flat, closer to a loafer's coverage — this is what gives the piece its "hybrid" identity and adds a bit more structure holding the foot in place versus a true ballet flat's minimal vamp.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Hidden, Not Announced

A single quiet detail, smaller and more casual in scale than any other shoe in the catalogue, appropriate to an everyday flat that's meant to disappear into the outfit:

- **Heel counter inset:** a small emerald enamel dot set into the heel counter (the back of the shoe, low), discovered only when the shoe is off or being put on — the same "quiet, private detail" logic as the Anchor Heel's blind-debossed insole mark (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 5), at a smaller and more casual scale suited to this piece.
- **Open question carried into the manufacturing brief:** whether the enamel inset creates a pressure point against the Achilles at the heel counter needs a physical fit check, not just a design check — flagged explicitly in `manufacturing-brief.md` Section 8.

No exterior hardware beyond this single dot. A quieter reference than any heeled piece in the catalogue, matching the "everyday pieces stay quiet" rule already established for the Anchor Heel.

## 6. Optional Variation — Mary Jane Strap

A secondary variation, not a required build: the same last, wedge, and upper material, with a thin ankle strap and a small silver-tone buckle added at the vamp. Same "prove the core, then expand" logic as the Anchor Heel's three-variation structure — the primary slip-on is the one to sample first (Section 7 of `manufacturing-brief.md`); the Mary Jane strap is a second-phase colorway-equivalent addition once the base last and wedge concealment are validated.

## 7. Sizing & Last Notes

- **Least room to compensate for a fit error of any shoe in the catalogue** — a flat has no heel, no boot shaft, no strap system to take up slack the way other categories can; the last has to be right the first time.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** the same rule the Anchor Heel applies (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 7) — independent grading for US 5–7, not a linear shrink from an 8–9 master last.
- **Width options:** Medium (M) as standard; Narrow (B) and Wide (W) as a second-phase addition, same sequencing the Anchor Heel uses.
- **Brand-running note carried from the original concept doc:** confirm whether the sourced last runs narrow — flats have the least room in the whole catalogue to absorb a narrow-running last without a real fit complaint.
