# The Perfect Flat — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for factory quoting and prototyping
**Prepared for:** Footwear manufacturer, Shenzhen
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending factory confirmation of wedge-concealment construction and enamel-inset tooling)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into dimensions, materials, and construction methods for an initial quoting and prototyping conversation. Section 8 lists what needs factory engineering confirmation before tooling begins.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A women's flat, ballet/loafer hybrid, with a fully concealed 12–14mm internal heel-side wedge tapering to 3–4mm at the forefoot. Slip-lasted or cement construction, thin rubber outsole, memory-foam-topped footbed over the concealed wedge. Primary material: aniline nappa calfskin. One optional Mary Jane strap variation.

## 2. Wedge & Sole Specification

| Parameter | Target | Tolerance |
|---|---|---|
| Heel-side internal stack height | 12–14mm | ±0.5mm |
| Forefoot internal stack height | 3–4mm | ±0.5mm |
| Wedge taper | Smooth, continuous taper heel-to-forefoot, no visible step | Confirm via physical sample — this is a feel/silhouette spec, not just a dimension |
| Wedge material | Molded EVA or cork | Confirm factory standard; cork adds foot-shaping-over-time benefit at modest cost premium |
| Outsole thickness | As thin as durability allows — target under 4mm | Confirm minimum thickness the factory's standard rubber compound can hold without excessive wear |
| Toe shape | Rounded / soft almond | Confirm exact last toe-box profile against reference silhouette, not a generic "round toe" spec |
| Vamp height (front coverage) | Raised ~8–10mm above a standard ballet-flat vamp line | Confirm against reference silhouette — this is the detail that reads as "loafer hybrid" rather than "plain ballet flat" |

## 3. Construction Method

- **Construction: slip-lasted or cement construction.** Explicitly not Blake or Goodyear welt — both add sole rigidity this design doesn't want. Confirm factory's standard method for lightweight flats and which of the two (slip-lasted vs. cement) better supports fully concealing the Section 2 wedge without a visible seam at the outsole edge.
- **Wedge concealment — the single most important construction requirement in this brief:** the wedge insert must sit fully enclosed within the sole unit, with no visible platform edge, step, or seam line at the outsole perimeter from any external viewing angle. This is a silhouette-critical spec, not a nice-to-have — the entire design argument of this piece depends on it reading as a standard thin flat. Request a cross-section sample cut alongside the first prototype to confirm concealment before approving the full sample.
- **Footbed build (top to bottom):** soft napped leather sock lining → thin memory foam layer → wedge/EVA structure → thin rubber outsole.

## 4. Materials

| Priority | Material | Tannage / Finish | Thickness | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Aniline nappa calfskin | Vegetable-tanned or veg-chrome combination, full aniline dye, no corrected grain | 1.0–1.2mm | Flagship material — thinner spec than the Anchor Heel's upper leather since this upper doesn't bear heel-structure load |
| 2 | Bonded microfiber (vegan option) | Premium bonded microfiber construction, not standard PU/pleather | 1.0–1.3mm equivalent | Priced and positioned as the accessibility/vegan option, not a flagship cost-cutting substitute — confirm hand-feel sample against the aniline option before approving |

**Critical sourcing note:** as with the Anchor Heel, the aniline finish on material #1 is non-negotiable — do not substitute a corrected-grain leather even if more readily available.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Manufacturing Notes

- **Heel counter enamel dot:** a small recessed metal (silver-tone) cup set into the leather heel counter, filled with a Starlink Emerald (`#0F5C3D`, see [`../../color-system.md`](../../color-system.md)) vitreous enamel or durable enamel-resin hybrid fill. Confirm with the factory whether true vitreous enamel is achievable at this small scale on a leather-mounted cup, or whether a colorfast enamel-resin fill is the more practical small-batch approach.
- **Pressure-point check — flagged as a required physical test, not assumed safe:** confirm the enamel cup's cup depth and edge finishing don't create a pressure point against the Achilles tendon at the heel counter. This needs an in-wear test on the first prototype, not a design-review sign-off alone.

## 6. Optional Mary Jane Variation

| Spec | Value |
|---|---|
| Base | Same last, wedge, and upper material as the primary slip-on |
| Strap | Thin ankle strap, silver-tone buckle |
| Sequencing | Second-phase build — sample the primary slip-on first, add the strap variation once the wedge concealment and last are validated |

## 7. Sizing & Last

- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** independently grade US 5–7 rather than linearly scaling down from an 8–9 master last — the same highest-leverage fit note as the Anchor Heel brief (see [`../anchor-heel/manufacturing-brief.md`](../anchor-heel/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 8).
- **Width options:** Medium (M) standard first; Narrow (B) and Wide (W) as second-phase additions.
- **Narrow-running risk:** confirm whether the selected factory's standard last runs narrow — flag explicitly, since a flat has the least room of any shoe in this catalogue to absorb a narrow-running last without a real fit complaint.

## 8. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Factory Conversation

1. **Wedge concealment (Section 3)** — the gating requirement for this entire piece; request a cross-section sample before approving the full prototype.
2. **Enamel-inset tooling and durability (Section 5)** — confirm minimum feature size and fill method, and test for heel-counter pressure points in wear.
3. **Aniline leather sourcing (Section 4)** — same specialized-material sourcing question as the Anchor Heel; confirm supply chain access.
4. **Last width/narrow-running confirmation (Section 7)** — get this in writing before committing to a last.
5. **Vamp height tooling** — confirm the last-maker can hit the raised vamp line precisely; a vamp cut too low collapses this piece back into a plain ballet flat, undercutting the "hybrid" identity.

## 9. Requested Next Step

A running prototype of the primary slip-on version only, single size (US 7 recommended, matching the Anchor Heel's reference sizing choice), single colorway (Cloud Ivory), with a cross-section sample cut alongside it to confirm wedge concealment before any width expansion, Mary Jane variation, or production tooling commitment.
