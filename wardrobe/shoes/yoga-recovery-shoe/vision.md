# The Yoga / Recovery Shoe — Design Vision

*The comfort-core recovery slip-on — High tier, the single most comfort-forward shoe in the entire catalogue. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the full shoe catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing design doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**The Yoga / Recovery Shoe.**

Every other shoe in this catalogue balances comfort against some other design priority — the Anchor Heel balances comfort against height, the Statement Mule balances comfort against an open-back silhouette, the Rain/Snow Boot balances comfort against waterproofing. This piece has no competing priority to balance against: arch support and cushioning aren't secondary to the silhouette here, they're the entire point of the piece existing. It's the footwear-category proof of "comfort is the aesthetic," the same principle the original wardrobe brief and manifesto both open with — and it does that specifically through a loafer-inspired silhouette rather than an obvious slipper or slide, so the shoe can leave the house (errands, the walk to and from a studio) without reading as "forgot to change shoes." That range of use matters more at this comfort tier than at any other shoe in the system, since a piece that only works indoors gets worn far less often.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 0–0.25" (0–6mm), essentially flat | No leg-lengthening priority at all here — the one shoe category where that math is fully set aside |
| Silhouette | Loafer-inspired, low-profile | Close to the foot, no bulk — reads as "left the house on purpose," not as a slipper |
| Closure | Slip-on, elasticized topline entry | Easy on/off without sacrificing a secure fit |
| Arch support | Substantial, contoured | More assertive than the Anchor Heel's moderate arch support (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 4), since this shoe's entire job is recovery/comfort rather than balancing comfort against a heel |
| Cushioning | Full memory foam footbed, 8–10mm | Thicker than the standard spec used elsewhere in the catalogue (typically 4–6mm) — the one shoe where maximum cushioning is correct without a competing design priority to balance it against |

## 3. Material Palette — Two Options

### 1st — Soft Knit (flagship, maximum comfort)
- **Construction:** flat-knit or circular sock-knit upper, engineered for stretch-to-fit comfort and maximum breathability — the direct footwear parallel to a soft cotton lounge legging (see `../../bottoms/leggings/07-cotton-lounge-legging.md`).
- **Why it fits:** the more purely comfort-forward of the two options, and the one that most literally embodies "comfort is the aesthetic."

### 2nd — Brushed Suede
- **Finish:** natural nap, light protective treatment only.
- **Why it's ranked second, not lesser:** the slightly more "put together" alternative for the same silhouette — offered for wearers who want this shoe's comfort profile in a form that reads a half-step more finished than knit, without changing anything about the underlying comfort spec.

## 4. Construction Details

- **Knit upper construction — a genuinely different manufacturing process from the rest of the catalogue:** a flat-knit or circular sock-knit upper is built on knitting machinery, not cut-and-sewn from leather panels the way every other shoe upper in this system is constructed. This is worth naming explicitly because it may mean sourcing from a different manufacturing partner (or a different production line within the same factory group) than this brand's leather-based footwear — see `manufacturing-brief.md` Section 3.
- **Elasticized topline:** a stretch-knit or elastic-reinforced entry point at the topline, allowing easy slip-on/off without a loose, sloppy fit once on the foot.
- **Footbed build:** substantial contoured arch support built directly into the footbed (not a removable insert), topped with an 8–10mm memory foam layer — thicker than anywhere else in the catalogue, since there's no heel height or hardware detail competing for the shoe's design attention.
- **Outsole:** flexible rubber, contoured for natural foot movement — explicitly not a stiff flat sole, since rigidity would work against the recovery/comfort brief as directly as excess heel height would work against the leg-lengthening brief elsewhere in the catalogue.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Deliberately Absent

**Like the Rain/Snow Boot, this is a function-first piece where a decorative ring reference would work against the design brief rather than support it.** No hardware, no hidden color thread, no enamel inset — a fully soft-construction piece with nothing on it to carry a reference. Comfort is the entire statement this shoe makes, and adding a Starlink One detail here for the sake of the catalogue's "every pair has one" convention would be exactly the kind of decoration-without-purpose the manifesto's "structure earns its place" rule exists to prevent. This piece and the Rain/Snow Boot are the two considered exceptions to the "every pair carries at least one reference" rule, both for the same reason: function-first pieces don't get a reference forced onto them.

## 6. Colorway Notes

Cloud Ivory, Sterling Grey, and Sage — quiet, soft neutrals consistent with the loungewear register this piece pairs into (leggings, sweat shorts, the crewneck sweatshirt).

## 7. Sizing & Last Notes

- **Slight room in the toe box preferred over a snug fit** — consistent with a recovery/comfort use case; this is a deliberate fit philosophy difference from the rest of the catalogue, where a closer, "true to size" fit is usually the goal.
- **Should feel correct immediately:** a "recovery" shoe that needs breaking in has failed its own brief — if a sample needs a wear-in period to feel right, that's a construction issue, not normal break-in.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** same rule as the rest of the catalogue for US 5–7.
