## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- An open back elongates the visible line of the foot and ankle — no back strap or heel counter breaking up the leg the way a closed shoe does, which reads as leg-lengthening in a genuinely different way than heel height alone.
- Proportion notes: a low, sculptural block heel (1.5–2") keeps this stable and grounded rather than precarious — mules are inherently less secure on the foot than a closed shoe (nothing holds the heel in place), so heel height and base width both stay conservative here even though the silhouette reads as more of a statement piece. A rounded or softly squared toe, not pointed, for the same grounding reason.
- Comfort priority: since a mule has no back strap, the vamp/instep strap (see below) has to do more of the job of actually keeping the shoe on the foot — it needs real structure, not just decoration.

## Technical Specs
- Heel height: 1.5–2", sculptural block (curved, low, wide base — not a straight cylindrical block)
- Sizing notes: true to size; consider half-size-down guidance if the brand's mules run loose in the heel, a common mule fit issue
- Sole: leather sole with a rubber grip pad at the forefoot and heel contact points
- Closure type: open back (no strap), single wide instep strap across the vamp

## Fabric & Feel
- Upper material: leather or suede
- Hand-feel / skin feel: cushioned footbed at the forefoot specifically, since mules concentrate more pressure there than a closed shoe with full foot support
- Breathability: excellent (open back)
- Break-in behavior: the instep strap should fit correctly from the first wear — a mule strap that's loose on day one will only get looser, unlike a closed shoe that can mold to the foot over time

## Chaos Incorporated Notes
- Aesthetic tie-in: the going-out counterpart to the Anchor Heel's everyday quietness — same "soft shape, hard architecture" logic (sculptural curved heel = soft shape, visible chain-link hardware = hard architecture) applied to the statement-tier register instead of the everyday one.
