## Why It Works for Her Frame (64")
- A loafer-inspired silhouette (rather than an obvious slipper or slide) means this shoe can leave the house — errands, the walk to and from a studio — without reading as "forgot to change shoes." That range of use matters more at this comfort tier than at any other, since a piece that only works indoors gets worn far less often.
- Proportion notes: low-profile, close to the foot, no bulk — this is a "disappears and lets the rest of the outfit lead" piece, the shoe-category equivalent of the fitted crew tee.
- Comfort priority: this is the single most comfort-forward shoe in the entire catalogue — arch support and cushioning aren't secondary to the silhouette here, they're the entire point of the piece existing.

## Technical Specs
- Heel height: 0–0.25" (essentially flat)
- Sizing notes: true to size; slight room in the toe box preferred over a snug fit, consistent with a recovery/comfort use case
- Sole: flexible rubber outsole, contoured for natural foot movement rather than a stiff flat sole
- Closure type: slip-on, elasticized entry at the topline for an easy on/off without sacrificing a secure fit

## Fabric & Feel
- Upper material: soft knit or brushed suede — knit for maximum breathability and stretch-to-fit comfort, suede as the slightly more "put together" alternative for the same silhouette
- **Arch support:** substantial, contoured — more assertive than the Anchor Heel's moderate arch support, since this shoe's entire job is recovery/comfort rather than balancing comfort against a heel
- **Cushioning:** full memory foam footbed, thicker than the standard spec used elsewhere in the catalogue (target 8–10mm) — this is the one shoe where maximum cushioning is correct without a competing design priority to balance it against
- Hand-feel / skin feel: soft against bare feet or thin socks, no rigid seams anywhere in the foot-contact area
- Breathability: excellent (knit version), good (suede version)
- Break-in behavior: should feel correct immediately — a "recovery" shoe that needs breaking in has failed its own brief
