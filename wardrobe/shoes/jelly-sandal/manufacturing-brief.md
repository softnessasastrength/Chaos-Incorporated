## 1. Product Overview

A women's jelly sandal, 0–0.5" heel height, flexible soft-touch PVC or TPU compound, single or double strap (or woven/lattice slip-on upper), co-molded comfort zone at the footbed, three colorways (Starlink Emerald translucent, Cloud Ivory frosted, Crystal Clear).

## 2. Shoe Specification

| Parameter | Target | Tolerance |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 0–13mm (0–0.5") | ±0.5mm |
| Compound hardness (strap/sole) | Soft-touch PVC or TPU, lower Shore A than a traditional rigid jelly sandal | Confirm factory's standard soft-compound options and request physical hardness samples across a range before selecting a target Shore A |
| Compound hardness (footbed comfort zone) | Softer than the strap/sole compound — see Section 3 | Confirm achievable hardness differential within a single mold |
| Toe shape | Rounded | Confirm mold tooling reflects this, not a generic pointed jelly-sandal toe some stock tooling defaults to |

## 3. Construction Method — Molded, Not Stitched

**This is the core manufacturing departure from every other item in this catalogue, and needs to be understood as such before quoting proceeds.**

- **Single-shot vs. two-shot (multi-shot) injection molding:** confirm with the factory whether the co-molded comfort zone at the footbed (a softer compound zone within an otherwise firmer strap/sole structure) is achievable via two-shot molding (two different compounds injected into the same mold in sequence, bonding at the shot boundary) — this is an established plastics-manufacturing technique, not a custom invention, but it's a real capability question, not every molder runs multi-shot tooling.
- **If two-shot molding isn't available:** confirm the fallback — a single uniform softer compound throughout (simpler, cheaper, but loses the firm-strap/soft-footbed distinction) vs. a separately molded footbed insert bonded in afterward (adds an assembly step this construction category doesn't normally have). Get both fallback options quoted alongside the two-shot approach.
- **Edge finishing:** confirm deflashing and edge-rounding is built into the mold tooling itself at the strap and footbed edges — there's no post-mold finishing step (no leather edge burnishing, no fabric binding) the way every other shoe in this catalogue has, so mold quality is the entire comfort-at-the-edges answer.
- **Hardware mounting (if a buckle closure is used):** confirm the metal buckle mount is designed for a flexible PVC/TPU strap specifically — a mounting method borrowed from a leather-strap buckle assumption may not hold securely in a more flexible, lower-friction molded material.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Manufacturing Notes

- **Translucent Starlink Emerald colorway is itself the reference** — no additional hardware or applied color detail needed on this colorway; confirm the factory understands the color accuracy requirement is the entire spec here, not a secondary detail.
- **Buckle hardware (if used):** small silver-tone metal buckle, confirm plating/finish matches the system-wide hardware rule, same standard as every other shoe in the catalogue.

## 6. Sizing & Last

- **No break-in period exists for this material** — confirm fit is correct and comfortable in the first physical sample; there's no "leather softens after a few wears" grace period to fall back on if the first sample doesn't fit well.
- **Test actual width forgiveness of the flexible compound** against a real foot-width range before deciding whether a single width covers the intended size run, or whether width options are needed the way this catalogue's leather shoes typically offer them.
- **Mold grading for US 5–7:** confirm the factory grades the mold tooling independently per size rather than uniformly scaling one mold — the same last-grading principle applied to injection-molded tooling instead of a traditional last.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Factory Conversation

1. **Two-shot co-molding feasibility for the comfort zone (Section 3)** — the core comfort claim of this product; confirm capability and get fallback options quoted in parallel.
2. **Translucent color-match process for Starlink Emerald (Section 4)** — confirm consistency across a production run, not just a single approved sample.
3. **Buckle-to-flexible-strap mounting durability** — if a buckle closure is used, confirm the mount holds under repeated use in a more flexible material than this brand's other hardware mounts are designed for.
4. **Vendor category confirmation** — confirm the quoting conversation is happening with an actual plastics/injection-molding specialist, not a leather-goods footwear factory being asked to quote outside its core capability.
5. **Width forgiveness testing** — confirm with a physical sample before committing to a single-width production plan.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A running prototype in the Starlink Emerald translucent colorway, single size (US 7), single strap configuration — with the two-shot co-molded comfort zone (Section 3) and the translucent color match (Section 4) as the two specs to validate most carefully, since both are genuinely new territory for this brand's production relationships and neither can be assumed to transfer cleanly from this catalogue's leather-goods manufacturing experience.
