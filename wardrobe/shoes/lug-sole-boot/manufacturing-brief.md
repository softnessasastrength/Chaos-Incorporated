# The Lug-Sole Combat Boot — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for factory quoting and prototyping
**Prepared for:** Footwear manufacturer, Shenzhen
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending factory confirmation of genuine technical outsole sourcing)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into dimensions, materials, and construction methods for an initial quoting and prototyping conversation. Section 7 lists what needs factory engineering confirmation before tooling begins. Per the Experimental tier's own rule, this brief specs a single first bet — one material, one colorway — not a multi-variation launch.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A women's lace-up combat-style boot, 0.5–1" near-flat heel, genuine deep-tread lug outsole, waxed/oiled leather upper, lace-up closure with a functional inner side zip, and chain-link-shaped lace aglets as the single Starlink One reference.

## 2. Boot Specification

| Parameter | Target | Tolerance |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 13–25mm (0.5–1") | ±1.0mm |
| Outsole | Rubber lug, aggressive tread depth | Confirm this is sourced as a genuine technical/utility outsole with real depth and durability — not a cosmetic lug pattern on a standard fashion-boot sole. This is the load-bearing spec behind the entire product's design rationale (see `vision.md` Section 1) |
| Shaft height | Ankle-to-mid-calf | Confirm against reference silhouette |
| Eyelets | 6–8, lace-up | Confirm eyelet hardware finish (silver-tone, satin) |

## 3. Construction Method

- **Lace-up plus functional inner zip:** confirm with the factory this combination is achievable without the zip disrupting the lace-up silhouette's visual line — same combination logic as the Hug Boot's gusset-plus-zip (see [`../hug-boot/manufacturing-brief.md`](../hug-boot/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 3), adapted here to a lace closure instead of an elastic gusset.
- **Lug outsole sourcing — the single most important confirmation in this brief:** request the factory's existing technical/utility outsole options (the same category of outsole used on the Rain/Snow Boot, see [`../rain-snow-boot/manufacturing-brief.md`](../rain-snow-boot/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 2) rather than a standard fashion-boot lug. Get a physical tread-depth and durability comparison between the proposed outsole and a standard fashion sole before approving — the product's entire design justification depends on this being a genuine upgrade, not just a visual one.
- **Footbed:** memory foam over EVA, same standard as the rest of the catalogue — confirm this isn't dropped or downgraded for an "Experimental" item, since the comfort rule applies regardless of validation tier.

## 4. Materials

| Priority | Material | Finish | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (only option for this bet) | Waxed or oiled leather | Heavier utility-appropriate finish, still patina-friendly at crease points | Per the Experimental tier's "one material, one colorway" rule — do not expand to a second material option before this bet is validated |

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Manufacturing Notes

- **Chain-link lace aglets:** confirm the factory's or a hardware subcontractor's ability to cast small chain-link-shaped aglets (replacing standard cylindrical metal aglet tips) at functional lace-tip scale — request a physical hardware sample, since this is a custom component, not a stock lace-aglet order.
- **Finish:** silver-tone, satin — confirm no other exterior hardware carries a Starlink One reference on this build, consistent with the vision document's "quiet, single reference" scope for this Experimental piece.

## 6. Sizing & Last

- **Shaft fit at the calf:** confirm across a real range of calf circumferences — a boot shaft that gapes or pinches is a common real fit failure for this silhouette category.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** independently grade US 5–7, same rule as the rest of the catalogue.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Factory Conversation

1. **Genuine technical outsole sourcing (Section 3)** — the product's core design justification; confirm this is a real utility/technical outsole, not a cosmetic lug pattern, with a physical durability comparison before approval.
2. **Lace-up + functional zip combination** — confirm achievable without visually disrupting the lace-up silhouette.
3. **Custom chain-link aglet casting** — confirm sourcing path and request a physical sample before bulk hardware order.
4. **Calf-shaft fit across a real size range** — confirm with physical fit testing, not last-spec assumptions alone.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A single running prototype — one material (waxed/oiled leather), one colorway, one size (US 7) — per the Experimental tier's build rule. Confirm the lug outsole's genuine durability/traction advantage over a standard fashion sole before wider sampling, and plan the trial-period review (a season, or ~10 wears) named in the vision document before considering any expansion to a second material or colorway.
