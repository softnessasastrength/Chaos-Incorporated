# Block Heel Pump — Manufacturing Brief

**Document Type:** Technical Specification / Manufacturing Brief for factory quoting and prototyping
**Prepared for:** Footwear manufacturer, Shenzhen
**Revision:** 1.0 (Draft — pending factory confirmation of heel stability at height and satin dye-to-match sourcing)
**Governing doc:** [`vision.md`](vision.md) — read for design rationale; this document is the buildable spec.

**Status note, read first:** this brief translates an approved design direction into dimensions, materials, and construction methods for an initial quoting and prototyping conversation. Section 7 lists what needs factory engineering confirmation before tooling begins.

---

## 1. Product Overview

A women's block/stacked-heel pump, 2.5–3" heel height, leather sole with a rubber grip pad, slip-on or ankle-strap closure. Two colorways: Jet Black leather (everyday-within-statement) and Starlink Emerald dyed-to-match satin (flagship, reserved for pairing with the ring statement dress).

## 2. Heel & Pump Specification

| Parameter | Target | Tolerance |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 64–76mm (2.5–3") | ±1.0mm |
| Heel shape | Block or stacked | Confirm factory can hold a genuinely stable base width at this height — not a tapered stiletto profile scaled up |
| Heel base width (ground contact) | Confirm against a stability-focused reference (wider than a stiletto base, narrower than the Anchor Heel's own base given the taller, more formal silhouette) | Request factory's recommended minimum for stability at 2.5–3" |
| Toe shape | Rounded or almond | Confirm last toe-box profile against reference silhouette |
| Ankle strap (if built) | Confirm strap width and buckle placement for security without restricting ankle movement | — |

## 3. Construction Method

- **Heel attachment:** confirm the factory uses a heel nail/dowel-through-shank attachment plus adhesive bonding, the same class of secure attachment as the Anchor Heel (see [`../anchor-heel/manufacturing-brief.md`](../anchor-heel/manufacturing-brief.md) Section 2) — do not accept a lighter cemented-only attachment at this heel height; the taller heel sees more leverage stress than a 2.0–2.25" heel does.
- **Sole:** leather sole with a rubber grip pad at ground-contact points — confirm grip-pad placement and rubber compound against a genuine wet/smooth-floor slip-resistance standard, not just a cosmetic tread.
- **Footbed:** cushioned footbed with soft lining at any ankle-strap contact point.

## 4. Materials

| Priority | Material | Finish | Used In |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Smooth leather | Warm, patina-friendly finish | Jet Black colorway |
| 2 | Satin | Dyed-to-match | Starlink Emerald colorway — confirm dye-to-match process and minimum order quantity for a custom-dyed satin batch against the `#0F5C3D` reference (see [`../../color-system.md`](../../color-system.md)) |

**Sourcing note:** the emerald satin colorway likely requires either a dedicated dye-to-match service or a made-to-order satin dyeing relationship — confirm lead time and MOQ before committing to this colorway for a first production run, since a color this specific rarely comes from stock satin.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — Manufacturing Notes

- **Satin color match:** confirm the factory's or a dye specialist's process for hitting `#0F5C3D` accurately and consistently across a production run — satin dye lots can vary batch to batch, and this colorway is a direct, deliberate match reserved for pairing with the ring statement dress, so consistency matters more here than on an ordinary colorway.
- **Ankle strap buckle:** silver-tone, confirm plating/finish matches the system-wide hardware rule.

## 6. Sizing & Last

- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** independently grade US 5–7, same rule as the rest of the catalogue — flag as more consequential here since a poorly graded last compounds with heel height to affect balance stability, not just comfort.
- **Heel stability:** confirm no break-in period is needed or expected for heel stability at this height in the first prototype.

## 7. Critical Technical Challenges — Open Items for Factory Conversation

1. **Heel stability at 2.5–3" (Section 2/3)** — confirm the factory's standard heel-attachment method holds up at this height without modification; this is the single highest-stakes safety question in this brief.
2. **Satin dye-to-match sourcing (Section 4)** — confirm lead time, MOQ, and batch-to-batch consistency for the Starlink Emerald colorway against `#0F5C3D`.
3. **Grip-pad slip resistance** — confirm against a real wet/smooth-floor standard, not a cosmetic tread pattern alone.
4. **Last grading for smaller sizes** — get in writing given the added consequence of heel-height + poor grading compounding on balance.

## 8. Requested Next Step

A running prototype in the Jet Black leather colorway only, single size (US 7), confirming heel stability and grip-pad slip resistance before committing to the Starlink Emerald satin dye-to-match run — the satin colorway's dye sourcing and MOQ questions (Section 4) are worth resolving in parallel but shouldn't gate the first structural prototype.
