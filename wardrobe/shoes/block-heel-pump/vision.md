# The Block Heel Pump — Design Vision

*The going-out statement heel — Experimental (Phase 3), the system's single considered high heel. Sits alongside [`../statement-mule/vision.md`](../statement-mule/vision.md) in the going-out register — a closed pump vs. an open-back mule, not a duplicate. See [`../README.md`](../README.md) for its place in the full 8-shoe catalogue and [`../../wardrobe-manifesto.md`](../../wardrobe-manifesto.md) for the governing design doctrine.*

---

## 1. Name & Philosophy

**Block Heel Pump.**

Per the shoes README's own note, a moderate heel worn often gives more usable leg-lengthening across a week than a high heel worn rarely — this piece intentionally breaks that rule as a single, considered exception for genuine going-out occasions, which is exactly what a Phase 3 statement piece is for. It's named explicitly in `../../build-plan.md`'s Phase 3 scope as "a single considered heeled pair rather than several speculative ones" — the system makes one real bet on height instead of hedging across several. The design argument stays consistent with the rest of the catalogue even at this height: a block or stacked heel, never a stiletto, because genuine stability at 2.5–3" matters more than a thinner heel's extra formality, and the manifesto's non-negotiable comfort rule doesn't get suspended just because this is a going-out shoe.

## 2. Exact Specifications

| Spec | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Heel height | 2.5–3" (64–76mm), block or stacked | The system's one deliberate exception to the "moderate heel" rule |
| Heel shape | Block or stacked, not stiletto-thin | Stability at this height requires real heel-base width — the same "grounding over height" argument as the Anchor Heel, applied at statement scale |
| Toe shape | Rounded or almond, not pointed | A pointed toe shortens the visual foot-to-leg line more than a rounded one on a smaller foot — this holds even at statement-heel height |
| Closure | Slip-on or ankle strap | Ankle strap adds security at this height for occasions with more movement (dancing, a full evening out) |

All figures are target specs pending a physical sample — see `manufacturing-brief.md` Section 2 for the buildable tolerance table.

## 3. Material Palette — Two Options

### 1st — Leather (everyday-within-statement option)
- **Finish:** smooth leather, warm patina-friendly finish, Jet Black colorway.
- **Why it fits:** the more durable, more frequently wearable of the two options — the pump a wearer reaches for across most going-out occasions.

### 2nd — Satin (flagship color-statement option)
- **Finish:** dyed-to-match satin, specifically for the Starlink Emerald colorway (Section 5) — satin is the correct material for achieving a true, saturated emerald match in a way a dyed leather typically can't hit as precisely.
- **Why it's ranked second, not lesser:** lower durability and higher care requirements than leather, which is why it's reserved specifically for the one colorway built to pair with the ring statement dress rather than offered as a general everyday-within-statement option.

## 4. Construction Details

- **Heel attachment:** a genuinely stable heel core and attachment method are required even at 2.5–3" — this is not a license to use a lighter-duty attachment than the Anchor Heel's own heel nail/dowel-through-shank method (see [`../anchor-heel/vision.md`](../anchor-heel/vision.md) Section 4); if anything, the taller heel needs the same or greater attachment security.
- **Sole:** leather sole with a rubber grip pad — the grip pad is a genuine functional requirement at this heel height, not a finishing touch, since a smooth leather-only sole at 2.5–3" carries real slip risk.
- **Footbed:** cushioned, with soft lining at any strap contact point (ankle-strap version) — comfort is not suspended by the "it's a going-out shoe" framing, per the manifesto's non-negotiable comfort rule restated in Section 1.

## 5. Starlink One Reference — The System's Most Direct Color Statement

- **Starlink Emerald satin colorway:** a direct, deliberate color match to the ring's own emerald (`#0F5C3D`, see [`../../color-system.md`](../../color-system.md)) — reserved specifically to pair with the ring statement dress. This is the boldest single Starlink One reference of any everyday-adjacent shoe in the catalogue, appropriate to its Phase 3 statement role.
- **Deliberate restraint when worn separately:** worn with the Jet Black leather pump instead of the emerald satin, this keeps the outfit's saturated-green statement to one place (the dress) rather than doubling it — the same "one loud element at a time" rule used across the wardrobe and the Gallery One pen's color direction (see [`../../../objects/pen/vision.md`](../../../objects/pen/vision.md) Section 9).
- **Hardware:** ankle strap buckle, if present, silver-tone.

## 6. Sizing & Last Notes

- **True to size.**
- **Heel stability shouldn't need breaking in:** if the heel feels wobbly on day one at this height, it's a construction failure, not a fit issue to work through.
- **Last grading, not linear scaling:** same rule as the rest of the catalogue for US 5–7 — arguably more consequential at this heel height, since a poorly graded last compounds with heel height to affect balance, not just toe-box comfort.
- **Storage note carried from the original concept doc:** shoe trees recommended between wears to hold shape, given satin's greater tendency to lose form than leather.
