# Internal Layers — The Reveal Sequence

*Order of reveal, tissue, inserts, wrapping, scent. Every step here is a deliberate beat, not just a packing method — read [`philosophy.md`](philosophy.md) for why pacing itself is treated as a design material.*

## The Sequence, Step by Step

**1. Ribbon.** She unties the grosgrain ribbon closure. Two-handed, slow, deliberate — the first physical gesture of the whole experience, and intentionally not a fast one.

**2. Lid lift.** A gentle, light friction fit (not vacuum-tight) — enough resistance to feel considered, not so much that it fights her. See `technical-specs.md` for the exact removal-force spec.

**3. First sight.** The interior color (Blush Mauve or seasonal Sage) is the first thing she sees — a deliberate contrast against the Cloud Ivory exterior. Resting on top of a folded tissue layer is the note card, placed at a slight, deliberate angle rather than perfectly squared to the box edge — a small "placed by hand" imperfection that reads as personal rather than machine-packed. See `personal-touch.md` for the note itself.

**4. Note card lift.** Underneath the note: the tissue-wrapped garment, sealed closed with a small paper seal sticker (heart or chain-link motif, matte, palette-colored — never a plastic film sticker).

**5. Tissue unwrap.** The garment is revealed. Folded with acid-free interleaving tissue between each fold to prevent crease lines — this matters most for the `intricate/` tops and the ring statement dress, where a hard crease across a puff sleeve or a tiered hem would visibly mar the piece.

**6. Scent.** A small scent card is tucked into a corner pocket of the tissue — never sprayed directly onto the garment. See the "On Scent" section below for why.

**7. The small extra.** On select orders, a complementary accessory (a hair ribbon or silk scrunchie from the wardrobe's accessories line, see `wardrobe/accessories/04-hair-ribbons-scrunchies.md`) sits nested beside the folded garment in its own tiny pouch — reinforcing the wardrobe system's own "everything is designed to pair" thesis from `wardrobe-architecture.md`, now extended into the unboxing moment itself.

**8. The base layer.** Underneath everything, flat against the box floor: a reusable soft drawstring dust bag, folded flat. It doubles as a final soft layer of protection and becomes a keepsake she keeps using long after the box itself is set aside.

## Tissue Paper Spec (Summary — full spec in `technical-specs.md`)

- Acid-free, mid-weight (not the thin, crinkly extreme — a thin tissue sounds cheap; spec'd for a soft rustle, not a sharp crinkle).
- Color: Cloud Ivory or Blush Mauve, matching the box interior.
- Custom print: a subtle, single spot-color repeat of the heart + chain-link motif — small scale, low contrast (tone-on-tone), meant to be discovered rather than announced.
- Fold method: cross-fold/envelope-fold wrap, no visible tape. Tape is a utilitarian, cold material cue and is avoided everywhere it's not structurally necessary — the paper seal sticker (Step 4) does the closure job instead.

## On Scent

A light, natural "just-washed linen" register — soft cotton and a faint floral note, nothing sharp or perfume-heavy. Delivered as a **removable scent card**, never sprayed on fabric directly, for three reasons:

1. **Fabric safety** — some scent oils can stain or discolor delicate fabrics (voile, lace, silk-blend camisoles) used throughout the `intricate/` tops line.
2. **Allergen/sensitivity control** — a removable card can be left out of an order entirely on request; a sprayed garment can't be un-scented.
3. **Longevity** — a card can be kept in a drawer with the garment for a lasting, low-level scent presence, which a one-time spray can't provide.

Full fragrance compliance requirements (IFRA, hypoallergenic sign-off) are in `technical-specs.md` Section 6.
