# Tactile & Sensory Details

*How the package should feel, sound, and look at every stage of opening. Where `philosophy.md` explains the "why," this doc is the sense-by-sense checklist that keeps every material decision consistent with it.*

## Touch

- **Box exterior:** soft-touch (velvet) matte lamination — a slightly textured, non-slick surface that's pleasant to rest a palm on. Never a slick gloss finish.
- **Corners:** rounded (3mm radius) everywhere a hand makes contact — a physically softer sensation than a sharp edge, independent of the visual effect.
- **Ribbon:** smooth grosgrain, not a stiff or wiry ribbon that fights the fingers when untying.
- **Tissue:** soft, mid-weight, acid-free — not the thin, brittle extreme that catches on skin or fabric.
- **Note card stock:** soft-touch or cotton-fiber paper, not a stiff coated card stock.

## Sound

Sound is treated as seriously as any visual spec, because a cheap-sounding package undoes an expensive-feeling one instantly.

- **No plastic sounds anywhere** — no cellophane crinkle, no bubble wrap pop, no poly bag rustle. These are eliminated at the material level (see `philosophy.md` Section IV), not just minimized.
- **Tissue rustle:** should be soft, not sharp — this is a direct function of the GSM chosen (see `technical-specs.md`); too thin a tissue produces a sharp crinkle regardless of how careful the handling is.
- **Ribbon:** should glide, not squeak, against itself and the box slits.
- **Lid removal:** a soft, gentle "give," never a pop, snap, or vacuum-release sound. This is the direct consequence of choosing a ribbon closure with only a light friction fit on the lid, instead of a magnetic closure or a tight vacuum-fit lid (see `outer-box.md`) — those alternatives are mechanically satisfying but sonically cold, and they're excluded on that basis.

## Sight

- **Restrained palette:** no more than three colors visible at any single stage of the reveal (see the sequence in `internal-layers.md`) — enough for the eye to register a considered choice, not so much that it reads busy or overwhelms the "minimalist but emotionally rich" brief.
- **The one big visual beat:** the color shift from Cloud Ivory (exterior) to Blush Mauve/Sage (interior) on lid-lift is the single deliberate "surprise" moment — everything else in the visual language is quiet on purpose so this one beat lands.
- **Matte over gloss, everywhere, no exceptions** — matte diffuses light softly; gloss throws hard reflections and reads corporate/cold under any lighting condition, which directly contradicts the brief.
- **Deliberate asymmetry:** the note card's slight off-angle placement (see `internal-layers.md` Step 3) is the one intentional visual "imperfection" in an otherwise precise package — a hand-placed cue, not sloppiness.

## Weight & Pace

- **Heft:** the empty box should feel substantial without being heavy — see the weight target in `outer-box.md`. Too light reads flimsy/cheap; too heavy reads over-engineered and works against the warmth brief.
- **Pace is a sensory detail too.** Every choice in this document — the ribbon over the magnet, the light friction fit over a vacuum seal, the tissue-then-note-then-tissue-then-garment sequencing — exists to slow the opening down. A package that can be torn open in two seconds delivers a completely different feeling than one that takes thirty. Slowness, here, is the luxury signal — not spectacle, not shine.
