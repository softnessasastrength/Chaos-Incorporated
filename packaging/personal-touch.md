# Personal Touch

*The note, the extras, and the difference between "packaging" and "a gift." Placement in the reveal sequence is defined in [`internal-layers.md`](internal-layers.md) Step 3–4.*

## The Note

- **Format:** small card, roughly A6 (105 × 148mm), printed on a soft-touch or cotton-fiber paper stock — never glossy card stock, which reads like a receipt or a marketing insert.
- **Construction:** a hybrid of printed and handwritten. The Chaos Incorporated wordmark and any fixed copy (care line, a short brand line) is printed via letterpress or blind deboss — quiet, tactile, consistent with the box's own finishing language. Below that, a blank space is reserved for an actual handwritten line from Branden, per order — not a printed "Dear valued customer," an actual name and an actual sentence.
- **Tone:** specific and warm, never generic marketing copy. The banned-language list from `philosophy.md` applies directly here — no "Thank you for your purchase," no "We hope you love it." A real example of the register this should hit: *"Renn — made this one thinking about how it'd feel to actually wear all day, not just look good for five minutes. Hope it does exactly that."* Personal, specific to the piece, no exclamation-point marketing voice.
- **Signature:** handwritten, every time. This is the single highest-leverage detail in the whole package for making it feel like it came from a person, not a company.

## Care Card

A second, smaller card in the same paper-stock family as the note (so they read as a matched pair, not two random inserts), carrying the actual care instructions for the specific piece inside. This content already exists — every item in the wardrobe system documents its own care instructions in the "Chaos Incorporated Notes" section of its item file (see any file under `wardrobe/`, e.g. `wardrobe/bottoms/jeans/01-straight-leg-jean.md`). Packaging doesn't need to invent new copy here — it pulls directly from the existing per-item documentation, which also guarantees the printed care instructions can never drift out of sync with the actual garment spec.

## The Small Extras

- **Reusable dust bag** — soft drawstring bag (see `technical-specs.md` for fabric spec), small debossed or embroidered heart-and-chain-link motif. Functions past the unboxing moment as a travel pouch or a keepsake — this is the one packaging component explicitly designed to have a life after the box itself is set aside.
- **Complementary accessory sample** — on select orders, a matching hair ribbon or silk scrunchie from `wardrobe/accessories/04-hair-ribbons-scrunchies.md`, nested beside the garment. Low cost, high delight, and it reinforces the wardrobe's own "everything pairs" design thesis at the moment she's most receptive to it.
- **Wax-free seal sticker** — closes the tissue wrap (see `internal-layers.md` Step 4). Small, matte, palette-colored, heart or chain-link motif. Paper, never a plastic film sticker.

## Personalization at Scale

While order volume is low, the handwritten note and hand-selected extra are fully bespoke per order. If volume grows past what one person can handwrite per order, the fallback is a **name card slot** — her name still handwritten, with the body copy limited to a small set of pre-written, still-specific (never generic) message variants tied to product category (first jean purchase vs. a repeat order vs. a gift order, etc.) rather than one universal message. This is a scaling decision to revisit later, not a current constraint — noted here so it doesn't get quietly genericized under order-volume pressure without a deliberate call.
