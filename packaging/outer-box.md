# Outer Box

*Materials, color, size, closure, exterior details. Governed by [`philosophy.md`](philosophy.md) — every choice below is tested against "structure protects, softness reveals."*

## Box Type

A rigid two-piece **setup box** (separate base + lid, not a fold-flat mailer or a one-piece tuck box). Reasoning: a rigid box has real, reassuring heft the instant it's picked up — that heft is doing emotional work in the "reassurance" phase named in the philosophy doc. A flat mailer arrives feeling like shipping; a rigid box arrives feeling like a gift.

Not used: a magnetic-closure rigid box (the Apple-style default for this box type). See `philosophy.md` Section IV — the closure is a ribbon, and the box is engineered around that decision rather than defaulting to magnets. See `technical-specs.md` for the resulting friction-fit spec on the lid itself.

## Materials

- **Core:** rigid chipboard/greyboard, wrapped construction (not a printed folding-carton — the wrapped rigid construction is what gives the soft-touch exterior its continuous, seamless feel).
- **Exterior wrap:** dyed matte paper stock, finished with a soft-touch (velvet) matte lamination film. Deliberately not gloss, not a textured/embossed-all-over stock (which can read busy) — a smooth, quiet matte surface that's pleasant to hold and rest a hand on.
- **Interior wrap:** a contrasting color from the same palette (see below), same soft-touch finish, so the "before/after opening" color shift is itself the one big visual surprise in the box.

## Color

Pulled directly from the wardrobe's existing [`color-system.md`](../wardrobe/color-system.md) — no new colors introduced for packaging, so the box reads as unmistakably part of the same system as what's inside it.

| Element | Color | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Exterior (base + lid) | **Cloud Ivory** | The warmest, softest neutral in the palette — reads calm and inviting rather than stark. Cool-undertone-safe, consistent with the rest of the brand. |
| Interior (base + lid, visible on open) | **Blush Mauve**, rotating seasonally to **Sage** | The "reveal" color moment — a considered contrast the eye wasn't expecting, without introducing a color outside the established system. |
| Deboss / accent hardware | **Sterling silver** (matte foil, used sparingly) | Consistent with the system-wide "hardware is silver-family only" rule from `wardrobe-architecture.md` — no gold or warm-toned foil anywhere in the packaging. |
| Ribbon closure | **Blush Mauve** (default), **Starlink Emerald** for gifting/statement occasions | Direct callback to the ribbon-tie details already in the wardrobe system. |

## Corner Treatment

**Rounded corners, 3mm radius**, on both base and lid. This is a small spec with an outsized effect: a sharp 90° corner reads as corporate/technical, a rounded corner reads as soft before it's even touched. Every edge a hand might contact is rounded — no sharp box edges anywhere in the package.

## Exterior Branding

- **Blind deboss** (no ink, no foil) of the Chaos Incorporated wordmark, centered on the lid top. Deboss over foil-stamp by default — it's a detail you *feel* before you see it in certain light, which is a quieter, more intimate brand gesture than a shiny foil logo.
- A small companion deboss of the **heart + chain-link motif** (direct callback to Starlink One's own signature detail) placed at a lower corner of the lid, subtle scale — not a repeating pattern, a single quiet mark.
- Optional: a single hit of matte silver foil confined to just the heart shape within that motif (not the full deboss) — a small, deliberate glint rather than a shiny logo block. Confirm with factory whether foil-on-deboss (combination finishing) is standard tooling or requires a separate die; flag as a cost/complexity item to discuss.

## Closure

A **grosgrain ribbon wrap**, threaded through two slits cut into the base, tied in a bow or simple knot on top of the closed lid. The ribbon does two jobs: it's the closure, and it's the pull-tab that lifts the lid once untied (no separate ribbon pull-tab needed inside).

- Untying is a deliberate, two-handed gesture — slower and warmer than a magnetic snap, which is the entire point per `philosophy.md`.
- Ribbon width, exact attachment method, and pull-test tolerance are specified in `technical-specs.md`.

## Sizing Matrix

Sized tight to the folded product plus a minimum tissue clearance (~12–15mm on each dimension) — never a generically oversized box. See the full dimension table by product category in `technical-specs.md` Section 2; the categories are: folded top, folded bottoms, folded outerwear, dress (flat-pack with tissue rolls to prevent creasing at the fold line), and a separate small-format box for Starlink One and future fine jewelry.

## Weight

Target an empty-box weight (base + lid + interior wrap, no contents) in the range of **180–260g** depending on size tier — enough to feel substantial in the hand, not so heavy it reads as wasteful or over-engineered. Exact target confirmed once chipboard thickness is finalized with the factory (see `technical-specs.md`).
